# Cefotaxime

## Pharmacological Class

- **Class**: `Third-generation cephalosporin antibiotic`
- **Subclass**: `β-lactam antibiotic`

## Mechanism of Action

- Inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis
  → Binds to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs)
  → Inhibits transpeptidation step in peptidoglycan synthesis
  → Cell lysis and death (bactericidal)

## Indications

1. **Infections caused by susceptible organisms**:

   - Septicaemia
   - Lower respiratory tract infections (e.g., pneumonia)
   - Meningitis (esp. neonatal and childhood)
   - Genitourinary tract infections
   - Skin and soft tissue infections
   - Bone and joint infections
   - Intra-abdominal infections (e.g., peritonitis)
   - Pelvic inflammatory disease
   - Gonorrhoea (uncomplicated)
   - Surgical prophylaxis (esp. in colorectal surgery)

## Contraindications

- **Absolute**:

  - Hypersensitivity to cephalosporins

- **Relative**:

  - History of severe hypersensitivity to penicillins (due to cross-reactivity)

## Side Effects

1. **Common**:

   - Diarrhoea
   - Nausea, vomiting
   - Rash
   - Injection site pain/inflammation

2. **Serious**:

   - Pseudomembranous colitis (C. difficile)
   - Hypersensitivity reactions (anaphylaxis, urticaria)
   - Blood dyscrasias (e.g., neutropenia, thrombocytopenia)
   - Elevated liver enzymes
   - Seizures (especially in high doses or renal impairment)

## Route of Administration

- **IV (intravenous)**
- **IM (intramuscular)**

## Dosage

### Adults

- **Usual**: `1–2 g IV/IM every 8–12 hours`
- **Severe infections**: `Up to 12 g/day divided every 6–8 hours`
- **Meningitis**: `2 g IV every 4–6 hours`

### Children

- **Neonates (0–7 days)**: `50 mg/kg/dose every 12 hours`
- **Neonates (8–28 days)**: `50 mg/kg/dose every 8 hours`
- **Infants & children**: `50–180 mg/kg/day in 2–6 divided doses`
- **Meningitis**: `200 mg/kg/day in divided doses (max 12 g/day)`

## Use in Pregnancy

- **Pregnancy Category B (US FDA)**
  → No evidence of fetal harm in animal studies
  → Use when clearly needed

## Use in Lactating Mothers

- Excreted in small amounts in breast milk
- Generally considered **safe in breastfeeding**
  → Monitor infant for diarrhoea or candidiasis
