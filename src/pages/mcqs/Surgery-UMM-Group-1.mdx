**1. 75-year-old female presents with displaced intracapsular fracture of the femoral neck.**

<details>
<summary>A. Hemiarthroplasty is the preferred treatment for this patient.</summary>
`True` - Elderly patients with displaced intracapsular femoral neck fractures usually benefit from hemiarthroplasty to allow early mobilization and reduce complications.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Total hip replacement is indicated for active, healthy elderly patients.</summary>
`True` - Active, medically fit elderly patients may benefit from total hip replacement for better function and lower risk of acetabular erosion.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Non-surgical management is appropriate for this fracture.</summary>
~~False~~ - Non-surgical management is usually reserved for non-ambulatory or extremely frail patients; most displaced fractures require surgery.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Internal fixation with screws is the best option for displaced fractures.</summary>
~~False~~ - Internal fixation has high failure rates in elderly with displaced intracapsular fractures due to poor bone quality.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) is a potential complication.</summary>
`True` - DVT is a common complication post-hip fracture, especially if immobilized.
</details>

**2. 10-year-old boy with fever, thigh swelling, tenderness, periosteal elevation on X-ray.**

<details>
<summary>A. The most likely diagnosis is osteomyelitis.</summary>
`True` - Fever, localized pain, swelling, elevated WBC/CRP, and periosteal elevation on X-ray indicate acute hematogenous osteomyelitis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Staphylococcus aureus is the most common causative organism.</summary>
`True` - S. aureus is the predominant pathogen in pediatric acute osteomyelitis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Oral antibiotics are sufficient for treatment.</summary>
~~False~~ - Severe or acute cases usually require IV antibiotics; oral antibiotics alone are often insufficient initially.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Surgical debridement may be required.</summary>
`True` - Surgical intervention is needed if there is abscess, sequestrum, or failure to respond to antibiotics.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Both life and limb are in danger with this infection.</summary>
`True` - Severe osteomyelitis can threaten limb function; systemic spread may risk life.
</details>
