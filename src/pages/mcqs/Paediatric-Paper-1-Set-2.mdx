**1. Followings are true regarding the pattern of growth**

<details>
<summary>A. rate of growth is maximum in utero</summary>
`True` - Growth velocity → highest in fetal life (esp. 2nd trimester) → slows after birth.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. growth of nervous system is most rapid between 6 to 10 years</summary>
~~False~~ - Brain/nervous system growth → rapid in first 2–3 years → not at 6–10 years.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. tonsils of a child of 10 years old is smaller than that of an adult</summary>
~~False~~ - Tonsils → relatively larger in childhood (peak at 4–7 yrs) → regress in adulthood.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. rapid genital growth is seen at the onset of puberty</summary>
`True` - Pubertal spurt → activation of HPG axis → rapid genital growth.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. upper segment and lower segment ratio is highest in the newborn period</summary>
`True` - Newborn body → relatively short legs → US:LS ratio ~1.7 at birth (highest).
</details>
