**1. Followings are true regarding the pattern of growth**

<details>
<summary>A. rate of growth is maximum in utero</summary>
`True` - Growth velocity → highest in fetal life (esp. 2nd trimester) → slows after birth.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. growth of nervous system is most rapid between 6 to 10 years</summary>
~~False~~ - Brain/nervous system growth → rapid in first 2–3 years → not at 6–10 years.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. tonsils of a child of 10 years old is smaller than that of an adult</summary>
~~False~~ - Tonsils → relatively larger in childhood (peak at 4–7 yrs) → regress in adulthood.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. rapid genital growth is seen at the onset of puberty</summary>
`True` - Pubertal spurt → activation of HPG axis → rapid genital growth.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. upper segment and lower segment ratio is highest in the newborn period</summary>
`True` - Newborn body → relatively short legs → US:LS ratio ~1.7 at birth (highest).
</details>

**2. Following factors influence the physical growth and development of children**

<details>
<summary>A. height of both parents</summary>
`True` - Genetic potential → mid-parental height → major determinant.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. maternal smoking during pregnancy</summary>
`True` - Nicotine → intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR).
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. birth asphyxia</summary>
`True` - Hypoxic insult → CNS damage → poor growth and development.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. emotional deprivation</summary>
`True` - Psychosocial dwarfism → stress hormones suppress GH axis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. bronchial Asthma</summary>
`True` - Chronic illness + steroids → impaired growth.
</details>

**4. Causes of short stature include**

<details>
<summary>A. hypothyroidism</summary>
`True` - Deficiency of thyroid hormone → growth retardation.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Down syndrome</summary>
`True` - Chromosomal abnormality → associated with short stature.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. congenital heart disease</summary>
`True` - Chronic hypoxia/illness → poor growth.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. rickets</summary>
`True` - Defective mineralization → growth plate abnormalities → short stature.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. marasmus</summary>
`True` - Severe protein-energy malnutrition → impaired linear growth.
</details>

<details>

**8. Poor prognostic factors for neonatal tetanus are**

</details>

<details>
<summary>B. hypothermia</summary>

</details>

<details>
<summary>C. generalized tonic-clonic seizures</summary>
`True` - Severe CNS involvement → poor prognosis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. period of onset shorter than 48 hours</summary>
`True` - Rapid onset after first symptom → poor outcome.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. jaundice</summary>
~~False~~ - Not a recognized prognostic factor in neonatal tetanus.
</details>

**10. Early signs of rickets include**

</details>
