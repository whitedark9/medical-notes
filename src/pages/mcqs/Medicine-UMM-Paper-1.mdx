**1. Causes of sudden cardiac death include**

<details>
<summary>A. coronary artery disease</summary>
`True` - Most common cause of sudden cardiac death due to acute myocardial infarction or arrhythmia.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. hypertrophic cardiomyopathy</summary>
`True` - Common cause in young athletes due to ventricular arrhythmias.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. mitral stenosis</summary>
~~False~~ - Rarely causes sudden death; death is usually due to progressive heart failure.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. myocarditis</summary>
`True` - Can cause sudden death due to ventricular arrhythmia or acute pump failure.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. pericarditis</summary>
~~False~~ - Usually presents with chest pain and pericardial effusion; sudden death is uncommon.
</details>

**2. Regarding dilated cardiomyopathy**

<details>
<summary>A. arrhythmia may occur at any stage</summary>
`True` - Arrhythmias can occur throughout the course due to ventricular dilation and fibrosis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. alcohol may be one of the causes</summary>
`True` - Alcoholic cardiomyopathy is a well-recognized cause.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. HIV is one of the risk factors for this condition</summary>
`True` - HIV infection can lead to myocarditis and subsequent dilated cardiomyopathy.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. most cases are due to autosomal recessive trait</summary>
~~False~~ - Most familial cases are autosomal dominant, not recessive.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. thromboembolism may occur</summary>
`True` - Blood stasis in dilated chambers predisposes to mural thrombus and embolism.
</details>
