# Surgery UMM Group 3


**1. Patients with osteoarthritis of the hip**

<details>
<summary>A. May present with pain in the knee</summary>
`True` - Hip OA can cause referred pain to the knee.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. May benefit from osteotomy</summary>
`True` - Osteotomy can be used in younger patients to realign joint and delay arthroplasty.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Have a wide joint spaces on X-ray due to bony destruction</summary>
~~False~~ - OA typically shows **narrowed** joint spaces due to cartilage loss.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Rarely suffer pain when resting at night</summary>
`True` - Mild OA pain may improve at rest, though severe cases can disturb sleep.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Suffer joint stiffness and pain that is often worse in the morning</summary>
~~False~~ - OA stiffness usually improves with activity; morning stiffness is brief (\<30 min) compared to inflammatory arthritis.
</details>

**2. Regarding thyrotoxicosis**

<details>
<summary>A. It is the usual sequel of Hashimoto’s disease</summary>
~~False~~ - Hashimoto’s usually causes hypothyroidism, not thyrotoxicosis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. It increases vascularity of the thyroid gland</summary>
`True` - Hyperthyroidism increases thyroid blood flow.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Graves’ disease is the result of excess pituitary TSH</summary>
~~False~~ - Graves’ is due to TSH receptor antibodies, not pituitary TSH excess.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. It may cause cardiac arrhythmias</summary>
`True` - Atrial fibrillation is common in thyrotoxicosis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. It can be caused by solitary cold nodule</summary>
~~False~~ - Cold nodules are non-functioning and usually do not cause thyrotoxicosis.
</details>

**3. Regarding Buerger’s disease (thromboangitis obliterans)**

<details>
<summary>A. It is the occlusion disease of the end arterioles of the extremities</summary>
`True` - Buerger’s affects small and medium arteries and veins of extremities.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. It causes an acute ischaemia of the lower limb</summary>
~~False~~ - It usually causes **chronic** ischemia, not acute.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. It affects male under 40</summary>
`True` - Typically affects young male smokers under 40.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Intermittent claudication is the common presentation</summary>
`True` - Claudication is the usual initial symptom.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Femoral-popliteal bypass is the beneficial procedure</summary>
~~False~~ - Bypass is rarely feasible due to distal vessel involvement.
</details>

**4. Causes of acute pancreatitis include**

<details>
<summary>A. Alcoholism</summary>
`True` - Alcohol is a common cause of acute pancreatitis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Trauma</summary>
`True` - Blunt abdominal trauma can precipitate pancreatitis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Hypercalcaemia</summary>
`True` - High calcium can trigger pancreatic enzyme activation.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Hyperparathyroidism</summary>
`True` - Secondary hypercalcemia from hyperparathyroidism can precipitate pancreatitis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. ERCP</summary>
`True` - ERCP can induce iatrogenic pancreatitis.
</details>

**5. Precipitating physiological events to the metabolic response to injury include**

<details>
<summary>A. Hypothermia</summary>
`True` - Hypothermia worsens metabolic stress response.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Tissue oedema</summary>
`True` - Tissue injury and edema increase metabolic response.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Early mobilization</summary>
~~False~~ - Early mobilization is beneficial, not a precipitating factor.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Starvation</summary>
`True` - Starvation exaggerates catabolic response to injury.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Continuing haemorrhage</summary>
`True` - Ongoing blood loss increases stress response.
</details>

<details>
<summary>A. The patient being nursed in a darkened room</summary>
~~False~~ - Not essential; focus is on airway, breathing, circulation.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. An x-ray of the skull</summary>
~~False~~ - CT is preferred; skull X-ray is rarely diagnostic.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. The patient being nursed strictly in the supine position</summary>
~~False~~ - Head elevation is recommended to reduce ICP.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Immediate tracheostomy</summary>
~~False~~ - Tracheostomy is only indicated if prolonged airway management required.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Using coma scale</summary>
`True` - GCS assessment is essential to monitor level of consciousness.
</details>

**9. Clinical features of shock include**

<details>
<summary>A. Low cardiac output</summary>
`True` - Shock is characterized by inadequate tissue perfusion, often with low cardiac output.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. A rapid thready pulse</summary>
`True` - Tachycardia with weak pulse is typical.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Decreased respiratory rate</summary>
~~False~~ - Respiratory rate is usually increased due to compensation.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Fall in urine output</summary>
`True` - Oliguria occurs due to hypoperfusion.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Warm clammy extremities</summary>
`True` - Peripheral vasoconstriction and cool extremities are common in hypovolemic/ cardiogenic shock; distributive shock may be warm.
</details>

**10. In the management of cardiac arrest**

<details>
<summary>A. Airway must be patent</summary>
`True` - Airway management is crucial for oxygenation.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Resuscitation should be started only when an assistant is present</summary>
~~False~~ - Immediate CPR is required; do not wait for help.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Endotracheal tube may be inserted</summary>
`True` - Advanced airway can be used if trained personnel available.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. ECG should be done</summary>
`True` - To identify cardiac rhythm and guide treatment.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Pulse is checked by palpating the radial artery</summary>
~~False~~ - Central pulse (carotid) is preferred; radial may be absent.
</details>

**11. Dysphagia**

<details>

<details>

</details>

**13. Fracture neck of femur**

<details>
<summary>A. Occur most commonly in elderly female</summary>
`True` - Osteoporotic fractures of femoral neck are common in elderly women.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Present with pain and internally rotated limb</summary>
~~False~~ - Classic presentation: shortened, externally rotated limb.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Is best treated by early internal fixation</summary>
~~False~~ - Displaced fractures in elderly are usually treated with arthroplasty.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Is associated with 30-days mortality of about 30%</summary>
~~False~~ - 30-day mortality is lower (~5–10%), 1-year mortality is higher (~30%).
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Requires prolonged bed rest</summary>
~~False~~ - Early mobilization post-surgery is preferred to reduce complications.
</details>

**14. A fistula will persist if**

<details>
<summary>A. A foreign body is present</summary>
`True` - Foreign material prevents healing.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Tuberculosis is present</summary>
`True` - TB fistulae persist without specific therapy.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. The wall becomes lined with epithelium</summary>
`True` - Epithelialization prevents closure.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. There is unrelieved obstruction proximal to the fistula</summary>
`True` - Obstruction maintains fistula output.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Intervening abscess cavity present</summary>
`True` - Abscess prevents closure and promotes persistent fistula.
</details>

**16. Regarding peptic ulceration**

<details>
<summary>A. H. pylori is a gram-positive bacillus</summary>
~~False~~ - H. pylori is a **gram-negative** curved bacillus.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Duodenal is more common than gastric ulceration</summary>
`True` - Duodenal ulcers are more common than gastric ulcers.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Zollinger-Ellison syndrome is associated with gastrin hypo-secretion</summary>
~~False~~ - It is associated with **gastrin hypersecretion**.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. H2-blockers will heal 85-95% of duodenal ulcers in 8 weeks</summary>
`True` - H2 blockers are effective in healing most duodenal ulcers within 6–8 weeks.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Triple therapy can eradicate H. Pylori in 80% of patients in one week</summary>
~~False~~ - Triple therapy usually requires 1–2 weeks for 80–90% eradication.
</details>

**18. Risk factors for perforation of appendix**

<details>
<summary>A. Extreme of age</summary>
`True` - Very young and elderly patients have higher risk of perforation.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Diabetes mellitus</summary>
`True` - Immunocompromised states increase risk of complications.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Faecolith obstruction</summary>
`True` - Obstruction promotes increased intraluminal pressure leading to perforation.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Retrocaecal appendix</summary>
~~False~~ - Position may delay diagnosis but does not directly increase perforation risk.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Immunosuppression</summary>
`True` - Reduced immune response increases risk of perforation.
</details>

**21. General effects of burn injury**

<details>
<summary>A. Are independent of the size of the burn area</summary>
~~False~~ - Severity depends on total body surface area burned.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. Include immune suppression</summary>
`True` - Burns impair immune response.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. Result in decreased urinary nitrogen concentrations</summary>
~~False~~ - Burns cause **catabolic state**, increasing nitrogen excretion.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Include hypoalbuminemia</summary>
`True` - Protein loss occurs due to fluid shifts and catabolism.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. Include hyponatremia</summary>
`True` - Fluid shifts and resuscitation may lead to hyponatremia.
</details>
