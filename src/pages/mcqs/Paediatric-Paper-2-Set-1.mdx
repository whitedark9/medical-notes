**1. The differential diagnosis of pansystolic murmur in lower left sternal edge:**

<details>
<summary>A. VSD</summary>
`True` - VSD → left-to-right shunt → pansystolic murmur best heard at LLSE.
</details>

<details>

`True` - Tricuspid regurgitation → pansystolic murmur → LLSE, ↑ with inspiration.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. MI(transmitted)</summary>
`True` - MR murmur can transmit to LLSE, though primary site = apex.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. Functional</summary>
`True` - Functional murmurs may mimic pansystolic at LLSE.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. PDA with pulmonary hypertension</summary>
~~False~~ - PDA → continuous murmur at left infraclavicular, not pansystolic LLSE.
</details>

**4. Management of heart failure includes**

<details>
<summary>A. preload regulation</summary>
`True` - Diuretics → ↓ venous return → ↓ preload.
</details>

<details>
<summary>B. afterload reduction</summary>
`True` - ACE inhibitors/vasodilators → ↓ afterload → ↑ CO.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. improvement of cardiac contractility</summary>
`True` - Inotropes (digoxin) → ↑ contractility in failure.
</details>

<details>
<summary>D. control of heart rate</summary>
`True` - β-blockers, digoxin → prevent tachycardia-induced failure.
</details>

<details>
<summary>E. surgical treatment if indicated</summary>
`True` - Correct structural lesion (e.g. VSD closure, valve surgery).
</details>

**16. Complications of acute watery diarrhoea**

</details>

<details>

~~False~~ - Diarrhoea → potassium loss → hypokalaemia, not hyperkalaemia.
</details>

<details>
<summary>C. hypotonic dehydration</summary>
`True` - Na⁺ loss > water loss → hypotonic dehydration.
</details>

<details>

~~False~~ - Diarrhoea does not cause hyperglycemia; may cause hypoglycemia due to poor intake.
