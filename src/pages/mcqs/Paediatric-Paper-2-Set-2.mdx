**1. Complications of VSD include**

<details>
  <summary>A. repeated chest infection</summary>
  `True` - Large VSD → L→R shunt → pulmonary overcirculation → pulmonary
  congestion → recurrent chest infections.
</details>

<details>
  <summary>B. Eisenmenger syndrome</summary>
  `True` - Chronic L→R shunt → pulmonary hypertension → reversal of shunt (R→L)
  → cyanosis (Eisenmenger).
</details>

<details>
  <summary>C. heart failure</summary>
  `True` - Large VSD → volume overload of LV + pulmonary circulation → CHF in
  infancy.
</details>

<details>
  <summary>D. infective endocarditis</summary>
  `True` - Turbulent flow across VSD → endothelial damage → nidus for infection
  → IE.
</details>

<details>
  <summary>E. growth retardation</summary>
  `True` - Chronic CHF + recurrent infections → poor nutrition → growth
  retardation.
</details>

**2. Management of hypercyanotic spell in Tetralogy of Fallot include**

<details>
  <summary>A. left lateral position</summary>
  ~~False~~ - Not beneficial; knee-chest position is recommended to ↑ systemic
  resistance.
</details>

<details>

</details>

<details>
  <summary>C. s.c./i.m. morphine</summary>
  `True` - Morphine → sedation + ↓ hyperpnea → improved pulmonary blood flow.
</details>

<details>

</details>

<details>

  `True` - O₂ → relieves hypoxia though effect is limited compared to
  positional/medical measures.
</details>
