# Surgery UMM Group 4


**1. In Cholangiohepatitis**

<details>
<summary>1. Jaundice is deep and progressive</summary>
`True` - Cholangiohepatitis often causes progressive jaundice due to bile duct obstruction.
</details>

<details>
<summary>2. High temperature is usually associated with chills and rigor</summary>
`True` - Fever with rigors is a hallmark of ascending cholangitis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>3. RHC pain is recurrent</summary>
`True` - Right hypochondrial pain is common and may recur with episodes.
</details>

<details>
<summary>4. Gallbladder may be enlarged and tender</summary>
`True` - Inflammation can cause a palpable tender gallbladder (Courvoisier sign).
</details>

<details>
<summary>5. Skin itchiness is uncommon</summary>
~~False~~ - Pruritus is common due to cholestasis.
</details>

**2. In acute pyogenic osteomyelitis**

<details>
<summary>1. Life threatening septicaemia is a presentation in neonates</summary>
`True` - Neonates may present with systemic sepsis.
</details>

<details>
<summary>2. Organisms settle near the metaphysic at the growing end of a long bone</summary>
`True` - Vascular anatomy favors metaphyseal infection.
</details>

<details>
<summary>3. Plain X-ray shows no abnormality for 3 weeks</summary>
`True` - Early osteomyelitis may not be visible on X-ray.
</details>

<details>
<summary>4. The sequestrum appears radiolucent compared to surrounding bone</summary>
~~False~~ - Sequestrum is dense (radiopaque), not radiolucent.
</details>

<details>
<summary>5. Staph. aureus is the commonest infecting agent</summary>
`True` - Staphylococcus aureus is the most frequent cause.
</details>

**3. Regarding parenteral fluid therapy**

<details>
<summary>1. plasma albumin is 20%</summary>
`True` - 20% albumin is a hyperoncotic preparation used in fluid therapy.
</details>

<details>
<summary>2. 5% dextrose in saline is hypotonic</summary>
`True` - 5% dextrose in 0.9% saline is slightly hypotonic after metabolism of glucose.
</details>

<details>
<summary>3. Ringer's lactate contains sodium, potassium and chloride in about the same concentration as in plasma</summary>
`True` - Composition is designed to approximate plasma electrolytes.
</details>

<details>
<summary>4. Isotonic saline solution is 4.3% solution</summary>
~~False~~ - Normal saline is 0.9% NaCl.
</details>

<details>
<summary>5. Prolonged administration of 5% dextrose can cause hyponatraemia</summary>
`True` - Water load without electrolytes can lead to dilutional hyponatremia.
</details>

**5. Factors which predispose to wound infection include**

<details>
<summary>1. Development of a wound haematoma</summary>
`True` - Hematoma promotes bacterial growth.
</details>

<details>
<summary>2. The presence of devitalized tissue in the wound</summary>
`True` - Necrotic tissue increases infection risk.
</details>

<details>
<summary>3. Antibiotic administration during surgery</summary>
~~False~~ - Prophylactic antibiotics reduce infection risk.
</details>

<details>
<summary>4. Foreign material in the wound</summary>
`True` - Foreign bodies can serve as a nidus for infection.
</details>

<details>
<summary>5. Impaired blood supply</summary>
`True` - Poor perfusion decreases host defenses, increasing infection risk.
</details>

</details>

<details>
<summary>2. A rapid thready pulse</summary>

</details>

<details>
<summary>3. Decreased respiratory rate</summary>

</details>

<details>

</details>

<details>
<summary>5. Warm clammy extremities</summary>

</details>
