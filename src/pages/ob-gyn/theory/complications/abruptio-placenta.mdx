# Abruptio Placentae (Placental Abruption)

> **Premature separation of a normally situated placenta** (i.e., in upper uterine segment) **after 24 weeks** and **before delivery of the fetus**

## Types / Classification:

1. **Revealed (external) hemorrhage**

   - Blood escapes through cervix → **vaginal bleeding visible**

2. **Concealed hemorrhage**

   - Blood collects behind placenta → **no vaginal bleeding**

3. **Mixed type**

   - Some blood escapes, some remains concealed

## Grades / Severity (Clinical Grading):

| Grade                  | Features                                                                     |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Grade 0**            | Diagnosed retrospectively (e.g., after delivery, blood clot behind placenta) |
| **Grade 1 (Mild)**     | Slight vaginal bleeding, no fetal/maternal compromise                        |
| **Grade 2 (Moderate)** | Moderate bleeding, **fetal distress**, mother stable                         |
| **Grade 3 (Severe)**   | Heavy bleeding, **fetal death**, maternal shock, DIC                         |

## Etiology / Risk Factors:

1. Maternal hypertension (most common)
1. Pre-eclampsia / eclampsia
1. Trauma (e.g., RTA, domestic violence)
1. Smoking
1. Cocaine abuse
1. Multiparity
1. Previous abruption
1. Polyhydramnios with sudden decompression
1. Premature rupture of membranes
1. Thrombophilias

## Pathophysiology:

- Insult (e.g., hypertension, trauma) → **rupture of uteroplacental vessels**
  → **Bleeding into decidua basalis**
  → **Placental separation**
  → ↓ uteroplacental blood flow
  → **Fetal hypoxia or death**
  → **Retroplacental clot** forms → uterine irritability, DIC may follow

## Clinical Features:

### 1. Symptoms:

- **Painful vaginal bleeding**
- **Sudden onset of abdominal pain**
- Reduced fetal movements
- Symptoms of shock (in severe cases)

### 2. Signs

- **Uterus tense, tender, rigid (woody hard)**
- Fundal height may increase (due to concealed blood)
- Fetal heart rate abnormal or **absent**
- **Couvelaire uterus** (blue, ecchymosed due to bleeding into myometrium)
- **Signs of DIC** in severe cases

## Diagnosis

### History:

- **Painful, continuous bleeding** (vs. painless in previa)
- Risk factors (HTN, trauma, smoking)

### Physical Examination:

- **Tense, tender uterus**
- Fundus higher than expected
- Fetal heart → may be absent or abnormal
- Shock may be **out of proportion to visible bleeding** (if concealed)

### Investigations

#### a. For Diagnosis:

- **Clinical diagnosis** (mainstay)
- **Ultrasound** → low sensitivity (\~25%); may show retroplacental clot

#### b. For Cause:

- **BP monitoring**, urine dipstick (pre-eclampsia)
- History of trauma, drug use

#### c. For Complications:

- **CBC** → anemia, thrombocytopenia
- **Coagulation profile** → PT, APTT, fibrinogen ↓ in DIC
- **Kleihauer test** → fetomaternal haemorrhage if Rh negative
- **CTG** → fetal distress or death

## Management

### ABCDEF Emergency Approach

#### Airway, Breathing, Circulation

- High-flow **oxygen**
- IV access (2 large-bore)
- **IV fluids + crossmatch blood** (4–6 units)
- **Monitor vitals**, urine output (catheter)

#### Disability (CNS status)

- Monitor consciousness, shock, seizures

#### Evaluate for delivery

- **Fetal status** (CTG, USG)
- **Maternal stability**
- **Gestational age**

#### Find and treat cause

- Manage hypertension, stop trauma, manage coagulopathy

## Definitive Management

### Viable fetus, no distress:

- Close monitoring if mild, \<37 weeks
- **Corticosteroids** if \<34 weeks (Betamethasone 12 mg IM q24h × 2)

### Fetal distress or dead fetus:

- **Immediate delivery**
- **Mode:**

  - **Vaginal delivery** preferred if fetus dead and no contraindication
  - **LSCS** if fetus alive and distress or cervix not favorable

### Severe cases:

- Blood & component therapy (PRBCs, FFP, platelets)
- Correct DIC aggressively
- **Oxytocin 10 IU IM or IV** post-delivery to prevent PPH

## Postpartum Care

- Observe for **PPH**, DIC
- Treat anemia
- Monitor renal function (risk of ATN from shock)
- **Anti-D immunoglobulin** if Rh-negative

## Contraception Advice

### Preferred:

1. **Progestin-only methods**:

   - **DMPA 150 mg IM q12w** (after 6 weeks postpartum)
   - **Implant (Implanon)** – long acting, reversible
   - **POP** (desogestrel 75 mcg)

2. **IUD (after involution, no infection signs)**

3. **Tubal ligation** if family complete

### Avoid:

- Combined OCPs early postpartum (risk of thrombosis)
- Avoid IUD if infection/DIC/anaemia

### Best choice

- **DMPA or Implant** – safe, long-acting, low risk of uterine bleeding or complications
- **Tubal ligation during LSCS** if desired
