# Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)

> Blood loss **≥500 mL after vaginal delivery** or **≥1000 mL after cesarean** or any amount of blood loss causing **hemodynamic instability**

## Types

| Type                 | Timing           | Definition                                  |
| -------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **Primary (Early)**  | \<24 hours       | Most common (usually within 2 hrs)          |
| **Secondary (Late)** | 24 hrs – 6 weeks | Often due to infection or retained products |

## Causes of Primary PPH – The "4 Ts"

| Cause               | Description                             | Common Conditions                                                         |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tone (70%)**      | Uterine atony (most common)             | Overdistension (twins, polyhydramnios), prolonged labor, oxytocin overuse |
| **Tissue (10%)**    | Retained placenta/membranes             | Incomplete separation, morbid adherence (accreta, increta)                |
| **Trauma (20%)**    | Lacerations, uterine rupture, inversion | Instrumental delivery, episiotomy, CS, grand multiparity                  |
| **Thrombin (\<1%)** | Coagulopathies                          | DIC, pre-eclampsia, HELLP, IUFD, sepsis, abruption                        |

## Causes of Secondary PPH

- **Retained products of conception**
- **Endometritis** (infection)
- **Subinvolution** of uterus
- **Coagulopathy** (e.g., von Willebrand disease)
- **Choriocarcinoma** (rare)

## Complications of PPH

### Maternal Complications

| Type                   | Examples                                                    |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hypovolemia**        | Shock, multi-organ failure                                  |
| **Anemia**             | Postpartum fatigue, delayed healing                         |
| **Sheehan’s Syndrome** | Pituitary necrosis → lactation failure, amenorrhea          |
| **Infection**          | Endometritis, sepsis                                        |
| **Operative risks**    | Hysterectomy, bladder/ureter injury                         |
| **Death**              | Leading cause of maternal mortality in developing countries |

### Neonatal Complications (Indirect via maternal compromise)

- Prematurity (if emergency delivery)
- Poor breastfeeding
- Neglect due to maternal illness

## Management

> Immediate, multidisciplinary, stepwise and protocol-based

### I. Stepwise Emergency Management

#### A – Airway

- Ensure patency
- Oxygen (10–15 L/min via non-rebreather mask)

#### B – Breathing

- Monitor respiratory rate, O₂ saturation

#### C – Circulation

- **2 large bore IV lines** (14/16G)
- **Fluids:** Rapid infusion of crystalloids (1–2 L NS or RL)
- **Send blood:**

  - FBC, blood group + crossmatch (4–6 units)
  - Coagulation profile (PT, aPTT, INR)
  - Fibrinogen, U\&E, LFT
  - ABG/lactate (for perfusion)

- **Blood transfusion:**

  - If >1000 mL loss or ongoing bleeding
  - **Massive transfusion protocol** if >1500 mL loss or unstable:

    - 4 units PRBC : 4 units FFP : 1 pack platelets
    - Fibrinogen (Cryoprecipitate) if \<2 g/L

#### D – Disability (CNS, Monitoring)

- GCS, pain score
- Foley catheter with urometer → monitor UO ≥30 mL/hr
- Frequent BP, pulse every 10–15 min

#### E – Exposure

- Remove wet linen
- Maintain warmth to avoid hypothermia
- Keep patient in Trendelenburg or left lateral position

---

### II. Definitive Management

#### 1. TONE (Uterine Atony – 70% of PPH)

##### First line – Uterotonics + Uterine Massage

- **Oxytocin** 10 IU IM or IV bolus
  → then **Infusion** 20–40 IU in 500 mL NS/RL over 4 hours
- **Ergometrine** 0.2 mg IM/IV
  → _Avoid in hypertension/pre-eclampsia_
- **Misoprostol** 800–1000 µg PR/oral/sublingual
- **Carboprost (15-methyl PGF2α)** 250 µg IM q15 min (max 2 mg)
  → _Avoid in asthma, cardiac disease_
- **Tranexamic acid** 1 g IV over 10 min (within 3 hrs), repeat once after 30 min if bleeding continues

##### Second line – Mechanical Measures

- **Bimanual uterine compression**
- **Uterine massage**
- **Uterine packing** (gauze if balloon unavailable)

##### Third line – Intrauterine Tamponade

- **Bakri balloon**, **Sengstaken-Blakemore**, or **condom catheter**
  → Insert into uterus, fill with 300–500 mL saline
  → Leave for 12–24 hours

##### Fourth line – Surgery

- **Uterine compression sutures** (B-Lynch suture)

  - Vertical compression with delayed-absorbable suture

- **Uterine artery ligation** (O'Leary suture)
- **Internal iliac artery ligation**
- **Uterine artery embolization** (if interventional radiology available)
- **Subtotal or total hysterectomy** (last resort)

---

#### 2. TISSUE (Retained Placenta / Clots / Membranes)

- **Inspect placenta** after delivery: any missing cotyledons?
- **Manual removal of placenta**:

  - Under anesthesia in theatre
  - Antibiotic cover
  - Gentle exploration of uterus

- **Curettage** only if retained bits remain after removal
- Use **ultrasound** to confirm RPOC if unsure

---

#### 3. TRAUMA (Genital Tract Tears, Uterine Rupture, Inversion)

##### Vaginal / Cervical / Perineal Tears

- Systematic visual and speculum exam
- Repair with absorbable sutures under good lighting and analgesia

##### Uterine Rupture

- Immediate laparotomy
- Repair if feasible
- Hysterectomy if extensive or bleeding uncontrolled

##### Uterine Inversion

- **Immediate repositioning**:

  - Johnson’s method (manual)
  - Use **tocolytics** (e.g., nitroglycerin 50–100 µg IV or terbutaline 250 µg SC) to relax uterus before repositioning
  - After reposition → give **oxytocin**

---

#### 4. THROMBIN (Coagulopathy)

- Suspect if bleeding continues despite firm uterus and no trauma
- **DIC screen**: Platelets, PT, APTT, fibrinogen, D-dimer
- Management:

  - **Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)** – for clotting factors
  - **Platelets** – if \<50 × 10⁹/L and bleeding
  - **Cryoprecipitate** – if fibrinogen \<2 g/L
  - Hematology consult if persistent

---

### III. Adjunct Therapies

- **Tranexamic Acid**: Early use reduces mortality

  - Dose: 1 g IV over 10 min (within 3 hours), repeat in 30 min if needed

- **Recombinant factor VIIa**: For severe PPH unresponsive to all standard measures (last resort)

---

### IV. Ongoing Monitoring and ICU Referral

- ICU care if:

  - Blood loss >1500–2000 mL
  - Ongoing need for transfusion
  - Multi-organ dysfunction

- Monitor:

  - Hemoglobin daily
  - Vitals 4 hourly
  - Input/output strictly

---

### V. Postpartum Care and Follow-up

#### Maternal

- **Treat anemia**: IV iron or transfusion as needed
- **Emotional support**, especially after surgical interventions
- **Counsel on future pregnancy risk** (especially after uterine surgery)

#### Neonatal

- Resuscitation if compromised at birth
- Encourage **breastfeeding** if stable

---

### VI. Prevention (in all deliveries)

- **Active Management of 3rd Stage of Labor (AMTSL):**

  - Oxytocin 10 IU IM after delivery of anterior shoulder
  - Controlled cord traction
  - Uterine massage post-placenta

---

### Summary Algorithm (Mnemonic)

**“C-B-U-B-T”** =
**Call for help → Bimanual → Uterotonics → Balloon → Theatre**
