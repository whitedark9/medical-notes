# Vaginal Birth After Caesarean (VBAC)

## Definition

- **VBAC**: Vaginal delivery in a woman who has had a previous **Lower Segment Caesarean Section (LSCS)**.
- **TOLAC (Trial of Labour After Caesarean)**: Planned attempt at vaginal delivery in women with previous LSCS.

## Modes of Delivery

1. **Planned Elective Repeat Caesarean Section (ERCS)**
2. **Planned VBAC** if criteria met

## Requirements for VBAC (Eligibility Criteria)

- One **previous uncomplicated LSCS** with **transverse lower uterine segment incision**
- **Singleton** pregnancy
- **Cephalic presentation**
- **≥37 weeks** gestation
- **No other contraindications to vaginal delivery** (e.g. placenta previa, malpresentation)
- **No history of uterine rupture**
- **No classical (vertical) uterine scar or multiple CS scars**
- **Facility available for emergency CS** (24-hour OT, blood, experienced team)
- **Informed consent obtained**

## Factors Influencing Successful VBAC

- Previous **vaginal delivery**, especially **VBAC**
- **Spontaneous onset of labour**
- **Bishop score ≥6**
- **Inter-delivery interval ≥18 months**
- **Normal maternal BMI**
- Absence of **complicating medical or obstetric conditions** (e.g. DM, PIH)

## Success Rate

- Overall: **60–80%**
- ↑ if previous vaginal birth or spontaneous labour
- ↓ if:

  - Labour induction needed
  - Obesity
  - Advanced maternal age
  - Short interpregnancy interval

## Risk Factors for Failed VBAC or Uterine Rupture

- **Induction of labour** (especially with prostaglandins)
- **Short inter-pregnancy interval (\<18 months)**
- **Macrosomia (>4 kg)**
- **Obstructed labour**
- **Previous uterine rupture**
- **Multiple previous CS**

## Management Plan

### 1. Pre-conception Care

- Review **surgical history** (operative note for scar type)
- Assess **risk vs benefit** of VBAC vs ERCS
- **Encourage inter-pregnancy interval ≥18 months**
- **Folic acid 400 mcg/day**
- Optimize comorbidities (e.g. DM, HTN)
- **Counsel** regarding next delivery plan

### 2. Antenatal Care (ANC)

- Early booking and regular follow-up
- Discuss mode of delivery options:

  - VBAC vs ERCS
  - Risks and success rates

- **Ultrasound**: fetal lie, placental location, fetal weight estimate
- **No prostaglandin for induction**
- Avoid unnecessary induction
- Monitor for:

  - **Scar tenderness**
  - **Signs of dehiscence**
  - Fetal growth

- Birth planning finalized by 36 weeks:

  - If VBAC → admit at onset of labour
  - If ERCS → book at 39 weeks

### 3. Intrapartum Management (During Trial of Labour)

- **Admit early in labour**
- **Continuous CTG monitoring**
- **IV line, blood grouping & crossmatch**
- **No prostaglandins for induction**
- If augmentation needed → **oxytocin cautiously**
- Monitor for:

  - **Scar tenderness**
  - **Fetal distress**
  - **Prolonged labour or failure to progress**

- Signs of **uterine rupture**:

  - Sudden pain, scar tenderness, fetal bradycardia, loss of station, hematuria
  - → **Immediate emergency CS**

### 4. Postpartum Management

- Routine postpartum care
- Monitor for:

  - **Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH)**
  - **Infection**
  - **Scar integrity**

- Assess psychological well-being
- Discuss **outcome** and **plan for next pregnancy**

### 5. Follow-Up

- 6-week postnatal visit
- Review:

  - Delivery outcome
  - Complications (if any)
  - Contraceptive needs

- Counsel on **interpregnancy interval**

## Contraception (Postpartum)

### Why Important?

- Prevent **short birth intervals** (\<18 months)
- Reduce risk of **uterine rupture** in future pregnancies

### Recommended Methods

#### Long-acting reversible contraception (LARC) – Preferred

- **Intrauterine Device (IUCD)**: Copper or LNG-IUS

  - Insert at **6 weeks postpartum** or immediately postpartum if safe

- **Progestogen Implant**: Implanon

  - **Safe in breastfeeding**, immediate use

#### Progestogen-only methods

- **Progestogen-only pill (POP)**: Requires strict daily compliance
- **Depot Medroxyprogesterone (DMPA)**: 3-monthly injections

#### Permanent

- **Tubal ligation** if family is complete

  - Can be done with CS or laparoscopically

#### Avoid Initially

- **Combined oral contraceptives** in early postpartum

  - ↑ Risk of thromboembolism
  - May affect milk production

## Counseling Points

- Explain:

  - **VBAC and ERCS options**
  - **Risks of uterine rupture**, emergency CS
  - **Success rates of VBAC**

- Ensure patient **understands and consents** to chosen mode
- Reinforce need for **hospital delivery**
- Discuss **contraception** postpartum
- Emphasize importance of **interpregnancy interval**
