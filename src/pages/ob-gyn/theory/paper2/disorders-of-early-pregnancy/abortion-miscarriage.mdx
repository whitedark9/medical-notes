# Abortion - Miscarriage

> **Abortion** (broader term): Termination of pregnancy before viability.

> **Miscarriage** (spontaneous abortion): **Natural loss** of pregnancy before **24 weeks** (in UK) or **20 weeks** (in US).

## Classification of Abortion

| Type                             | Definition                                                  |
| -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Spontaneous Abortion**      | Naturally occurring termination of pregnancy                |
| **2. Induced Abortion**          | Deliberate medical or surgical termination of pregnancy     |
| **3. Therapeutic Abortion**      | Medically indicated termination (maternal/fetal indication) |
| **4. Criminal/Illegal Abortion** | Performed in violation of the law or unsafe settings        |

## Classification of Spontaneous Abortion (Miscarriage)

| Type                       | Description                                                                  |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Threatened abortion** | Vaginal bleeding, **cervix closed**, viable fetus on scan                    |
| **2. Inevitable abortion** | Bleeding with **open cervix**, pregnancy cannot continue                     |
| **3. Incomplete abortion** | **Some products** of conception (POC) expelled, some retained                |
| **4. Complete abortion**   | **All POC expelled**, uterus empty, bleeding settled                         |
| **5. Missed abortion**     | **Fetal death with retention** of POC, no symptoms, non-viable fetus on scan |
| **6. Septic abortion**     | Miscarriage with **uterine infection**, may be life-threatening              |
| **7. Recurrent abortion**  | ≥ **3 consecutive** spontaneous miscarriages before 20 weeks                 |

## Diagnosis

### A. Clinical Features

| Feature               | Description                                          |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vaginal bleeding**  | Common in all types except missed miscarriage        |
| **Abdominal cramps**  | More in inevitable/incomplete types                  |
| **Passage of tissue** | Suggests incomplete or complete miscarriage          |
| **Amenorrhoea**       | Pregnancy symptom may disappear (missed miscarriage) |

### B. Physical Examination

- **Speculum exam**: bleeding, cervical os status (open or closed), tissue at os
- **Bimanual exam**: uterine size, tenderness

### C. Investigations

#### 1. Urine Pregnancy Test

- Usually positive

#### 2. Serum β-hCG

- May be falling or plateauing in miscarriage
- Used in serial monitoring if diagnosis uncertain

#### 3. Transvaginal Ultrasound (TVUS)

- **Gold standard**
- Findings:

| Type           | TVUS Finding                                                               |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Threatened** | Intrauterine sac, fetal pole ± heartbeat, closed cervix                    |
| **Missed**     | Non-viable fetus (no cardiac activity), gestational sac without fetal pole |
| **Incomplete** | Retained POC in uterus                                                     |
| **Complete**   | Empty uterus after confirmed IUP                                           |
| **Inevitable** | Low-lying gestational sac ± dilated cervix                                 |
| **Septic**     | Retained tissue + endometrial thickening ± gas                             |

#### 4. FBC, CRP, Blood group, Rh type

- To assess anemia, infection risk, Rh prophylaxis need

## Management of Spontaneous Abortion

- **Management varies by type** depending on:

  - Cervical status
  - Amount of bleeding
  - Retention of products of conception (POC)
  - Hemodynamic status
  - Patient's preference for future fertility

### I. Management Overview

| Type           | Cervix      | Uterus                   | Management Options                     |
| -------------- | ----------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| **Threatened** | Closed      | Viable pregnancy         | Expectant ± Supportive                 |
| **Inevitable** | Open        | Viable or non-viable     | Surgical or Medical                    |
| **Incomplete** | Open        | POC retained             | Surgical, Medical, or Expectant        |
| **Complete**   | Closed      | Empty                    | Observation only                       |
| **Missed**     | Closed      | Non-viable, retained POC | Expectant, Medical, Surgical           |
| **Septic**     | Open/Closed | Retained infected POC    | Resuscitation + IV Abx + Surgical      |
| **Recurrent**  | Variable    | Variable                 | Investigate cause, refer to specialist |

#### 1. Expectant Management

→ Let the uterus expel products naturally over time.

##### Indications:

- **Stable patient**
- Minimal bleeding
- Incomplete or missed abortion
- **Patient preference** to avoid intervention

##### Method:

- Monitor at home or clinic
- Weekly follow-up: **bleeding, pain, ultrasound, β-hCG**

##### Advantages:

- Natural, avoids surgery or drugs
- Preferred by some patients

##### Disadvantages:

- Unpredictable timing
- May lead to **prolonged bleeding**, infection
- Risk of **retained products** → delayed resolution

---

#### 2. Medical Management

##### Indications:

- **Missed, incomplete, or inevitable abortion** (esp. \<12–14 weeks)
- Patient prefers to avoid surgery
- **No infection or heavy bleeding**

##### Drugs:

- **Mifepristone 200 mg orally**, followed after 24–48 hrs by
- **Misoprostol 800 mcg vaginally** (or sublingually every 3 hrs × 3 doses)

> If Mifepristone not available: **Misoprostol alone** may be used

##### Monitoring:

- Bleeding starts within 4–6 hours
- Assess by USG in 1–2 weeks
- May repeat misoprostol if incomplete

##### Advantages:

- Non-invasive
- High success rate (\~85–95%)
- Can be done at home

##### Disadvantages:

- Cramping pain
- Unpredictable bleeding
- **Incomplete evacuation** in 5–10%
- Longer time to complete resolution than surgery

---

#### 3. Surgical Management

##### Indications:

- Heavy bleeding
- Signs of infection
- Failed medical/expectant management
- Patient preference
- Septic abortion

##### Procedure Options:

- **Manual Vacuum Aspiration (MVA)** → First trimester
- **Electric Vacuum Aspiration / Suction & Curettage**
- **Dilation and Evacuation (D\&E)** → If >12–14 weeks

##### Advantages:

- **Quick, definitive** treatment
- Useful in emergencies
- Less waiting or bleeding duration

##### Disadvantages:

- Requires **trained personnel & facility**
- Risk of:

  - Uterine perforation
  - Infection
  - Intrauterine adhesions (Asherman’s syndrome)

---

#### 4. Septic Abortion Management (Emergency)

| Step  | Management                                                                                                       |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **A** | Secure **Airway**, give **O2**                                                                                   |
| **B** | Support **Breathing**                                                                                            |
| **C** | **Circulation**: 2 wide-bore IV lines, fluids, blood grouping                                                    |
| **D** | Definitive: **IV broad-spectrum antibiotics** (e.g., ceftriaxone + metronidazole) → then **surgical evacuation** |
| **E** | Evaluate for complications (DIC, sepsis)                                                                         |
| **F** | Follow-up with cultures, repeat imaging                                                                          |

| Type           | Preferred Approach                                                                 |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Threatened** | Reassurance + USG monitoring ± progesterone support                                |
| **Inevitable** | Medical or surgical evacuation                                                     |
| **Incomplete** | Medical or surgical; expectant if mild                                             |

### Follow-Up for All Types

- **Rh-negative women** → give **Anti-D**
- **USG follow-up** to confirm complete evacuation
- Discuss **future contraception or fertility plan**

## Septic Abortion

### Causal Organisms

1. Aerobic bacteria

   - _Escherichia coli_
   - _Streptococcus pyogenes_ (Group A)
   - _Staphylococcus aureus_
   - _Enterococcus_ spp.
   - _Klebsiella_ spp.

1. Anaerobes

   - _Clostridium perfringens_
   - _Bacteroides fragilis_
   - _Peptostreptococcus_

1. Sexually transmitted pathogens
   - _Chlamydia trachomatis_
   - _Neisseria gonorrhoeae_
1. Polymicrobial infection
   - Mixed aerobic and anaerobic flora → most common

> ⚠️ **Clostridium** may cause **gas gangrene and sepsis** → life-threatening!

### Complications

#### A. Immediate (Acute)

| Complication                                     | Mechanism                                        |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Septicemia**                                   | Bacteria invade bloodstream → systemic infection |
| **Pelvic peritonitis**                           | Spread of infection to pelvic peritoneum         |
| **Tubo-ovarian abscess**                         | Extension to adnexa                              |
| **Endotoxic shock**                              | From Gram-negative organisms (esp. _E. coli_)    |
| **Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)** | Triggered by severe sepsis                       |
| **Renal failure**                                | Secondary to sepsis/hypoperfusion                |
| **Death**                                        | Due to overwhelming sepsis or multiorgan failure |

#### B. Delayed (Chronic)

| Complication                 | Mechanism                                                |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Chronic pelvic pain**      | Adhesions and infection sequelae                         |
| **Infertility**              | Tubal blockage, endometrial damage (Asherman's syndrome) |
| **Ectopic pregnancy**        | Tubal scarring                                           |
| **Recurrent pregnancy loss** | Endometrial destruction or intrauterine adhesions        |

### Prevention

#### A. Primary Prevention

1. **Safe abortion access**

   - Legal, accessible abortion services
   - Qualified personnel, sterile techniques

2. **Sexual health education**

   - Avoid unsafe sex, STI screening

3. **Effective contraception**

   - Prevent unintended pregnancies

4. **Antiseptic precautions**

   - During MVA or surgical abortion

#### B. Secondary Prevention

1. **Early recognition** of abortion-related infection signs
2. **Prompt antibiotic treatment** if signs of infection
3. **Complete evacuation** of retained products
4. **Postabortal care**

   - Counseling, follow-up, and contraception

#### C. Tertiary Prevention

- Minimize long-term damage:

  - **Timely surgical intervention**
  - **Treat adhesions or infertility**
  - Referral to **gynecology or fertility specialists**
