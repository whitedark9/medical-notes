# **Ectopic Pregnancy**

- Implantation of the **fertilized ovum outside the uterine cavity**
- Most common site: **ampullary portion** of fallopian tube

## **I. Common Sites of Ectopic Pregnancy**

| Site               | Frequency (%)                          |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| **Fallopian tube** | \~95% (ampullary > isthmic > fimbrial) |
| **Ovary**          | 0.5–1%                                 |
| **Cervix**         | \<1%                                   |
| **Abdominal**      | \~1%                                   |
| **Cesarean scar**  | Increasingly reported                  |

---

## **II. Risk Factors**

1. **Tubal damage**: PID, tubal surgery, sterilization
2. **Previous ectopic pregnancy**
3. **IVF or ART**
4. **Smoking**
5. **Intrauterine device (IUD)** in situ
6. **Congenital tubal anomalies**

---

## **III. Clinical Features**

| Feature                        | Description                                          |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Amenorrhoea**                | Typically **6–8 weeks** from LMP                     |
| **Abdominal pain**             | **Lower abdominal pain** (unilateral or generalized) |
| **Vaginal bleeding**           | Usually **light spotting**                           |
| **Shoulder tip pain**          | If **intraperitoneal bleeding** irritates diaphragm  |
| **Dizziness or collapse**      | Suggests **rupture** → hypovolemia                   |
| **Adnexal tenderness or mass** | On bimanual exam                                     |
| **Cervical motion tenderness** | Classic finding in pelvic exam                       |

> ⚠️ Always suspect ectopic in **reproductive-aged woman** with **amenorrhoea + abdominal pain ± bleeding**

---

## **IV. Investigations**

### **A. For Diagnosis**

#### 1. **Urine/Serum Pregnancy Test**

- **β-hCG positive** but typically **lower than expected** for gestational age
- Does **not exclude ectopic**

#### 2. **Transvaginal Ultrasound (TVUS)**

- **No intrauterine gestational sac** + β-hCG >1500 IU/L (discriminatory zone) → suspect ectopic
- May see:

  - **Adnexal mass or ring**
  - **Free fluid** in pouch of Douglas (suggests rupture)
  - **Pseudogestational sac** in uterus (misleading)

#### 3. **Serial β-hCG Measurements**

- In normal IUP: hCG **doubles every 48–72 hrs**
- In ectopic: hCG **rises slowly, plateaus or falls**

---

### **B. For Complications or Associated Risks**

- **CBC** → assess for **anemia or blood loss**
- **Group and crossmatch** → if surgical or rupture suspected
- **LFT/RFT** → pre-op workup
- **Progesterone level** (optional) → \<5 ng/mL suggests nonviable pregnancy

---

## **V. Complications**

### **A. Early Complications**

| Complication             | Mechanism                                         |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tubal rupture**        | Most dangerous → massive intraperitoneal bleeding |
| **Hemorrhagic shock**    | Due to rupture and blood loss                     |
| **Infection**            | If secondary infection of retained tissue         |
| **Pain and infertility** | Due to scarring and adhesions                     |

### **B. Long-term Complications**

| Complication                    | Mechanism                                |
| ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Infertility**                 | Tubal damage, adhesions                  |
| **Recurrent ectopic pregnancy** | Risk increases after 1st ectopic (\~10%) |
| **Chronic pelvic pain**         | Due to tubal scarring                    |

---

## **VI. Summary of Key Diagnostic Points**

| Tool                                   | Finding                                       |
| -------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **β-hCG >1500 + empty uterus on TVUS** | Suggests ectopic                              |
| **Adnexal mass + free fluid**          | Likely ruptured ectopic                       |
| **Slow-rising β-hCG**                  | Non-viable pregnancy (ectopic or miscarriage) |
