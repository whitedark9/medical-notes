# Imperforate Hymen

> Primary Amenorrhoea with Pelvic Mass and Cyclical Pain

## Causes of Primary Amenorrhoea

### A. With Normal Secondary Sexual Characteristics

| Cause                                     | Features                                                               |
| ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Imperforate hymen**                     | Bulging bluish hymen, cyclical pain, hematocolpos                      |
| **Transverse vaginal septum**             | Higher obstruction, may not show bulge                                 |
| **Müllerian agenesis (MRKH syndrome)**    | Absent uterus & upper vagina, normal breasts, normal karyotype (46,XX) |
| **Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome (AIS)** | XY karyotype, female phenotype, absent uterus, sparse pubic hair       |

### B. With Absent or Poor Secondary Sexual Characteristics

| Cause                                            | Features                                          |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Gonadal dysgenesis (e.g., Turner’s syndrome)** | 45,XO, short stature, streak ovaries, high FSH/LH |
| **Hypothalamic amenorrhoea**                     | Stress, weight loss, low GnRH → low FSH/LH        |
| **Kallmann syndrome**                            | Anosmia + hypogonadotropic hypogonadism           |
| **Pituitary failure/tumor**                      | Low gonadotropins, other pituitary signs          |

## Clinical Features

- **Primary amenorrhoea**
- **Cyclical abdominal pain**
- **Pelvic mass** (uterine enlargement)
- **Urinary retention or constipation** (mass effect)
- On exam:

  - **Bulging, bluish hymenal membrane**
  - **No visible vaginal opening**

## Investigations

### A. For Diagnosis

1. **Pelvic Ultrasound (Transabdominal)**

   - **Hematocolpos** (distended vagina with fluid level)
   - **Hematometra**
   - Uterus present and distended

2. **MRI Pelvis** (if anatomy unclear)

   - Confirms site and extent of obstruction
   - Differentiates imperforate hymen from transverse septum

### B. To Assess Other Causes (if unclear)

3. **Karyotyping**

   - If Müllerian agenesis or gonadal dysgenesis suspected

4. **Hormonal Profile**

   - FSH, LH, Estradiol → assess ovarian function
   - TSH, Prolactin → rule out pituitary/hypothyroid

5. **Examination Under Anaesthesia (EUA)**

   - For uncertain anatomy or surgical planning

## Management

### Diagnosis Recap

- **Primary amenorrhoea**
- **Cyclical lower abdominal pain**
- **14-week size pelvic mass**
  → Most likely: **Imperforate hymen** → → **Hematocolpos ± Hematometra**

### I. Goals of Management

1. **Drain retained menstrual blood** (relieve pressure, prevent complications)
2. **Restore normal menstrual flow**
3. **Preserve reproductive and sexual function**

### II. Step-by-Step Management

#### A. Preoperative Preparation

- **Admission** to gynecology unit
- **IV fluids** if dehydrated
- **Analgesia** for pain
- **Antibiotics** if signs of infection or prolonged retention
- **Pelvic ultrasound** to confirm extent (hematocolpos only vs hematometra/hematosalpinx)
- **Informed consent** and counseling (procedure, fertility, menstruation)

#### B. Definitive Surgical Management → Hymenotomy

##### 1. Procedure: Cruciate Incision

- Performed **under general anaesthesia**
- **Sterile technique**
- Make a **cruciate (X-shaped) incision** over the hymen
- Allow **gradual drainage** of collected blood (to prevent vasovagal shock or fallopian tube reflux)

##### 2. Drainage and Evacuation

- Gently press over lower abdomen to assist drainage
- Avoid forceful expression → prevent retrograde spill into tubes

##### 3. Hemostasis and Re-shaping

- Excise residual hymenal tags (to prevent re-closure)
- **Suture hymenal edges** to vaginal mucosa (marsupialization)
  → prevents future adhesions and stenosis

#### C. Postoperative Care

- Monitor for bleeding, infection
- Analgesia and hygiene instruction
- Vaginal dilation may be considered if there’s post-op narrowing
- **Reassure** about restoration of normal menses in next cycle

### III. Follow-Up

- Review after **first menses**
- Educate about menstrual hygiene and normal cycle
- Monitor for:

  - Recurrence of outflow obstruction
  - Signs of secondary infection

### IV. Complications if Untreated

- Endometriosis (from retrograde menstruation)
- Infertility
- Pyometra, pelvic adhesions
- Tubal damage → hydrosalpinx or hematosalpinx
- Renal tract compression → hydronephrosis (rare)

### V. Summary

| Step          | Action                                                       |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Initial**   | Admit, pain relief, imaging, antibiotics if needed           |
| **Surgery**   | Cruciate hymenotomy with controlled drainage                 |
| **Post-op**   | Hygiene, follow-up, monitor menstruation                     |
| **Long-term** | Normal menses expected, low recurrence with proper technique |
