# Endometriosis

## Definition

- Presence of **functional endometrial glands and stroma** **outside the uterine cavity**,
- Undergoes **cyclical bleeding** in response to ovarian hormones,
- Leads to **chronic inflammation, fibrosis, and adhesions**.

## Common Sites of Endometriosis

### I. Pelvic (Most Common)

1. **Ovaries** → most common site → may form **endometrioma ("chocolate cyst")**
2. **Pouch of Douglas**
3. **Uterosacral ligaments**
4. **Pelvic peritoneum**
5. **Fallopian tubes**
6. **Rectovaginal septum**

### II. Extra-pelvic

1. **Umbilicus**
2. **Abdominal scars** (esp. post-CS or laparotomy)
3. **Lungs and pleura** → catamenial pneumothorax
4. **Bladder or ureter**
5. **Bowel (rectosigmoid)**

## Symptoms

| Symptom                      | Description                                                                |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Dysmenorrhoea**            | Painful periods (progressive, starts before menses)                        |
| **Chronic pelvic pain**      | Constant or cyclical                                                       |
| **Dyspareunia**              | Deep pain during intercourse                                               |
| **Dyschezia**                | Painful defecation, especially during menses                               |
| **Infertility**              | Due to tubal distortion, adhesions                                         |
| **Menstrual irregularities** | Heavy bleeding, premenstrual spotting                                      |
| **Urinary / GI symptoms**    | If bladder/bowel involved (e.g., frequency, rectal bleeding during menses) |

## Diagnosis

### History

- **Cyclical pelvic pain**, dysmenorrhoea starting before periods
- **Deep dyspareunia**
- **Infertility**
- **Dyschezia** (painful defecation), especially during menstruation
- **Dysuria or hematuria** (if bladder involved)
- **Chronic fatigue**

> **Note:** Symptoms may be **cyclical** or **non-cyclical**, and **do not correlate** with disease severity.

### Physical Examination

| Finding                                                    | Interpretation                  |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **Fixed retroverted uterus**                               | Due to adhesions                |
| **Tender uterosacral ligaments**                           | Common site of deposits         |
| **Adnexal tenderness or mass**                             | Endometrioma                    |
| **Tender nodules** in Pouch of Douglas or posterior fornix | Deep infiltrating endometriosis |

### Investigations

#### 1. Transvaginal Ultrasound (TVUS)

- Detects **endometriomas** ("chocolate cysts")
- Features: **ground-glass appearance**, homogeneous low-level echoes
- May detect deeply infiltrating lesions

#### 2. MRI Pelvis _(if deep pelvic endometriosis suspected)_

- Superior for mapping:

  - Rectovaginal septum involvement
  - Uterosacral ligaments
  - Bowel/bladder infiltration

#### 3. Diagnostic Laparoscopy (Gold Standard)

- Direct **visualization of lesions**:

  - Powder-burn spots
  - Red, blue, white opacified lesions
  - Adhesions or endometriomas

- Allows **histological confirmation** by biopsy

> **Indications for laparoscopy:**

- Unclear diagnosis
- Suspected endometriosis in infertility or chronic pelvic pain
- Failure of empirical medical treatment

### Supportive Tests (if indicated)

- **CA-125:** May be elevated, especially in advanced disease
  → **Not diagnostic**, but helpful for **monitoring** in treated cases
- **CBC, ESR/CRP**: Nonspecific, rule out infection or malignancy

## Management

### Goals of Management

- Relieve pain
- Improve fertility (if desired)
- Suppress progression
- Improve quality of life

### Stepwise Management Plan

#### 1. Expectant Management

- For mild/asymptomatic disease
- Reassure + observe

#### 2. Medical Management (Hormonal suppression)

| Drug                                                                       | Mechanism                                   | Use                                 | Side Effects                                |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **COCPs**                                                                  | Inhibit ovulation, endometrial thinning     | 1st-line in mild-moderate symptoms  | Nausea, breast tenderness                   |
| **Progestins** (e.g. `medroxyprogesterone`, `norethisterone`, `dienogest`) | Endometrial atrophy                         | Moderate-severe pain                | Breakthrough bleeding                       |
| **GnRH agonists** (e.g. `leuprolide`, `goserelin`)                         | ↓ LH/FSH → hypoestrogenic state             | Severe pain, before IVF/surgery     | Menopause-like symptoms (hot flashes, ↓BMD) |
| **GnRH antagonists** (e.g. `elagolix`)                                     | Similar to agonists, faster onset           | Alternative to agonists             | Similar SEs                                 |
| **Mirena (LNG-IUS)**                                                       | Local progestogen → endometrial suppression | Long-term control of pain, bleeding | Irregular bleeding                          |
| **Danazol** (rarely used now)                                              | Androgenic → suppresses pituitary           | Severe cases                        | Weight gain, acne, hirsutism                |

> Consider **add-back therapy** (e.g. `estrogen` + `progestin`) with GnRH analogues to prevent osteoporosis & SEs

#### 3. Surgical Management

##### A. Conservative (Fertility-preserving)

**Indications**: Pain not controlled medically, Infertility with endometriosis

- **Laparoscopic excision/ablation** of endometriotic lesions
- **Adhesiolysis** for tubo-ovarian adhesions
- **Cystectomy** for endometriomas (not drainage)

> Improves both pain and fertility

##### B. Definitive Surgery

**Indications**: Severe disease, completed family, failed other treatments

- **Total abdominal/laparoscopic hysterectomy + BSO (bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy)**
- +/- excision of deep lesions

#### 4. Fertility Management

- Mild: Try natural conception after surgery
- Moderate-severe or failed: Refer to IVF

  - **IVF is preferred** if tubal damage, ovarian reserve ↓, or age >35

### Complications of Untreated Endometriosis

- Chronic pelvic pain
- Infertility
- Adhesions → bowel/urinary symptoms
- Ovarian endometrioma → rupture, torsion
- Rarely malignant transformation (clear cell/ endometrioid carcinoma)

### Follow-Up

- Regular symptom review
- Monitor bone density if on long-term GnRH agonists
- Reassess fertility plans
