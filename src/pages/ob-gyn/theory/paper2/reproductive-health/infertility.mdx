# Infertility & Subfertility

> Infertility: Failure to conceive after **12 months of regular unprotected intercourse**.

> Subfertility: Reduced ability to conceive but **not absolute infertility**

## Types

1. **Primary subfertility**

   - No prior pregnancies

2. **Secondary subfertility**

   - Difficulty conceiving **after previous conception**, regardless of outcome

## History Taking in Infertility Evaluation

### Female Partner

- **Menstrual history**: regularity, duration, dysmenorrhea (→ ovulation)
- **Sexual history**: coital frequency, dyspareunia, lubrication
- **Past obstetric history**
- **Contraceptive history**
- **Medical history**: thyroid disease, diabetes, TB
- **Surgical history**: pelvic surgeries, appendectomy
- **Drug history**: antipsychotics, chemotherapy
- **Lifestyle**: smoking, alcohol, BMI, exercise
- **Family history**: early menopause, PCOS

### Male Partner

- **Past urological history**: mumps orchitis, trauma, surgery (hernia repair)
- **Sexual function**: erectile/ejaculatory issues
- **Occupational exposure**: heat, chemicals
- **Lifestyle**: smoking, alcohol, drugs, tight clothing
- **Systemic illness**: diabetes, varicocele

## Physical Examination

### Female:

- **BMI**: obesity or underweight
- **Hirsutism, acne** → PCOS
- **Thyroid, galactorrhoea** → prolactinoma
- **Abdominal scars or masses**
- **Pelvic exam**: uterus size, adnexal masses, cervical stenosis

### Male:

- **Secondary sexual characteristics**
- **Testicular volume** (normal: 15–20 mL)
- **Varicocele** (bag of worms)
- **Vas deferens palpation**

## Investigations

### Female Partner

#### For Ovulation Assessment

| **Test**                   | **Time**            | **Finding**                                   |
| -------------------------- | ------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Serum progesterone**     | Day 21 (mid-luteal) | >30 nmol/L confirms ovulation                 |
| **Basal body temperature** | Daily               | ↑ by ≥0.5°C after ovulation                   |
| **Urinary LH kits**        | Daily mid-cycle     | LH surge before ovulation                     |
| **TVS folliculometry**     | Day 10 onward       | Follicle grows 2 mm/day; ruptures at 18–24 mm |

#### Hormonal Tests

- **Day 2–5 FSH/LH** → ↑ FSH suggests ↓ ovarian reserve
- **Serum AMH** (>1.0 ng/mL normal) → Ovarian reserve
- **TSH, Prolactin**
- **Androgens (if PCOS suspected)**

#### Tubal Assessment

- **HSG (hysterosalpingography)** – day 7–10 of cycle

  - Spillage of dye = patent tubes

- **Sonohysterography**
- **Laparoscopy with chromopertubation** _(Gold standard)_

#### Uterine Assessment

- **Transvaginal ultrasound**: polyps, fibroids, anomalies
- **Hysteroscopy**: intracavitary lesions

---

### Male Partner

#### Semen Analysis

- Collected after **2–5 days abstinence**; evaluated within 1 hour.

| **WHO 2021 Normal Values** | **Normal Range** |
| -------------------------- | ---------------- |
| **Volume**                 | ≥1.4 mL          |
| **Sperm count**            | ≥16 million/mL   |
| **Total motility**         | ≥42%             |
| **Progressive motility**   | ≥30%             |
| **Morphology**             | ≥4% normal forms |
| **Vitality**               | ≥54%             |

> If abnormal → repeat in 3–4 weeks (2 samples)

#### Hormonal Profile

- FSH, LH, testosterone
- Prolactin (if low libido)
- TSH (thyroid disorder)

#### Other Tests

- **Scrotal ultrasound**: varicocele
- **Karyotyping**: Klinefelter, Y-chromosome microdeletions
- **Anti-sperm antibodies** (rare)

## Infertility Management for PCOS & Abnormal Sperm Count

### 1. PCOS-Related Infertility

#### General Principles

- PCOS → chronic anovulation → infertility
- Aim: **induce ovulation**
- Also manage **insulin resistance, obesity**, and **metabolic risks**

#### Stepwise Management

##### A. Lifestyle Modification (First-line)

- **Weight loss** (even 5–10%) → restores ovulation in 60%
- **Exercise** + **diet** (low-carb, high-fiber)

##### B. Ovulation Induction

| **Drug**                   | **Dose & Route**                 | **Mechanism**                     | **Notes**                                         |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Letrozole** _(1st line)_ | 2.5–5 mg/day on Day 2–6          | Aromatase inhibitor → ↑ FSH       | Higher ovulation + pregnancy rate than clomiphene |
| **Clomiphene Citrate**     | 50–100 mg/day on Day 2–6         | Estrogen receptor blocker → ↑ FSH | Risk: anti-estrogenic effect on endometrium       |
| **Metformin**              | 500–1500 mg/day                  | ↓ Insulin resistance              | Use if BMI >30 or insulin resistant               |
| **Gonadotropins**          | hMG or FSH injections            | Direct ovarian stimulation        | Risk: OHSS, multiple pregnancy                    |
| **hCG injection**          | Single dose when follicle >18 mm | Induces ovulation                 | Given after stimulation                           |
| **GnRH analogs**           | Suppress LH in resistant cases   |                                   | Used in IVF cycles                                |

##### C. Laparoscopic Ovarian Drilling

- For **clomiphene-resistant** PCOS
- Puncture multiple follicles with electrocautery → ↓ androgens → restore ovulation

##### D. ART – IVF/ICSI

- Indicated if:

  - Failure of above
  - Coexisting tubal/male factor
  - Age >35 years or long-standing infertility

#### Complications of Ovulation Induction

- Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS)
- Multiple pregnancy
- Ectopic pregnancy

### 2. Male Factor Infertility (Abnormal Sperm Count)

#### Definitions

| Abnormality        | WHO 2021 Criteria          |
| ------------------ | -------------------------- |
| **Oligospermia**   | \<16 million/mL            |
| **Asthenospermia** | Progressive motility \<30% |
| **Teratospermia**  | Morphology \<4% normal     |
| **Azoospermia**    | No sperm in semen          |

#### Stepwise Management

##### A. General Measures

- Lifestyle: avoid smoking, alcohol, heat exposure
- Treat infections (e.g., chlamydia)
- Hormonal abnormalities: treat thyroid, prolactin, testosterone issues

##### B. Specific Treatments

| Condition                             | Management                                                       |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Varicocele**                        | Varicocelectomy if symptomatic + oligospermia                    |
| **Hypogonadism (low T, high FSH/LH)** | Testosterone replacement **(not for fertility)**                 |
| **Obstructive azoospermia**           | Surgical sperm retrieval (PESA, TESA)                            |
| **Retrograde ejaculation**            | Alpha-agonists (e.g., pseudoephedrine), IVF with retrieved sperm |

##### C. ART Options Based on Severity

| Sperm Status                          | ART Method                                  |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Mild–moderate oligospermia            | **IUI** (intrauterine insemination)         |
| Severe oligospermia, teratozoospermia | **IVF**                                     |
| Very severe / azoospermia             | **ICSI** (intracytoplasmic sperm injection) |
| No viable sperm                       | **Donor sperm insemination**                |

### Combined Infertility (PCOS + Male Factor)

- Consider **ART (IUI, IVF/ICSI)** early
- Simultaneous management of both partners
