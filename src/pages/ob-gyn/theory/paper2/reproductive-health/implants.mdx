# Contraceptive Implants

> Subdermal **long-acting reversible contraceptives (LARC)** containing **progestogen**, inserted under the skin to inhibit ovulation and prevent pregnancy.

## Types

| Name                | Drug                  | Duration  | Typical Form              |
| ------------------- | --------------------- | --------- | ------------------------- |
| `Implanon`          | Etonogestrel 68 mg    | 3 years   | Single rod                |
| `Nexplanon`         | Etonogestrel 68 mg    | 3 years   | Single rod (radio-opaque) |
| `Jadelle`           | Levonorgestrel 150 mg | 5 years   | Two rods                  |
| `Sino-implant (II)` | Levonorgestrel 150 mg | 3–5 years | Two rods                  |

## Mechanism of Action

- **Inhibits ovulation** (suppresses LH surge)
- **Thickens cervical mucus** → impairs sperm penetration
- **Endometrial atrophy** → impairs implantation

## Indications

- Reliable long-term contraception
- Women who:

  - Want spacing
  - Cannot tolerate estrogen (e.g., breastfeeding)
  - Have contraindications to COC

- Good for adolescents, perimenopausal, or mentally challenged women

## Contraindications

### Absolute

- **Current breast cancer**
- **Unexplained vaginal bleeding**
- **Severe liver disease/tumor**
- **Known hypersensitivity** to progestogen

### Relative

- History of **thromboembolism**
- **Severe cardiovascular disease**
- **Diabetes with complications**

## Insertion and Use

- Inserted in **non-dominant upper arm** under local anaesthesia
- Best time: **within first 5 days of menstruation** (no backup needed)
- If inserted at any other time → **use backup contraception for 7 days**
- Effective **within 24 hours** of insertion (if proper timing)

## Non-Contraceptive Benefits

- Reduces:

  - Dysmenorrhea
  - Menstrual blood loss
  - Risk of endometrial cancer

- May help in:

  - Endometriosis
  - Fibroid-related menorrhagia

## Side Effects

- **Irregular bleeding** (most common): spotting, amenorrhea
- Weight gain
- Headache, mood changes
- Acne
- Breast tenderness

## Follow-Up

- **First review**: 3 months after insertion
- Then **annual check-up**
- Remove/replace before expiration
- Pregnancy test if amenorrheic before re-insertion

## Removal

- Simple **office procedure** under local anaesthesia
- Fertility usually returns within **7–14 days**

## Advantages

- Long-term (3–5 years)
- High efficacy (>99%)
- Rapid return of fertility
- Estrogen-free (suitable in breastfeeding and estrogen contraindications)
- Minimal user action needed

## Disadvantages

- Irregular or unpredictable bleeding
- Invasive insertion/removal (minor)
- Requires trained personnel

## Comparison with Other Contraceptives

| Feature          | Implant         | Copper IUD | LNG-IUS      |
| ---------------- | --------------- | ---------- | ------------ |
| Hormonal         | Yes             | No         | Yes          |
| Duration         | 3–5 years       | 5–10 years | 5–7 years    |
| Effect on Menses | Irregular/less  | Heavier    | Lighter/none |
| Insertion Site   | Subdermal (arm) | Uterine    | Uterine      |
