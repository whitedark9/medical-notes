# Multiple Pregnancy

> A multiple pregnancy is the **simultaneous development of more than one fetus** in the uterus.
> → Most commonly **twins**, but may be **triplets or higher-order multiples**.

## Classification

### A. By Zygosity (Genetic Origin)

1. **Monozygotic (Uniovular) Twins**

   - One ovum fertilized by one sperm → divides into two embryos
   - Genetically identical (same sex, same blood group)

2. **Dizygotic (Biovular) Twins**

   - Two separate ova fertilized by two sperms
   - Genetically distinct (can be same or different sex)

### B. By Chorionicity and Amnionicity (Important for Obstetric Management)

| Type                           | Description                  | Timing of Division                               |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Dichorionic diamniotic**     | 2 placentas, 2 amniotic sacs | All dizygotic & monozygotic division **≤3 days** |
| **Monochorionic diamniotic**   | 1 placenta, 2 sacs           | Monozygotic division **4–8 days**                |
| **Monochorionic monoamniotic** | 1 placenta, 1 sac            | Monozygotic division **8–13 days**               |
| **Conjoined twins**            | Incomplete division          | **>13 days**                                     |

## Risk Factors

### Maternal Factors

1. ↑ Maternal age (esp. >35 years)
2. Multiparity
3. Black race > White > Asian
4. Family history (maternal side)

### Assisted Reproduction

1. Ovulation induction (e.g. clomiphene, letrozole)
2. IVF / ART techniques

### Others

1. Stopping oral contraceptives
2. Taller or overweight women

## Differences: Uniovular vs Biovular Twins

| Feature              | Uniovular (Monozygotic)          | Biovular (Dizygotic)                |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Origin               | 1 ovum, 1 sperm → splits         | 2 ova, 2 sperms                     |
| Genetic              | Identical (same sex, same genes) | Non-identical (same/different sex)  |
| Frequency            | Constant (3–5/1000)              | Variable, influenced by race/family |
| Chorionicity         | Mostly monochorionic             | Always dichorionic                  |
| Sex                  | Always same                      | May be same or different            |
| Placenta             | Usually shared                   | Separate                            |
| Congenital anomalies | ↑ (e.g., TTTS, conjoined)        | Not increased                       |
| Complications        | More severe                      | Less compared to monozygotic        |

## Diagnosis

### A. Clinical Diagnosis

1. **Exaggerated symptoms**: Hyperemesis, anemia, weight gain
2. **Fundal height > gestational age**
3. **Palpation**:

   - More than 3 poles
   - Small parts in multiple locations

4. **Auscultation**: 2 separate fetal heart tones, ≥10 bpm apart

### B. Ultrasonography (Definitive)

- **Confirm number of fetuses**
- **Determine chorionicity and amnionicity** by:

  - **Lambda (twin peak) sign** → Dichorionic
  - **T-sign** → Monochorionic

- Assess fetal growth, anomalies, fluid levels

### C. Other Investigations

- ↑ Maternal serum AFP
- Hemoglobin (anemia), OGTT (GDM), BP (preeclampsia)

## Complications

### A. Maternal Complications

1. Early pregnancy
   - Worse minor ailments
   - Pressure symptoms (urinary tract)
   - Bleeding risk (Miscarriage)
1. Late Pregnancy
   - Polyhydramnios
   - GDM, PE
   - PPROM, PROM
   - Preterm
1. Intrapartum
   - PPH
   - Assisted delivery, CS
1. Puperium
   - Breastfeeding problems

| System         | Complication                                                         |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hematological  | Anemia, ↑ thromboembolism                                            |
| Cardiovascular | ↑ risk of pre-eclampsia, hypertension                                |
| Metabolic      | GDM, hyperemesis gravidarum                                          |
| Mechanical     | Polyhydramnios, overdistension, PROM                                 |
| Obstetric      | Malpresentation, APH (placenta previa/abruption), PPH, preterm labor |
| Others         | Operative delivery, psychological stress                             |

### B. Fetal Complications

| Type                         | Complication                                                                                                                              |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Perinatal**                | Preterm birth (major cause of morbidity/mortality)                                                                                        |
| **Growth**                   | IUGR, discordant growth                                                                                                                   |
| **Congenital**               | ↑ anomalies in monozygotic                                                                                                                |
| **Cord**                     | Cord entanglement (monoamniotic)                                                                                                          |
| **Death**                    | Single IUFD → risk of co-twin demise, brain injury                                                                                        |
| **Twin-Specific Syndromes**: | - **TTTS** (Twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome)<br/>- **TAPS** (Twin anemia polycythemia sequence)<br/>- **TRAP** (Acardiac twin syndrome) |

# Management

## A. Antenatal Care

### 1. Diagnosis & Initial Assessment

- Confirm multiple pregnancy by **early USG**
- Determine **chorionicity and amnionicity**:

  - **First trimester scan**: most accurate
  - Use **lambda sign (dichorionic)** / **T sign (monochorionic)**

- Base further management on chorionicity:

  - **Monochorionic twins** = higher risk → more intense monitoring

### 2. Maternal Monitoring

- More frequent visits (every 2–3 weeks after 24 weeks)
- Check:

  - Blood pressure (↑ risk of preeclampsia)
  - Hemoglobin (↑ anemia risk)
  - Blood sugar (GDM screening)

### 3. Fetal Monitoring

- **Growth scans**:

  - Dichorionic → every 4 weeks from 24 weeks
  - Monochorionic → every 2 weeks from 16 weeks (due to TTTS risk)

- Monitor:

  - **Growth discordance** (>20% = significant)
  - **Liquor volume** (TTTS suspicion if discrepancy)

### 4. Other Care

- **Nutritional advice**: Higher calorie and iron demand
- **Iron, folate, calcium** supplementation
- **Antenatal corticosteroids**:

  - If risk of preterm labor \<34 weeks
  - Betamethasone 12 mg IM × 2 doses, 24 hrs apart

### 5. Hospitalization

- Consider from **28–32 weeks** if complications arise:

  - Preterm labor
  - Growth discordance
  - TTTS
  - Hypertension

## B. Timing & Mode of Delivery

### 1. General Timing Recommendations (Ten Teachers OG)

| Chorionicity                 | Timing of Delivery             |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| **Monoamniotic**             | 32–34 weeks by **elective CS** |
| **Triplets or higher**       | 34–35 weeks (elective CS)      |
| **Monochorionic diamniotic** | 36–37 weeks                    |
| **Dichorionic diamniotic**   | 37–38 weeks                    |

## 2. Mode of Delivery

### Indications for Elective Caesarean Section:

- **First twin non-cephalic**
- **Monoamniotic twins**
- **Monochorionic twins with TTTS**
- **Weight discordance \>25%**
- **Fetal distress in either twin**
- **Previous uterine scar**
- **Higher-order multiples**

### Allow Vaginal Delivery If:

- **First twin is cephalic**
- Estimated fetal weight \>1.5 kg each
- No major complications
- Good labor progress

## C. Intrapartum Management

### First Stage of Labor

- Admit in **tertiary center with NICU and OT**
- Continuous **CTG monitoring** for both fetuses
- Crossmatch blood
- Prepare for **operative delivery**
- **IV access** and epidural analgesia encouraged

### Second Stage of Labor – Delivery of First Twin

- Deliver **as usual (cephalic presentation)**
- Active management of 3rd stage avoided until second twin delivered

## Delivery of the Second Twin – Stepwise Plan

| Step | Action                                                                                                                                            |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1.   | Immediately **palpate abdomen (examination) and do vaginal exam** to determine lie                                                                |
| 2.   | **Auscultate FHS**; if doubtful → **Ultrasound**                                                                                                  |
| 3.   | If **cephalic** → allow labor to continue or augment with `oxytocin` ± Artificial Rputure of Membrane (ARM)                                       |
| 4.   | If **breech** → assisted breech delivery if skilled                                                                                               |
| 5.   | If **transverse lie** → - Attempt **internal podalic version[^1]** + breech extraction (if trained)<br/>- If fails or not safe → **Emergency CS** |
| 6.   | Inter-delivery time ideally **\<30 minutes**<br/>- FHR monitored<br/>- Delay >30 min → reassess (consider CS)                                     |

## Special Situation: Twin with Transverse Lie

### Before Labor Onset

- **Elective CS** if first twin or second twin is transverse

### During Labor

- If second twin becomes transverse after first is delivered:

  - **Internal podalic version** (IPV) + breech extraction
  - If IPV fails → **Emergency CS**

- Only attempt if:

  - Experienced obstetrician
  - Adequate facilities
  - Adequate liquor and relaxed uterus

## Third Stage of Labor

- Active management after **both twins are delivered**
- **Oxytocin 10 IU IV bolus + 20 IU in drip**
- Be prepared for **PPH** (higher risk due to uterine overdistension)

## Postpartum Care

### Maternal

- Monitor for:

  - **PPH**
  - Anemia
  - Hypertension
  - Uterine involution

- Encourage **breastfeeding** both twins

### Neonatal

- Monitor for:

  - **Prematurity complications**
  - Growth restriction
  - TTTS (if monochorionic)
  - Jaundice

## Contraception After Multiple Pregnancy

### Best Methods:

- **LNG-IUD** (e.g., Mirena): Safe, effective, reversible
- **Injectables (DMPA)** or **Progestin-only pills**: Safe during breastfeeding
- **Barrier methods**: If spacing desired
- **Permanent method (tubal ligation)**: If family complete

### Avoid:

- **Combined OCPs** in early postpartum (↑ VTE risk)

## Summary of Key Points

| Step              | Key Action                                          |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **Antenatal**     | Early USG, assess chorionicity, frequent monitoring |
| **Timing**        | 36–38 weeks depending on type                       |
| **Delivery**      | Vaginal if first twin cephalic and no complications |
| **Second twin**   | Careful lie assessment + expedite delivery          |
| **Complications** | Prepare for PPH, prematurity, TTTS                  |
| **Postpartum**    | Iron, lactation support, contraception planning     |

---

[^1]: Internal podalic version (IPV): Better with intact amnniotic sac; Pull the fetus' feed through maternal vagina for breech presentation

[^1]: External cephalic version (ECV): Rotate the fetus into head presentation outside the abdomen
