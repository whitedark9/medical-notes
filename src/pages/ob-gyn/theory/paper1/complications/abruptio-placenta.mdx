# Abruptio Placentae (Placental Abruption)

> **Premature separation of a normally situated placenta** (i.e., in upper uterine segment) **after 24 weeks** and **before delivery of the fetus**.

## Types / Classification:

1. **Revealed (external) hemorrhage**

   - Blood escapes through cervix → **vaginal bleeding visible**

2. **Concealed hemorrhage**

   - Blood collects behind placenta → **no vaginal bleeding**

3. **Mixed type**

   - Some blood escapes, some remains concealed

## Grades / Severity (Clinical Grading):

| Grade                  | Features                                                                     |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Grade 0**            | Diagnosed retrospectively (e.g., after delivery, blood clot behind placenta) |
| **Grade 1 (Mild)**     | Slight vaginal bleeding, no fetal/maternal compromise                        |
| **Grade 2 (Moderate)** | Moderate bleeding, **fetal distress**, mother stable                         |
| **Grade 3 (Severe)**   | Heavy bleeding, **fetal death**, maternal shock, DIC                         |

## Etiology / Risk Factors:

1. Maternal hypertension (most common)
1. `Pre`-eclampsia / eclampsia
1. `Pre`vious abruption
1. `Pre`mature rupture of membranes
1. Polyhydramnios with sudden decom`pre`ssion
1. Trauma (e.g., RTA, domestic violence)
1. Thrombophilias
1. Smoking
1. Cocaine abuse
1. Multiparity

## Pathophysiology:

- Insult (e.g., hypertension, trauma) → **rupture of uteroplacental vessels**
  → **Bleeding into decidua basalis**
  → **Placental separation**
  → ↓ uteroplacental blood flow
  → **Fetal hypoxia or death**
  → **Retroplacental clot** forms → uterine irritability, DIC may follow

## Diagnosis

### History:

- **Painful vaginal bleeding**
- **Sudden onset of abdominal pain**
- Reduced fetal movements
- Symptoms of shock (in severe cases)

- Risk factors (HTN, trauma, smoking)

### Physical Examination:

- **Uterus tense, tender, rigid (woody hard)**
- Fundal height may increase (due to concealed blood)
- Fetal heart rate abnormal or **absent**
- **Couvelaire uterus** (blue, ecchymosed due to bleeding into myometrium)
- **Signs of DIC/Shock** in severe cases
- Shock may be **out of proportion to visible bleeding** (if concealed)

### Investigations

#### a. For Diagnosis:

- **Clinical diagnosis** (mainstay)
- **Ultrasound** → low sensitivity (\~25%); may show retroplacental clot

#### b. For Cause:

- **BP monitoring**, urine dipstick (pre-eclampsia)
- History of trauma, drug use

#### c. For Complications:

- **CBC** → anemia, thrombocytopenia
- **Coagulation profile** → PT, APTT, fibrinogen ↓ in DIC
- **Kleihauer test** → fetomaternal haemorrhage if Rh negative
- **CTG** → fetal distress or death

## Management

### I. Emergency Resuscitation

#### A – Airway

- Ensure patent airway
- Oxygen 10–15 L/min via face mask

#### B – Breathing

- Monitor respiratory rate and oxygen saturation
- Provide O₂ support

#### C – Circulation

- **2 large-bore IV cannulas**
- Blood samples: CBC, clotting profile, LFT, RFT, group & crossmatch (4–6 units)
- Start **IV fluids (crystalloids)** immediately
- **Blood transfusion** if Hb \<7 g/dL or evidence of shock
- Monitor vitals every 15 minutes

#### D – Disability

- Check consciousness, shock index, urine output
- Insert **urinary catheter** for strict I/O monitoring
- Maintain UO ≥ 30 mL/hr

#### E – Exposure

- Assess bleeding, abdomen, uterine tone, fetal heart sounds

#### F – Fetus

- Continuous CTG (if fetus alive)
- USG: Fetal status, retroplacental clot, AFI

---

### II. Definitive Management

#### 1. Fetus Alive & Gestation ≥37 weeks (Term)

> Immediate delivery!

- **Vaginal delivery** if:

  - Labor is established
  - No fetal/maternal distress
  - No coagulopathy

- **Caesarean section** if:

  - Fetal distress (non-reassuring CTG)
  - Major concealed abruption
  - No progress in labor
  - Previous CS scar or unstable maternal condition

#### 2. Fetus Alive & Gestation \<37 weeks (Preterm)

- **Mild abruption, no fetal/maternal distress**:

  - **Expectant management** in hospital
  - **Corticosteroids** for lung maturity (e.g., betamethasone 12 mg IM ×2 doses 24 hours apart)
  - Monitor vitals, fetal status daily

- **If any deterioration** → deliver immediately

#### 3. Fetal Death (IUD)

- **Vaginal delivery preferred** (unless contraindicated)
- **Stabilize mother first**
- Avoid CS unless:

  - Obstetric indication
  - DIC or uterine rupture

---

### III. Management of Complications

#### 1. Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC)

- Monitor: PT, aPTT, fibrinogen, platelets, D-dimer
- Replace:

  - Fresh frozen plasma (FFP)
  - Platelets
  - Cryoprecipitate (if fibrinogen \<1 g/L)

#### 2. Renal Failure

- Monitor urine output, urea, creatinine
- Maintain perfusion
- Dialysis if needed

#### 3. Postpartum Hemorrhage

- Active management of 3rd stage of labor
- Uterotonics: `oxytocin`, `ergometrine`, `misoprostol`
- Blood products ready
- Surgical intervention if needed: B-Lynch suture, vessel ligation, hysterectomy

---

### IV. Postpartum Management

#### Maternal

- Monitor for:

  - Bleeding
  - Infection
  - Anemia
  - Coagulopathy

- Iron/folate supplements
- Psychological support if IUD

#### Neonatal

- NICU care if premature
- Treat anemia, hypoxia, respiratory distress

### V. Contraception Advice

- Best: **Progestin-only method** (POP, DMPA, LNG-IUD) in postpartum period
- Avoid COCs immediately postpartum due to thrombotic risk
- Consider permanent contraception if family complete and severe complications occurred

---

### Drug Summary

| Drug                   | Indication                | Dose                            |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| `Betamethasone`        | Lung maturity if \<34 wks | 12 mg IM ×2 doses 24 hrs apart  |
| `Oxytocin`             | Labor induction/PPH       | 10 IU IV/IM; 20 IU infusion     |
| `Tranexamic Acid`      | Adjunct in PPH            | 1 g IV over 10 min              |
| `Ergometrine`          | Uterine atony             | 0.2 mg IM/slow IV               |
| `Misoprostol`          | PPH or uterine atony      | 800–1000 mcg rectal             |
| `FFP, Platelets, Cryo` | DIC management            | Based on labs and clinical need |
