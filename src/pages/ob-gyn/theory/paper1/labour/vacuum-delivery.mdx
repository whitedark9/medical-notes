# Vacuum-Assisted Vaginal Delivery (Ventouse Delivery)

## Definition

- **Operative vaginal delivery** using a suction cup (ventouse)
- Applied to the fetal scalp → traction applied during uterine contractions
- Used to assist **second stage of labor**

## Indications for Vacuum Delivery

### A. Fetal Indications

1. **Non-reassuring fetal heart rate (CTG abnormality)**
2. **Cord prolapse (if head low and imminent delivery)**
3. **Preterm delivery with fetal distress** (rare)

### B. Maternal Indications

1. **Prolonged second stage**

   - Primigravida: >2 hrs (without epidural), >3 hrs (with epidural)
   - Multipara: >1 hr (without epidural), >2 hrs (with epidural)

2. **Maternal exhaustion**
3. **Cardiac disease** (to avoid straining)
4. **Hypertensive crisis or intracranial pathology** (avoid pushing)

## Pre-requisites / Conditions to be Fulfilled

| Requirement                    | Details                                         |
| ------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Full cervical dilatation**   | 10 cm, no rim remaining                         |
| **Ruptured membranes**         | Amniotic sac must be absent                     |
| **Engaged head**               | Fetal head at station ≥ +2                      |
| **Known fetal position**       | To correctly apply the cup (flexion point)      |
| **Adequate pelvis**            | No suspicion of cephalopelvic disproportion     |
| **Empty bladder**              | Insert Foley catheter if needed                 |
| **Informed consent**           | Explain benefits and possible need for cesarean |
| **Skilled operator available** | With backup for cesarean if failed              |
| **Maternal effort present**    | Unless under general anesthesia (rare)          |

## Contraindications

### Fetal

- Gestational age \<34 weeks
- Suspected **fetal bleeding disorders** (e.g. hemophilia)
- **Face presentation** or malposition

### Maternal

- Incomplete cervical dilatation
- High head (not engaged)
- CPD or unknown position
- Operator not trained or no facilities for emergency CS

## Complications

### A. Maternal Complications

1. **Genital tract trauma** (vaginal, cervical, perineal tears)
2. **Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH)**
3. **Infection**
4. **Psychological trauma** (if delivery traumatic or prolonged)

### B. Fetal Complications

| Complication                                | Mechanism                                                |
| ------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cephalohematoma**                         | Blood between skull and periosteum (limited to one bone) |
| **Scalp laceration/abrasion**               | From suction cup                                         |
| **Subgaleal hemorrhage** (rare but serious) | Bleeding under scalp aponeurosis → shock                 |
| **Hyperbilirubinemia**                      | From hematoma resorption                                 |
| **Retinal hemorrhage**                      | Especially in preterm infants                            |
| **Failure of delivery**                     | May require emergency cesarean after failed attempt      |

## Procedure

### I. Preparation

#### 1. Confirm Indication & Prerequisites

Ensure:

- **Full cervical dilation** (10 cm)
- **Ruptured membranes**
- **Engaged head** (station ≥ +2)
- **Known fetal position**
- **No cephalopelvic disproportion**
- **Empty bladder** (catheterize if needed)
- **Informed consent** obtained

#### 2. Equipment Preparation

- Sterile **vacuum cup** (soft or rigid)
- **Vacuum machine** or manual pump
- **Suction tubing**
- Sterile gloves, lubricant
- **Resuscitation equipment for newborn**
- **Forceps and CS set** ready in case of failure

---

### II. Procedure Steps

#### 1. Position the Mother

- **Lithotomy position**
- Ensure good lighting and access

#### 2. Aseptic Technique

- Scrub, sterile gloves
- Clean vulva and perineum with antiseptic
- Drape the patient

#### 3. Identify the Fetal Position

- Use **vaginal examination** to locate:

  - **Posterior fontanelle**
  - **Sagittal suture**

→ Helps to identify **flexion point**

#### 4. Insert Vacuum Cup

- Choose appropriate cup size (usually 5–6 cm)
- Insert cup into vagina **with anterior edge facing pubic symphysis**
- Place **center of cup on flexion point**:
  → **3 cm anterior to posterior fontanelle**, over sagittal suture
  → Ensures proper flexion during traction

#### 5. Create the Vacuum

- Check cup seal and fetal scalp
- Remove air from the tubing
- **Stepwise suction:**

  1. Start with **100–150 mmHg** to confirm seal
  2. Then increase to **500–600 mmHg**

#### 6. Apply Traction (With Each Contraction)

- Wait for uterine contraction and maternal pushing
- Apply **downward traction initially**, then follow:
  → **Curve of Carus** (downward → horizontal → upward)
- Maintain **in-line axis traction** with fetal head
- Traction should be **intermittent**, **coordinated** with contractions

#### 7. Monitor Progress

- Descent should occur with **every pull**
- Delivery usually within **3 pulls or 15–20 minutes**
- Limit to **2 cup detachments ("pop-offs")**

#### 8. Delivery of Head

- Once head visible, release vacuum slowly
- Remove cup
- Deliver head and rest of body as in normal vaginal delivery

---

### III. Post-Delivery Care

#### Newborn

- Assess for:

  - **Cephalohematoma**
  - **Scalp abrasions**
  - **Jaundice** (later from hematoma resorption)
  - **Subgaleal hemorrhage** (swelling crossing suture lines)

- Neonatal resuscitation team must be present

#### Mother

- Examine **vaginal walls, cervix, and perineum** for trauma
- Manage **PPH** if occurs
- Provide **analgesia** and emotional support

---

### IV. Criteria for Abandoning Procedure

Stop and proceed to **cesarean section** if:

- No descent after **3 pulls**
- Delivery not achieved in **15–20 minutes**
- **≥2 pop-offs**
- **Fetal distress** develops
- **Cup misplacement** or bleeding occurs
