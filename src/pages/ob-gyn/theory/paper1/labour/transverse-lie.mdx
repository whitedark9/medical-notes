# Transverse Lie

> Fetal **long axis lies perpendicular** to maternal long axis<br/> Neither head nor breech presents at pelvic brim → **Shoulder**, **arm**, or **trunk** may present

## Causes

### A. Maternal Causes

1. **Grand multiparity**
   → Lax abdominal and uterine musculature → abnormal lie
2. **Uterine anomalies**
   → Bicornuate/septate uterus → prevents longitudinal alignment
3. **Pelvic contracture / pelvic tumors**
   → Prevents fetal engagement
4. **Placenta previa**
   → Lower segment occupied by placenta
5. **Polyhydramnios**
   → Excess fluid allows free fetal movement

### B. Fetal Causes

1. **Multiple pregnancy**
   → One fetus may lie transversely
2. **Prematurity**
   → Smaller size allows transverse position
3. **Fetal anomalies**
   → Anencephaly, hydrocephalus, or neuromuscular disorders
4. **Macrosomia**
   → Abnormal adaptation to pelvic inlet
5. **Intrauterine death**
   → Loss of fetal tone → abnormal lie

## Complications

### A. Maternal Complications

| Complication                    | Explanation                                           |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Obstructed labor**            | Shoulder cannot enter pelvis → mechanical obstruction |
| **Uterine rupture**             | With strong contractions and neglected labor          |
| **Infection**                   | Especially if prolonged PROM and labor                |
| **Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH)** | Atony or trauma during manipulation                   |
| **Operative delivery**          | ↑ Need for cesarean, classical incision if impacted   |

### B. Fetal Complications

| Complication                  | Explanation                                                               |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cord prolapse**             | Irregular presenting part doesn’t fill pelvis → cord slips through cervix |
| **Birth trauma**              | Manipulation in neglected transverse lie → arm injury, bruising           |
| **Hypoxia / IUFD**            | Cord compression or prolonged labor                                       |
| **Prematurity complications** | If preterm transverse lie leads to early CS                               |
| **Shoulder presentation**     | Arm may prolapse → dystocia, trauma                                       |
| **Congenital anomalies**      | Often associated with anomalies causing abnormal lie                      |

### C. During Labor (if not corrected)

- **Shoulder presentation** → no descent → **neglected transverse lie**
- **Hand prolapse** or **cord prolapse**
- **Uterine rupture** in multipara due to strong contractions

## Management of Transverse Lie

### I. Antenatal Management

#### A. Diagnosis

- Routine **clinical examination after 36 weeks**:

  - Fundal grip → empty lower pole
  - Fetal parts felt transversely
  - Auscultation → FHS heard far lateral

- **Ultrasound confirmation**:

  - Lie, presentation, fetal weight
  - Placental site, liquor volume, anomalies

---

#### B. At Term (≥37 Weeks)

##### 1. External Cephalic Version (ECV)

- **Best time**: 36–37 weeks in nullipara, 37–38 weeks in multipara

- **Prerequisites**:

  - Singleton pregnancy
  - Intact membranes
  - Normal CTG
  - No contraindications (e.g., placenta previa, oligohydramnios, uterine anomaly)

- **Technique**:

  - Tocolysis with `salbutamol` or `nifedipine`
  - Gentle abdominal manipulation to rotate fetus

- **Contraindications**:

  - Placenta previa
  - Abnormal CTG
  - Multiple gestation
  - Previous uterine scar (relative)

- **Success rate**: `50–60%`

##### 2. Elective Cesarean Section

- **Planned** at **39 weeks** if ECV fails or contraindicated

### II. Intrapartum Management

#### A. At Onset of Labor (No ECV done)

> Do not allow labor to progress with transverse lie!!!

- Admit for delivery at a **tertiary center**

##### If membranes intact and no labor:

- Attempt **ECV under tocolysis** (if no contraindication)
- If successful → allow vaginal delivery

##### If labor has started or membranes ruptured:

- **Cesarean Section** is the only safe option

---

### III. Delivery Management

#### A. Cesarean Section

##### Indications:

- Persistent transverse lie in labor
- Failed or contraindicated ECV
- Cord/hand prolapse
- Neglected transverse lie
- Fetal distress

##### Procedure:

- **Lower segment CS** if possible
- If deeply impacted shoulder:

  - **Classical CS** may be needed
  - Assistant may lift fetus from below per vaginum
  - Reverse breech extraction method may be used

#### B. Vaginal Delivery – Rare & Conditional

- `Internal Podalic Version + Breech Extraction`
- Indicated only for **second twin** if transverse
- Risks: Uterine rupture, cord prolapse, fetal trauma

---

### IV. Neglected Transverse Lie (Labor >12 hrs, shoulder impacted)

#### Management:

- **Stabilize patient**
- **Cesarean Section** → often classical if lower segment not accessible
- If fetal death:

  - **Destructive operation** (e.g., decapitation) if safe and skilled operator available

---

### V. Postpartum Care

#### Mother:

- Monitor for PPH
- Antibiotics if prolonged labor or rupture
- Counseling for future pregnancy (↑ risk of recurrence)

#### Neonate:

- Assess for:

  - Birth injury
  - Hypoxia
  - Prematurity complications (if early CS)

---

### VI. Future Pregnancy Advice

- Early ultrasound in next pregnancy to assess lie
- ECV planning at 36 weeks if transverse again
- Consider early elective CS if repeated transverse lie or uterine anomaly

---

### Summary Table

| Situation                         | Management                                   |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Before labor, ≥37 wks**         | ECV if no contraindication                   |
| **Failed ECV / contraindication** | Elective CS at 39 wks                        |
| **In labor (membranes ruptured)** | Emergency CS                                 |
| **Second twin transverse**        | Internal podalic version + breech extraction |
| **Neglected transverse lie**      | Emergency CS (classical if needed)           |
