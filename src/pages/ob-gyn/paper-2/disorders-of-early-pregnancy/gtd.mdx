# Gestational Trophoblastic Disease (GTD)

> Group of rare disorders arising from **abnormal proliferation of trophoblastic tissue** (which normally forms the placenta).

## Classification of GTD

### A. Benign Form

#### 1. Hydatidiform Mole (Molar Pregnancy)

> Abnormal proliferation and swelling of chorionic villi.

##### Subtypes:

1. **Complete Mole**

   - No fetus or embryonic tissue
   - All villi edematous
   - **46,XX** (paternal only, fertilization of empty ovum)
   - ↑↑ β-hCG
   - High risk of malignancy

1. **Partial Mole**
   - Fetus or fetal parts present
   - Some normal + some hydropic villi
   - **Triploid (69,XXY/XXX/XYY)**
   - Mild β-hCG elevation
   - Less risk of malignancy

### B. Malignant Forms (Gestational Trophoblastic Neoplasia – GTN)

> May arise after molar or non-molar pregnancy.

#### 2. Invasive Mole

- Molar tissue invades myometrium
- May cause uterine rupture or bleeding
- Locally invasive but **rarely metastasizes**

#### 3. Choriocarcinoma

- Highly malignant, **aggressive** tumor
- From any pregnancy (mole, abortion, ectopic, term)
- **Early hematogenous spread** (lungs, brain)
- ↑↑↑ β-hCG

#### 4. Placental Site Trophoblastic Tumor (PSTT)

- Arises from **intermediate trophoblast**
- Low β-hCG
- May occur **months–years** after pregnancy
- Resistant to chemotherapy

#### 5. Epithelioid Trophoblastic Tumor (ETT)

- Very rare, arises from chorionic-type intermediate trophoblast
- Similar to PSTT
- Intermediate malignancy

## Clinical Features

### 1. Complete Mole

- 1st trimester painless **vaginal bleeding**
- **Excessively large uterus** for gestational age
- **Hyperemesis gravidarum** (↑β-hCG)
- **Early-onset preeclampsia** (\<20 weeks)
- **No fetal heart sounds**
- **Theca lutein cysts** (bilateral ovarian enlargement)

### 2. Partial Mole

- Irregular or mild vaginal bleeding
- May have **fetal parts or cardiac activity**
- Uterus size = gestational age or small
- Less β-hCG-related symptoms

### 3. Invasive Mole

- Persistent vaginal bleeding **after evacuation of mole**
- Enlarging uterus, subinvolution
- May cause **uterine rupture** and hemoperitoneum

### 4. Choriocarcinoma

- Persistent/irregular vaginal bleeding after pregnancy (any type)
- **Severe symptoms due to metastasis**:

  - **Lungs**: cough, hemoptysis
  - **Brain**: headache, seizures
  - **Liver**: RUQ pain

- Very high β-hCG

### 5. PSTT

- **Mild/absent bleeding**
- Often presents **months–years** after pregnancy
- **Low or normal β-hCG**, despite large tumor mass

### 6. ETT

- Similar to PSTT
- Chronic irregular bleeding
- May mimic carcinoma

## Investigations

### A. Common to All GTD

| Investigation                | Purpose                                          |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Serum β-hCG**              | Elevated in all (except PSTT/ETT); monitor trend |
| **Pelvic Ultrasound (TVUS)** | First-line imaging                               |
| **CXR / CT / MRI**           | Evaluate metastasis (lungs, brain, liver)        |
| **Histopathology**           | Confirm type after evacuation or biopsy          |

## B. Key Differentiating Findings by Type

### Additional Tests if Needed

- **Doppler flow**: ↑ vascularity in GTN
- **MRI pelvis**: for myometrial invasion (invasive mole)
- **Liver function tests**: if metastasis suspected
- **Lumbar puncture**: if CNS symptoms with high β-hCG
