# Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

## Definition

- PID is an **infection and inflammation** of the **upper female genital tract**, including:

  - **Endometrium (endometritis)**
  - **Fallopian tubes (salpingitis)**
  - **Ovaries (oophoritis)**
  - **Pelvic peritoneum (peritonitis)**
  - Can also form **tubo-ovarian abscess**

- Most often caused by **ascending infection** from the lower genital tract

## Causes / Risk Factors

### 1. Infectious Organisms

| Type                              | Examples                                                                              |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sexually transmitted (common)** | _Chlamydia trachomatis_, _Neisseria gonorrhoeae_                                      |
| **Anaerobes / Vaginal flora**     | _Gardnerella vaginalis_, _Mycoplasma genitalium_, _Bacteroides_, _Peptostreptococcus_ |
| **Polymicrobial**                 | Common in post-abortal or postpartum PID                                              |

### 2. Risk Factors

- Multiple sexual partners
- Age \<25 years, sexually active
- Prior STI or PID
- Recent uterine instrumentation (e.g., IUD insertion, curettage)
- Post-abortion or postpartum infection
- Lack of barrier contraception

## Clinical Features

### General Symptoms

- **Lower abdominal and pelvic pain** (most common)
- **Fever**, chills
- **Abnormal vaginal discharge** (mucopurulent, foul-smelling)
- **Dyspareunia** (painful intercourse)
- **Menstrual irregularities**: post-coital bleeding, menorrhagia
- **Nausea, vomiting**, malaise

### Examination Findings

- Lower abdominal tenderness ± guarding
- **Cervical motion tenderness**
- **Adnexal tenderness** (bilateral)
- Uterine tenderness
- **Mucopurulent cervical discharge**

## Investigations

### A. For Diagnosis

| Test                                  | Findings                                                          |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **High vaginal & endocervical swabs** | Positive for _N. gonorrhoeae_, _C. trachomatis_, anaerobes        |
| **Pregnancy test (UPT)**              | To **exclude ectopic** pregnancy                                  |
| **Pelvic USG (TVS)**                  | May show tubo-ovarian abscess, free pelvic fluid, thickened tubes |
| **CBC**                               | ↑ WBC count, neutrophilia                                         |
| **CRP/ESR**                           | ↑ inflammatory markers                                            |

### B. For Complications

- **TVS with Doppler** → tubo-ovarian abscess
- **LFTs** → perihepatitis (Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome)
- **Blood cultures** if febrile
- **HIV, VDRL, HBsAg, HCV** → STI screening

## Management

### I. Outpatient (Mild to Moderate PID)

> Start **empirical treatment immediately** if PID is suspected
> **Oral Regimen:**

- `Ceftriaxone` 500 mg IM single dose
- `Doxycycline` 100 mg PO BD for 14 days
- `Metronidazole` 400 mg PO BD for 14 days

### II. Inpatient (Severe PID or complications)

**Admit if:**

- Severe pain or guarding
- High fever, vomiting
- Suspected **tubo-ovarian abscess**
- Pregnancy
- No improvement with oral treatment

**IV Regimen:**

- `Ceftriaxone` 1–2 g IV OD
- `Doxycycline` 100 mg IV/PO BD
- `Metronidazole` 500 mg IV/PO BD
  → Continue for 14 days\*\* total

> If tubo-ovarian abscess: consider **surgical drainage (laparoscopy/laparotomy)**

### III. Supportive Measures

- Analgesics: paracetamol, NSAIDs
- IV fluids if dehydrated
- Bed rest
- STI counseling
- Treat sexual partners (empiric antibiotics)
- Avoid intercourse until both partners treated

## Complications

| Type                                 | Description                            |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| **Chronic pelvic pain**              | Adhesions, ongoing inflammation        |
| **Tubal infertility**                | Due to tubal scarring                  |
| **Ectopic pregnancy**                | Risk ↑ ×6–10 due to damaged tubes      |
| **Tubo-ovarian abscess**             | May rupture → peritonitis              |
| **Perihepatitis (Fitz-Hugh-Curtis)** | RUQ pain, adhesions on liver capsule   |
| **Recurrent PID**                    | Each episode ↑ long-term risk          |
| **Sepsis**                           | Rare but life-threatening if untreated |

## Prevention

- Safer sex (condom use)
- Regular STI screening
- Prompt treatment of STIs
- Careful asepsis during procedures (IUD, curettage)
- Partner notification and treatment
