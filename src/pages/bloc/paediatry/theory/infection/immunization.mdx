## Diseases Prevented by Immunization in Myanmar

| Vaccine      | Preventable Diseases                                                                             |
| :----------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| BCG          | Tuberculosis (particularly severe forms like miliary TB and TB meningitis)                       |
| HBV          | Hepatitis B virus and Liver diseases (COL, HCC)                                                  |
| Rota         | Rota virus (severe gastroenteritis and diarrhoea)                                                |
| Polio        | Poliomyelitis (paralytic disease)                                                                |
| PCV          | Infections caused by _Streptococcus pneumoniae_ such as pneumonia, meningitis, and sepsis        |
| Penta-valent | Diphtheria, Pertussis (Whooping Cough), Tetanus, Hepatitis B, Haemophilus influenzae type b      |
| JE           | Japanese Encephalitis                                                                            |
| MR           | Measles, Rubella                                                                                 |
| HPV          | Cervical cancer, Genital warts, Other cancers (vaginal, vulvar, anal, and oropharyngeal cancers) |

## Current EPI schedule in Myanmar

### 1. BCG (Bacillus Calmette-Gu√©rin) Vaccine

- **Timing**:
  - At birth or 2 months
  - If missed, it can be given up to 5 years of age.
- **Type of Vaccine**: Live attenuated.
- **Preventable Disease**: Tuberculosis (particularly severe forms like miliary TB and TB meningitis).
- **Contraindications**:
  - Severe immunodeficiency (HIV, immunosuppressive therapy).
  - Acute illness or high fever.

### 2. Hepatitis B Vaccine

- **Timing**:
  - At birth within 24 hours.
  - Followed by Penta-valent vaccine at 2, 4, 6 months of age
- **Type of Vaccine**: Recombinant (inactivated).
- **Preventable Disease**: Hepatitis B virus infection
- **Contraindications**:
  - Severe allergic reaction to previous doses.

### 3. Rotavirus Vaccine

- **Timing**:
  - First dose - 2 months (Rota-1)
  - Second dose - 4 months (Rota-2)
- **Type of Vaccine**: Live attenuated oral.
- **Preventable Disease**: Rotavirus infection
- **Contraindications**:
  - Severe immunodeficiency.
  - History of intussusception.
  - Severe allergic reaction to a previous dose.

### 4. Polio Vaccine (OPV/IPV)

- **Timing**:
  - First dose - 2 months (OPV-1)
  - Second dose - 4 months (OPV-2 + IPV)
  - Third dose - 6 months (OPV-3)
- **Type of Vaccine**:
  - OPV: Live attenuated oral.
  - IPV: Inactivated.
- **Preventable Disease**: Poliomyelitis (paralytic disease).
- **Contraindications**:
  - Severe immunodeficiency (for OPV).
  - Moderate or severe illness with or without fever.

### 5. Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV)

- **Timing**:
  - First dose - 2 months (PCV-1)
  - Second dose - 4 months (PCV-2)
  - Third dose - 6 months (PCV-3)
  - Booster dose at 9-12 months.
- **Type of Vaccine**: Conjugate (inactivated).
- **Preventable Disease**: Infections caused by _Streptococcus pneumoniae_ such as pneumonia, meningitis, and sepsis.
- **Contraindications**:
  - Severe allergic reaction to previous doses.
  - Moderate to severe acute illness.

### 6. Penta-valent Vaccine

- **Timing**:
  - First dose - 2 months (Penta-1)
  - Second dose - 4 months (Penta-2)
  - Third dose - 6 months (Penta-3)
  - Fourth dose - 18 months (Penta-4)
  - If missed, it can be administered up to 1 year of age.
- **Type of Vaccine**:
  - Diphtheria and Tetanus: Toxoid.
  - Pertussis: Killed whole-cell or acellular.
  - Hepatitis B: Recombinant (inactivated).
  - Hib: Conjugate (inactivated).
- **Preventable Diseases**:
  - Diphtheria
  - Pertussis (Whooping Cough)
  - Tetanus
  - Hepatitis B
  - Haemophilus influenzae type b
- **Contraindications**
  - Severe allergic reaction to any previous dose.
    - Encephalopathy not attributable to another identifiable cause within 7 days of previous Pertussis vaccination.
    - Moderate or severe acute illness.

### 7. Japanese Encephalitis (JE) Vaccine

- **Timing**:
  - At 9 months
  - (Second dose - 12 months)
- **Type of Vaccine**: Inactivated.
- **Preventable Disease**: Japanese encephalitis (a mosquito-borne viral infection affecting the brain).
- **Contraindications**:
  - Severe allergic reaction to the vaccine.
  - Moderate or severe febrile illness.

### 8. Measles and Rubella (MR) Vaccine

- **Timing**:
  - First dose - 9 months (MR-1)
  - Second dose - 18 months (MR-2)
  - If missed, it can be given at any point before 5 years of age.
- **Type of Vaccine**: Live attenuated.
- **Preventable Diseases**:
  - Measles.
  - Rubella (Congenital Rubella Syndrome in pregnancy).
- **Contraindications**:
  - Pregnancy (for Rubella).
  - Severe immunodeficiency.
  - Moderate or severe febrile illness.

### 9. Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Vaccine

- **Timing**
  - Recommended for girls starting at **9-14 years** of age (usually before they become sexually active).
  - **Two-dose schedule**:
    - First dose - 9 years
    - Second dose - 10 years
    - (First dose at any time. Second dose 6-12 months after the first dose.)
  - (If the vaccine series is started at age 15 or older, a **three-dose schedule** is recommended (0, 1-2, and 6 months))
- **Type of Vaccine**
  - Subunit vaccine (recombinant, containing virus-like particles).
  - Common vaccines: **HPV-2**, **HPV-4**, and **HPV-9** (protect against 2, 4, or 9 strains, respectively).
- **Preventable Diseases**
  - **Cervical cancer**: Caused by HPV types 16 and 18 (which account for 70% of cases).
  - **Other cancers**: Such as vaginal, vulvar, anal, and oropharyngeal cancers.
  - **Genital warts**: Caused by HPV types 6 and 11 (covered by HPV-4 and HPV-9 vaccines).
- **Contraindications**
  - Severe allergic reaction to any previous dose or vaccine components.
  - Pregnancy
  - Moderate or severe illness
