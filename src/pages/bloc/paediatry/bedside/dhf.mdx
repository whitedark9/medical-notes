# Dengue Haemorrhagic Fever (DHF)

> \_\_\_ year old child presented with \_\_\_ days of fever and positive Hess's test. Take history and give the diagnosis.

## History

import { Steps } from "nextra/components";

<Steps>

### Introduction

- Introduce yourself
- Explain and get permission

### History of Present Illness

1. `Fever`
   - Onset (_sudden, gradual_)
   - Duration
   - Pattern (_continuous, intermittent, remittent_)
   - Temperature (_low grade, high grade_)
   - Associated symptoms:
     - chills & rigor
     - evening rise in temperature
     - sweating
     - skin rash (_bleeding manifestation_)
   - Medication (_sponge taping, paracetamol_)
1. `Bleeding Manifestation` (_DHF grade II_)
   - Nose bleeding
   - Gum bleeding
   - Vomiting (_coffee ground color_)
   - Black tarry stool (_malena_)
   - Rashes
1. `Warning Signs` (_dengue with warning signs_)
   - Abdominal pain
   - Features of shock (_DHF grade III_)
     - Restless or loss of interest to the surrounding or sleepy
     - Sudden fall of temperature
     - Cold & clammpy extremities
     - Oliguria
1. `Signs of Encephalopathy` (_severe dangue_)
   - Headache
   - Projectile vomiting
   - Convulsion
   - Irritability, restlessness
   - Imparied conscious state

### Past Medical History

1. `Similar Attack`
   - If present, grade of severity, any hospitalization, treatment
1. `History of hospitalization`

### Family & Social History

1. `Similar attack` in neighbour, family membrane, classmate

### Environmental History

1. Water collection places after rain
1. Type of water
1. Drainage system
1. Mosquito net, repellents

### Medications

1. `IV therapy` or ORS before admission
1. `NSAID` (_aspirin_)
1. `Indigenous medicine`

</Steps>

## Physical Examination

<Steps>

### Introduction [#PE]

- Introduce yourself
- Explain and get permission

### General Inspection

- Well or ill
- Alert or drowsy
- Interest to the surrounding (_sign of shock_)
- Nutritional status

### Head

- Febrile or afebrile
- Epistaxis (_bleeding manifestation_)
- Gum bleeding (_bleeding manifestation_)

### Extremities

- Cold and clammy extremities or warm (_sign of shock_)
- Capiliary refill test (_sign of shock_)
- Presence of petechiae (_bleeding manifestation_)
- Recovery rash
- Pulse
  - Rate [^1]
  - Volume (_weak, good_)
- Blood pressure [^2]
- Hess test

[^1]: **Normal heart rate**: 1 year (100-160/min), 2-5 year (95-140/min), 5-12 year (80-120/min), >12 year (60-100/min)
[^2]: **Normal blood pressure**: Systolic (90 + age in year), Diastolic (55 + age in year)

### Abdomen

1. `Inspection`
   - Distended (_ascites_)
1. `Palpation`
   - Light palpation: Tenderness
   - Deep palpation: Liver, Spleen
1. `Percussion`
   - Shifting dullness
     - If present, fluid thrill

### CVS

1. `Auscultation`
   - 1st and 2nd heart sound (_faint in pericardial effusion_)

### Respiratory

1. `Inspection`
   - Respiratory rate [^3] (_fast breathing_)
   - Movement of the chest (_reduced at the side of plural effusion_)
1. `Palpation`
   - Trachea (_shift to opposite in massive plural effusion_)
1. `Percussion` (Posterior only)
1. `Auscultation` (Posterior only)
   - Breath sound (_reduced at the side of plural effusion_)
   - Air entry (_reduced at the side of plural effusion_)
   - Added sound (_rhonchi and crepitations in pulmonary edema_)

[^3]: **Respiratory rate**: 0-2 months (>60/min), 2-12 months (>50/min), 1-5 years (>40/min)

</Steps>

## Differential Diagnosis

1. Dengue Fever Grade II
1. Dengue Shock Syndrome (Dengue Haemorrhagic Fever Grade III)
1. Chikengunya
1. Immune thrombocytopenic perpura (ITP)
1. Sepsis
