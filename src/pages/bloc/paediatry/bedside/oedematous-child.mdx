# Oedematous Child

> \_\_\_ year old child admitted for puffiness of face and generalized oedema. Take history to get the diagnosis.

## History

import { Steps } from "nextra/components";

<Steps>

### Introduction

- Introduce yourself
- Explain and get permission

### History of Present Illness

1. `Oedema`
   - Onset (_sudden, grandual_)
   - Duration
   - Started Site (_face, periorbital, generalized_)
   - Spread (_generalized_)
1. `Associated Symptoms`
   - Urine
     - Amount
     - Color (_smoky red, high, straw color_)
   - Vomiting (_haematamesis_)
   - Diarrhoea (_melaena_)
   - Breathlessness
   - Poor feeding
   - Yellowish coloration of skin or sclera (_chronic liver disease_)
   - Worm passing
1. `History of taking drugs`
   - Any medication before onset of illness (_angioneurotic edema_)
   - Particular food that can cause allergy
1. `Complications`
   - `Heart failure`
     - Breathlessness
     - Poor feeding
     - PND
     - Fatigue
     - Cough
   - `Hypertensive Encephalopathy`
     - Headache
     - Projectile vomiting
     - Convulsion
   - Abdominal pain

### Past Medical History

1. History of `skin infection` before oedema
1. History of `oedema`
1. History of `jaundice`
1. History of `sore throat, joint pain, palpitation, dyspnoea`
1. History of taking `prednisolone`
   - Dose
   - Duration
   - Response
1. History of `food allergy`
1. History of `congenital heart disease` or cardiac problem

### Immunization History

- BCG, Measles, HBV

### Drug History

- Medications (_steroid_)

### Social History

- Caretaker
- Mother's education
- Family income

### Family History

- Number of family members
- Type of family
- Silimar illness in family members

### Nutritional History

- Exclusive breast feeding
- Weaning diet
- Usual daily diet

</Steps>

## Physical Examination

<Steps>

### Introduction [#PE]

- Introduce yourself
- Explain and get permission

### General Inspection

- Well or ill
- Alert or drowsy or lethargy
- Dyspnoeic
- Nutritional status

### Head

- Febrile or afebrile
- Periorbital oedema

### Extremities

- Scars, Skin infection
- Pulse [^1]
  - Rate (_tachycardia_)
- Blood pressure [^2] (_Increase in APSGN_)
- JVP
- Pitting oedema in legs

[^1]: **Normal heart rate**: 1 year (100-160/min), 2-5 year (95-140/min), 5-12 year (80-120/min), >12 year (60-100/min)
[^2]: **Normal blood pressure**: Systolic (90 + age in year), Diastolic (55 + age in year)

### Abdomen

1. `Inspection`

   - Shape (_distension_)
   - Striae

1. `Palpation`

   - Light palpation: Tenderness
   - Deep palpation: Liver, Kidneys
   - Shifting dullness (_ascites_)
     - If present, fluid thrill
   - Assess urine albumin (_proteinuria_)

### Cardiovascular System

#### Palpation

- Apex beat

#### Auscultation

- 1st and 2nd heart sound

### Respiratory System

#### Inspection

- Scars, Chest tube
- Movement of chest
- Respiratory rate [^1] (_fast breathing_)

#### Auscultation (Posterior)

- (Sacral oedema)
- Air entery
- Basal crepitations

[^1]: **Fast breathing**: 0-2 months (>60/min), 2-12 months (>50/min), 1-5 years (>40/min)

</Steps>

## Differential Diagnosis

1. Acute post streptococcal glomerulonephritis
1. Nephrotic syndrome
1. Congestive cardiac failure
1. Chronic liver diseases (Hepatitis B & C, Cirrhosis of liver)
1. Malnutrition (Protein energy malnutrition)
1. Angioneurotic oedema
1. Salt and water retention due to prolong steroids
