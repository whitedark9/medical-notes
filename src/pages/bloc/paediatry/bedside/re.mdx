### History of Present Illness

1. `Pallor`
   - Onset (_sudden, gradual_)
   - Duration
1. `Symptoms of pallor`
   - Easily tired
   - Play like other children
   - Less active
   - Palpitation
   - Dyspnoea on exertion
   - Loss of appetide
1. `Symptoms of bleeding` (_is pallor proportional to bleeding_)
   - Red spots under the skin (_skin bleeding_)
   - Nose bleed, Gum bleeding, Haematamesis, Maelena (_mucosal bleeding_)
   - Headache, Vomiting during bleeding episode (_intracranial bleeding_)
   - Amount
   - Duration
1. `Haemolytic Symptoms`
   - Yellow colouration of skin and eyes (_haemolytic anaemia_)
   - High coloured urine (_haemoglobinuria_)
1. `Fever` (_low WBC, malaria, sepsis_)
   - Onset (_sudden, gradual_)
   - Duration
   - Pattern (_continuous, intermittent, remittent_)
   - Temperature (_low grade, high grade_)
   - Associated symptoms:
     - chills & rigor
     - evening rise in temperature
     - sweating
   - Medication (_sponge taping, paracetamol_)
1. `Joint Pain, Bone Pain` (_leukaemias, lymphoma_)
   - Swollen joints
1. `Lymphadenopathy`
   - Any lumps in the body (_neck, grion, abdomen_)
1. `Organomegaly`
   - Abdomen distension

### Past Medical History

- `Jaundice`
- History of `hospitalization`
  - Reason
  - Frequency
- `Transfusion` History
  - Type of blood products (_whole blood, packed red cells_)
  - Interval
  - Any recently shortened interval
  - Any chelating therapy
  - Use of properly screened blood
  - Reaction after transfusion
- `Chronic/recurrent infection` (_low WBCs_)
- `Worm passing` (_chronic infestation_)

### Drug History

- `Regular medication` (_steroids, anti-cancer_)
- Drugs that cause `aplastic anaemia`
  - Sulphur containing drugs
  - Chloramphenicol
  - Anti-epileptic drugs
  - Chemotherapeutic drugs
  - Gold containing indigenous medicine
- `Radiation` exposure
- Contact with `insecticides` or `fertilizers`, `lead`

### Dietary History

- Exclusive breast feeding
- `Iron` in weaning diet (_IDA_)

### Family & Social History

- `Consanguineous marriage`
- `Similar illness` in the family
- Travel to `malarai` endemic area

## Differential Diagnosis

1. Iron deficiency anaemia (IDA)
1. Thalassaemia (beta thalassaemia major)
1. Malaria
1. Acute intravascular haemolysis (e.g. G6PD deficiency)
1. Chronic infection/infestation
1. Aplastic anaemia
1. Hematological malignancy/lymphoma
