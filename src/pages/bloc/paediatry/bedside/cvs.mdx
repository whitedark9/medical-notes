# Cardiovascular System

> \_\_\_ year old child admitted for repeted chest infection. Examine the cardiovascular system.

## Physical Examination Only

import { Steps } from "nextra/components";

<Steps>

### Introduction

- Introduce yourself
- Explain and get permission

### General Inspection

- Well or ill
- Alert or drowsy
- Nutritional status
- Dyspnoeic

### Head

- Febrile or afebrile
- Facial abnormalities (_Down's syndrome_)
- Central cyanosis (_cyanotic heart disease - TOF_)
- Teeth & Gum (_healthy or not_)
- JVP in > 5 year old (_JVP raised in CHF_)

### Extremities

- Clubbing (_suppurative lung disease_)
- Peripheral cyanosis
- Palmar pallor
- Features of **Infective endocarditis**:
  - Splinter haemorrhage
  - Oslar's node
  - Janeway lesions
- Pitting edema in legs
- Pulse [^1]
  - Rate
  - Rhythm
  - Volume (_bounding pulse in respiratory failure_)
  - Character
  - Equality
  - Radiofemoral delay
  - Dorsalis pedis pulse
- Blood pressure [^2] (_wide pulse pressure in PDA_)

[^1]: **Normal heart rate**: 1 year (100-160/min), 2-5 year (95-140/min), 5-12 year (80-120/min), >12 year (60-100/min)
[^2]: **Normal blood pressure**: Systolic (90 + age in year), Diastolic (55 + age in year)

### Precordial Examination

1. **Inspection**

   - Shape (_precordial bulge_)
   - Scar, Chest tube
   - Visible pulsation & apex beat

1. **Palpation**

   - `Apex beat`
     - Site
     - Character (_heaving apex in LVH, taping in RVH_)
   - `Parasternal heave` (_in RVH_)
   - `Thrill` (_murmur grading at least 4/6_)
     - Type
     - Site
     - Radiation
   - `Palpable P2` (_pulmonary hyptertension_)

1. **Auscultation**

   - 1st and 2nd heart sound
     - Mitral, Tricuspid, Aortic, Pulmonary areas
   - Added sounds (Murmur)
     - Type
     - Site
     - Character
     - Loudest place
     - Grading
     - Radiation

### Examination for Complications

1. **Abodmen**
   - Liver (_IE_)
   - Spleen (_IE_)
1. **Chest (Posterior)**
   - Basal crepitation (_Heart Failure_)

</Steps>

## Murmur

### Grading

1. **Grade I** : Barely audible
1. **Grade II** : Soft, clearly audible
1. **Grade III** : Easy to hear, moderately loud
1. **Grade IV** : Loud with palpable thrill
1. **Grade V** : Very loud, thrill present, heard by touching with streptoscope
1. **Grade VI** : Heard without streptoscope

### Type

1. Pan-systolic murmur (PSM)
   - **Site**: Left lower sternal edge (LLSE)
   - **Radiation**: Along left sternal border (in VSD)
1. Ejection systolic murmur (ESM)
   - **Site**: Upper sternal border (in TOF)
1. Continuous machinery murmur
   - **Site**: 2nd left intercostal space
   - **Radiation**: Left clavicle and back (in PDA)
