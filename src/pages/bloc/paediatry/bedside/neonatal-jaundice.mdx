# Neonatal Jaundice

> Take history of the newborn baby (\_\_ days old) with neonatal jaundice.

## History Only

import { Steps } from "nextra/components";

<Steps>

### Introduction

- Introduce yourself
- Explain and get permission

### History of Present Illness

> အသက်, အဝါ, အတက်, fever, မ suck, အနာ

1. `Age` of the baby
1. `Jaundice`:

   - **Onset**
   - **Progress**: Is jaundice getting better or progressive?
   - **Condition**: Is the baby lethargic or irritable? Does the baby cry with high pitch sound? (_early kernicterus_)

1. `Fits` (_late kernicterus_):

   - If present, how frequent?

1. `Fever` (_neonatal sepsis_):

   - If present, ask onset, character, nature

1. `Poor feeding` (_early kernicterus_) and `Breast or Bottle feeding` (_breast milk jaundice_)
1. `Infection`

   - Redness around umbilicus or discharge from it (_umbilical sepsis_)
   - Any pustules, crusts or blisters over any part of baby (_Staphylococcal infection - Acquired infection_)

### Antenatal History

- `Maternal illness`
  - Any febrile illness with skin rashes during pregnancy (_congenital infection_)

### Birth History

1. `Parity`
   - **Parity of pregnancy, Abortion, Still birth** (_Rh incompatibility_)
1. `Sex`
   - A boy or a girl (_G6PD deficiency_)
1. `PROM`
   - **Duration from rupture of membrane to delivery** (_perinatal sepsis_)
1. `Foul smelling discharge before/after delivery` (_perinatal sepsis_)
1. `Mode of delivery`
   - **Instrumental delivery**, **Birth trauma** (_cephalhematoma_, _birth asphyxia_)
1. `Baby condition after birth` (_birth asphyxia_)

   - Cry immediately after birth
   - Sign of cyanosis, Oxygen administration
   - Any resuscitation

1. `Due date or Expected date of delivery` (_preterm or term baby_)
1. `Birth weight` (_low birth weight_)

### Family History

- Histor of neonatal jaundice in `previous children`
  - If present, how were they treated
- `Blood group` of mother, baby, father
- `Rh group` of mother, baby, father

</Steps>

## Differential Diagnosis

1. Physiological neonatal jaundice
1. Neonatal jaundice most probably due to sepsis
