# Hypertension

## Definition

| Category                                                                        | Systolic BP                        | Diastolic BP                    |
| :------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------- | :------------------------------ |
| **Blood pressure**<br/>Optimal<br/>Normal<br/>High normal                       | <br/>< 120<br/>< 130<br/>< 130-139 | <br/>< 80<br/>85<br/>< 85-89    |
| **Hypertension**<br/>Grade 1 (mild)<br/>Grade 2 (moderate)<br/>Grade 3 (severe) | <br/>140-159<br/>160-179<br/>≥ 180 | <br/>90-99<br/>100-109<br/>>110 |
| **Isolated systolic hypertension** <br/>Grade 1<br/>Grade 2                     | <br/>140-159<br/>≥ 160             | <br/>< 90<br/>< 90              |

<details>
<summary>Blood Pressure Formula</summary>

> BP = CO x SVR.

- BP = Blood Pressure
- CO = Cardiac Output
- SVR = Systemic Vascular Resistance

> CO = SV x HR.

- SV = Stroke Volume
- HR = Heart Rate

> SV = EDV - ESV.

- EDV = End-Diastolic Volume
- ESV = End-Systolic Volume

> MBP = DBP + 1/3(SBP - DBP).

- MBP = Mean Blood Pressure
- DBP = Diastolic Blood Pressure
- SBP = Systolic Blood Pressure

</details>

## Complications

> HMOD (Hypertion Mediated Organ Damage or Hypertensive Target Organ Damage)

1. Brain
   - Stroke
   - Hypertensive encephalopathy
1. Eyes
   - Hypertensive Retinopathy (silver wing, arteriovenous nipping, haemorrhage & exudates, papilloedema)
1. Heart
   - Left ventricular hypertrophy
   - Heart failure
   - Ventricular arrhythmias, Atrial fibrillation
   - Aortic dissection
   - Sudden cardiac death
1. Kidneys
   - Acute/Chronic kidney disease
1. Vessels
   - Transient ischemic attack (TIA)
   - Coronary artery disease (IHD, MI)
   - Peripheral arterial disease

<details>
<summary>Changes in vessels due to hypertension</summary>

1. Large arteries (> 1 mm in diameter):
   - Vessels dilate → Become tortuous
1. Small arteries (< 1 mm in diameter):
   - Arteriosclerosis → Lumen narrows → ~~Aneurysms~~
1. Plaques(Artheroma) in vessels → Narrow vessels → Worsen hypertension → More plaques → ↑Peripheral vascular resistance & ↓renal blood flow
1. ↓Renal blood flow → Activate RAAS (Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System) → **Hypertension**

</details>

<details>
<summary>Read about RAAS</summary>

- Has 4 organs
- `Liver` produce **Angiotensinogen** protein
- ↓Renal blood flow or ↓blood volume & pressure → `Kidney` produces **Renin** → **Renin** converts **Angiotensinogen** to **Angiotensin I** → **ACE (Angiotensin-Converting-Enzyme)** converts **Angiotensin I** to **Angiotensin II** in `Lungs` → Action of **Angiotensin II** (_vasoconstriction, thirst_) → Release of **Aldosterone** from `Adrenal glands` → Action of **Aldosterone** (_salt and water reabsorption_) → ↑Blood volume and pressure
- RAAS can be activated in hypertension when renal blood flow is decreased

</details>

## Clinical Features

1. **Asymptomatics**
1. **Features of underlying cause of hypertension**
   - Radio-femoral delay in coarctation of aorta
   - Enlarged kidneys in polycystic kidney disease
   - Abdominal bruits in renal artery stenosis
   - Characteristic facies and habitus of Cushing's syndrome
1. **Features of complications**
   - Signs of left ventricular hypertrophy, heart failure, MI
   - Impaired renal function
   - Stroke, aortic aneurysms
1. **Presence of comorbidities**
   - Diabetes
   - Hyperlipidaemia
   - Hyperuricemia
   - Ischemic heart disease
   - Cerebrovascular accident

## Investigations

<details>
<summary>How to measure blood pressure</summary>

- Use a **machine** that has been validated, well maintained and properly caliberated
- Measure **sitting** BP routinely, with additional **standing** BP in older and diabetic patients and those with possible postural hypotension; rest the patient for 2 minutes
- **Remove tight clothing** from the arm
- Support the **arm at the level of the heart**
- Use a **cuff of appropriate size** (the bladder must encompass more than two-thrids of the arm)
- **Lower** the pressure **slowly** (2 mmHg per second)
- **Read the BP** to the nearest 2 mmHg
- Use phase V (**disappearance of sounds**) to measure diastolic BP
- Take **two measurements** at each visit

</details>

1. **Ambulatory BP recording**: To assess borderline or 'white coat' hypertension
1. Tests to detect underlying causes
   - **Blood urea**, **electrolytes** and **creatinine**
   - **Renal ultrasound**: To detect possible renal disease
   - **Renal angiography**: To detect or confirm renal artery stenosis
   - **Urinary catecholamines**: To detect possible phaeochromocytoma
   - **Urinary cortisol and dexamethasone suppression test**: To detect possible Cushing's syndrome
   - **Plasma renin activity and aldosterone**: To detect possible primary aldosteronism
   - **Thyroid function test**: To detect hypo- or Hyperthyroidism
   - **Urine HCG test**: To detect pregnancy
   - **Serum total and HDL cholesterol** (High-density lipoprotein)
1. Tests to detect complications
   - **Urinalysis** for blood, protein and glucose
   - **12-lead ECG**: To detect myocardial ischaemia, left ventricular hypertrophy
   - **Chest X-ray**: To detect cardiomegaly, heart failure, coarctation of the aorta
   - **Echocardiogram**: To detect or quantify left ventricular hypertrophy
1. Tests to detect comorbidities
   - **Blood glucose (Fasting, Random, OGTT), HbA1c**: To detect diabetes mellitus

### General Management

> Life-style interventions <br/>may obviate the need for drug therapy in patients with borderline hypertension,<br/> reduce the dose or the number of drugs required in patients with established hypertension, and <br />directly reduce cardiovascular risk.

> SAVED

1. `S`moking
   - Cessation of smoking
1. `A`lcohol
   - Reduction or cessation of alcohol consumption
1. `V`accination
   - Consideration of influenza and pneumococcal vaccination
1. `E`ducation
   - Explanation of nature of disease, treatment and self-help strategies
1. `E`xercise
   - Regular moderate exercise within limit
1. `D`iet
   - Good nutrition and weight reduction
   - Avoidance of salty foods

## Hypertensive Urgency

> Severe hypertension (SBP ≥180mmHg, DBP ≥120mmHg) without acute end-organ damage.

### Management[#HU]

> Gradual BP reduction over 24-48 hours using oral medications.

- ACE inhibitor (`Captopril`: 25-50 mg every 1-2 hours)
- Peripheral alpha-2 adrenergic agonist (`Clonidine`: 0.1-0.2 mg every hour (max 0.7 mg))
- Alpha-1 and Beta-adrenoceptor antagonists (`Labetalol`: 200-400 mg orally every 2-3 hours)

---

## Hypertensive Emergency

> Severe hypertension (usually DBP ≥120mmHg) with acute end-organ damage.

### End Organ Damages

1. **Neurologic emergencies**: ischemic stroke, hemorrhagic stroke, hypertensive encephalopathy
1. **Cardiac emergencies**: acute heart failure, acute coronary syndrome
1. **Vascular emergencies**: acute aortic dissection
1. **Renal emergencies**: acute hypertensive nephrosclerosis (malignant nephrosclerosis)
1. **Sympathetic overactivity**: pheochromocytoma, severe autonomic dysfunction
1. Hypertensive emergencies **during pregnancy**

### Management[#HE]

> Rapid BP reduction with IV medications (reduce MAP by ≤25% in the first hour, then gradually over 24-48 hours).

- Nitrates (`Sodium nitroprusside`: 0.25-10 μg/kg/min)
- Calcium channel antagonists (`Nicardipine`: 5 mg/h, titrate to 15 mg/h)
- Alpha-1 and Beta-adrenoceptor antagonists (`Labetalol`: 20 mg IV, repeat or infusion 1-2 mg/min)
- Cardioselective beta-adrenoceptor antagonists (`Esmolol`: 500 μg/kg bolus, then 50-300 μg/kg/min)
- `Hydralazine`: 10-20 mg IV every 4-6 hours

[^1]: **PKD (Polycystic kidney disease)**: Genetics (Autosomal dominant/recessive) disorder where inside kidneys is numerous fluid-filled cysts → Hypertension (earliest), Kidneys [Size↑, Pain, Failure, Stones, UTI]

[^2]: **Coarctation of aorta**: Congenital heart defect where aorta is narrow → hypertension (in older children & adults)

[^3]: **Accelerated Hyeprtension**: Signs of papilloedema or retinal haemorrhage

[^4]: **Phaeochromocytoma**: A rare tumor of adrenal gland that causes hypertension by secreting excess catecholamine hormones, such as epinephrine and norepinephrine
