# Murmurs

| Disease / Lesion                                   | Murmur Type & Timing                                         | Loudest Area (Auscultation)               | Radiation / Characteristic Features                                               |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Aortic Stenosis**                                | Ejection systolic murmur (crescendo–decrescendo)             | Right 2nd intercostal space (aortic area) | Radiates to carotids; slow-rising pulse; ejection click                           |
| **Aortic Regurgitation**                           | Early diastolic decrescendo murmur                           | Left sternal edge (3rd–4th ICS)           | Best heard sitting forward in expiration; collapsing (water-hammer) pulse         |
| **Mitral Stenosis**                                | Mid-diastolic, low-pitched rumbling murmur with opening snap | Apex (mitral area, left 5th ICS, MCL)     | No radiation; accentuated in left lateral position; presystolic accentuation      |
| **Mitral Regurgitation**                           | Pan-systolic murmur                                          | Apex                                      | Radiates to left axilla; soft S1; displaced apex beat                             |
| **Tricuspid Regurgitation**                        | Pan-systolic murmur                                          | Lower left sternal border                 | Increases with inspiration (Carvallo’s sign); may have pulsatile liver            |
| **Tricuspid Stenosis**                             | Mid-diastolic rumbling murmur                                | Lower left sternal border                 | Louder on inspiration; associated with giant a wave in JVP                        |
| **Pulmonary Stenosis**                             | Ejection systolic murmur                                     | Left upper sternal border                 | May radiate to left shoulder; ejection click present                              |
| **Pulmonary Regurgitation**                        | Early diastolic decrescendo murmur                           | Left upper sternal border                 | Graham-Steell murmur (in pulmonary hypertension)                                  |
| **Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy (HOCM)** | Ejection systolic murmur                                     | Left sternal edge / Apex                  | Increases with Valsalva/standing; decreases with squatting; may radiate to axilla |
| **Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA)**                 | Continuous "machinery" murmur                                | Left infraclavicular area                 | Radiates to back; bounding pulse                                                  |
| **Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD)**                | Pan-systolic harsh murmur                                    | Lower left sternal border                 | Often with palpable thrill; may radiate widely                                    |
| **Atrial Septal Defect (ASD)**                     | Ejection systolic murmur (pulmonary flow) + fixed split S2   | Pulmonary area (left 2nd ICS)             | No significant radiation                                                          |
