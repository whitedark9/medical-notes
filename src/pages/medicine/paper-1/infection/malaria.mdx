# Malaria

## Incubation Period

| Organisms               | Incubation Period | RBC Age Affected            |
| ----------------------- | ----------------- | --------------------------- |
| _Plasmodium falciparum_ | 7-14 days         | All stages of RBCs          |
| _Plasmodium vivax_      | 12-17 days        | Reticulocytes (young RBCs)  |
| _Plasmodium ovale_      | 16-18 days        | Reticulocytes (young RBCs)  |
| _Plasmodium malariae_   | 18-40 days        | Senescent RBCs (older RBCs) |
| _Plasmodium knowlesi_   | 9-12 days         | All stages of RBCs          |

## Clinical Features

- Non-specific; Suspect anyone returning from an endemic area

### _P. falciparum_ infection

- Insidious onset
- Malaise, headache, vomiting
- Cough and mild diarrhoea
- Fever has no particular pattern
- Jaundice, Hepatomegaly, Splenomegaly
- Delirium, seizures, coma (Cerebral malaria)
- Abortion and intrauterin growth retardation
- ↑risk in previous splenectomy

### _P. vivax_ and _P. ovale_ infection

- Several days of continued high fever with rigor
- Then, fever on alternate days (Fever rises to about 40°C and falls gradually, repeated 48 hours later)
- Tender hepatomegaly and Splenomegaly
- Anaemia
- Relapses frequent in the frist 2 years
- Maybe acquired from blood transfusion

### _P. malariae_ and _P. knowlesi_ infection

- Mild symptoms
- Fever every third day
- Parasitemia persist many years → Glomerulonephritis and long-term nephrotic syndrome in children

## Investigations

- WHO recommends to confirm malaria diagnosis with **microscopy** or **RDT** before initiating treatment.
- **Molecular methods (PCR)** are recommended in research or complex cases but not for routine clinical settings.

### 1. Microscopic Diagnosis

- **Giemsa-stained blood smears:**
  - **Thick smears:** Used to detect and quantify malaria parasites in the blood.
  - **Thin smears:** Used to identify the species of _Plasmodium_.
- **Benefits:**
  - Gold standard for malaria diagnosis.
  - Allows determination of parasitemia levels.

### 2. Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs)

- Detect specific malaria antigens in blood (e.g., _Plasmodium falciparum_ histidine-rich protein 2 [PfHRP2], aldolase, or lactate dehydrogenase [LDH]).
- **Advantages:**
  - Quick results (15-30 minutes).
  - Useful in settings with limited laboratory facilities.
- **Limitations:**
  - May not differentiate species in non-falciparum malaria.
  - False negatives can occur with low parasitemia.

### 3. Molecular Diagnosis

- **Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR):**
  - Detects _Plasmodium_ DNA.
  - High sensitivity and specificity.
- **Use:**
  - Research settings or confirmation of species in complex cases.
  - Not recommended for routine clinical use due to cost and infrastructure requirements.

### 4. Serology

- Detects antibodies to malaria antigens.
- **Use:**
  - Not for active infection diagnosis but for epidemiological studies.

### 5. Other Investigations (for Complications)

- **Full Blood Count (FBC):** To detect anemia and thrombocytopenia.
- **Liver Function Tests (LFTs):** To assess liver involvement in severe cases.
- **Renal Function Tests:** To monitor for acute kidney injury (AKI).
- **Blood Glucose:** To detect hypoglycemia in severe malaria.
- **Arterial Blood Gas (ABG):** For metabolic acidosis in severe malaria.
- **Imaging:** Chest X-ray may be required in pulmonary complications.

## Management

### Management of Complications

1. Coma
   - Maintain airway
   - Nurse on side
   - Exclude other treatable causes of coma (e.g. hypoglycaemia, bacterial meningitis)
   - Avoid harmful ancillary treatments such as `glucorticoids`, `heparin` and `adrenaline`
   - Intubate if necessary
1. Convulsions
   - Maintain airway
   - Treat promptly with `diazepam`
1. Shock ('algid malaria')
   - Suspect Gram-negative spesis
   - Take blood cultures
   - Give parenteral `antimicrobials`
   - Correct haemodynamic disturbance
1. Acute kidney injury
   - Exclude pre-renal causes
   - Fluid resuscitaton if appropriate
   - `Peritoneal dialysis` (`haemofiltration` or `haemodialysis` if available)
1. Acute pulmonary oedema
   - Nurse at 45°
   - Give oxygen, venesect[^1] 250 mL of blood
   - Give `diuretic`, Stop IV fluids
   - Intubate and PEEP/CPAP in life-threatening hypoxaemia
   - Haemofilter
1. Aspiration pneumonia
   - Give parenteral `antimicrobials`
   - Change position
   - Physiotherapy
   - Give oxygen
1. Hyperparasitaemia
   - Consider exchange transfusion (e.g. > 10% of circulating erythrocytes parasitised in non-immune patient with severe disease)
1. Metabolic acidosis
   - Exclude or treat hypoglycaemia, hypovolaemia and Gram-negative spesis
   - Fluid resuscitaton
   - Give oxygen
1. Hyperpyrexia
   - Tepid sponging, fanning, cooling blanket
   - Antipyretic drug (`paracetamol`)
1. Spontaneous bleeding and coagulopathy
   - Transfuse screened fresh whole blood (`cryoprecipitate`/`fresh frozen plasma` and `platelets` if available)
   - `Vit K` injection
1. Severe anaemia (packed cell volume < 15%)
   - Transfuse `fresh whole blood` or `packed cells` if pathogen screening of donor blood is available
1. Hypoglycemia
   - Measure blood glucose
   - Give `50% dextrose` injection followed by `10% dextrose` infusion (glucagon may be ineffective)

### Specific Therapy for Severe _falciparum_ malaria

- **Preferred therapy**:
  - `Artesunate` 2.4 mg/kg IV at 0, 12 and 24 hrs,
  - Then once daily for 7 days
  - Once patient can take orally, switch to 2mg/kg orally once daily, to complete a total cumulative dose of 17-18 mg/kg
- **Alternative**:
  - `Quinine` loading dose 20 mg/kg IV over 4 hrs, up to a maximum of 1.4 g
  - Then, maintenance dose of 10 mg/kg `quinine salt` given as 4-hr infusions 3 times daily for the first 48 hrs, then twice a day, up to a maximum of 700 mg per dose or until the patient can take orally
  - Add `Doxycycline` (Use `Clindamycin` in pregnancy or young child)
  - ~~Loading dose is contraindicated if `Quinine`, `Quinidine` or `Mefloquine` in previous 24 hrs~~
  - Monitor ECG
- Avoid `Mefloquine` due to ↑risk of post-malaria neurological syndrome

<summary>Mild _falciparum_ malaria</summary>

- **Preferred therapy**: `Co-artemether` (`Artemether` + `Lumefantrine`)
- **Alternative**:
  - `Quinine` + `Doxycycline` (Use `Clindamycin` in pregnancy or young child) **OR**
  - `Atovaquone-proguanil` (`Malarone`)
- **Pregnancy**:
  - `Co-artemether` but avoid in early pregnancy
  - **Alternative**: `Quinine` + `Clindamycin`
- **Other Regimens**: `Artesunate` + `Mefloquine`

<details>
<summary>Non-_falciparum_ malaria</summary>

- **Preferred therapy**: `Chloroquine` + `Primaquine`
- **Chloroquine-resistant _P. vivax_**: `Co-artemether` as for _P. falciparum_
- **Patients with mild to moderate G6PD deficiency**: `Chloroquine` + `Primaquine` weekly orally for 8 weeks
