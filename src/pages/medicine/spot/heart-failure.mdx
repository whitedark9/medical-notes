# Heart Failure

> A 54-year-old man came with 2 days duration of breathlessness at rest and orthopnea. He has history of hypertension and IHD for 10 years. On examination, he has bilateral basal crepitation, raised JVP, enlarged tender liver and leg edema. Discuss the management plan for this patient.

## Diagnosis

- Symptoms: Breathlessness at rest, orthopnea (→ left heart failure).
- Signs: Bilateral basal crepitations (pulmonary edema), raised JVP, tender hepatomegaly, pedal edema (→ right heart failure).
- Background: Long-standing hypertension + IHD → common causes of HF

## Emergency Management

1. **Airway**

   - Ensure airway patency.
   - Oxygen therapy if SpO₂ \< 90% (target 90–94%).
   - Consider CPAP/NIV if severe pulmonary edema.

2. **Breathing**

   - Sit upright (↓ venous return, improves lung compliance).
   - Monitor RR, SpO₂, ABG if hypoxia/hypercapnia suspected.

3. **Circulation**

   - IV access, cardiac monitoring, BP monitoring.
   - ECG → check for ischemia/arrhythmia (precipitating cause).
   - IV loop diuretic (`Furosemide` 40–80 mg IV bolus, repeat if needed).
   - Administer nitrates: `IV glyceryl trinitrate`, `Buccal glyceryl trinitrate` (↓ preload/afterload).
   - Avoid aggressive fluids (risk of overload).
   - If ineffective, add inotropic agents: `Dobutamine`
   - IV `opiates` for distressed patients

4. **Disability**

   - Monitor mental status (hypoxia/low perfusion).
   - Check blood glucose, renal function.

5. **Exposure**

   - Examine for infection, ischemia, arrhythmia, thyroid disease (precipitants).

## Investigations

- **For diagnosis**

  - CXR → pulmonary congestion, cardiomegaly.
  - ECG → ischemia/arrhythmia.
  - Echocardiography → LV function, valve disease.
  - BNP/NT-proBNP (if available).

- **For causes**

  - Cardiac enzymes (troponin) → ACS.
  - Blood pressure profile.
  - Thyroid function tests.
  - Renal function & electrolytes.
  - Hb (anemia as precipitant).

- **For complications**

  - LFTs → congestive hepatopathy.
  - ABG → hypoxia, acidosis.
  - Renal function → cardiorenal syndrome.

- ACS → reperfusion / anti-ischemic therapy.
- Arrhythmias → rate/rhythm control.
- Hypertensive crisis → IV antihypertensives.
- Infection → antibiotics.

### 2. Pharmacological (Chronic HF regimen after stabilization)

1. Angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors (ACE inhibitors)
   - `Enalapril`, `Lisinopril`, `Captopril`
1. Angiotensin receptor blockers (ARB)
   - `Losartan`, `Valsartan`, `Candesartan`
1. Angiotension receptor neprilysin inhibitors (ARNI)
   - `Sacubitril + Valsartan`
1. β-blockers
   - `Bisoprolol`, `Carvedilol`, `Metoprolol`
1. MRA (Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists)
   - `Spironolactone`, `Eplerenone`
1. SGLT2i (Sodium-Glucose Co-Transporter 2 Inhibitors)
   - `Canagliflozin`, `Empagliflozin`, `Dapagliflozin`
1. Loop diuretics: `Furosemide`
1. Thiazide diuretics: `Bendroflumethiazide`

#### For IHD

1. Low dose (75 mg) `aspirin` or `clopidogrel` (75 mg daily)
1. `Statin` (e.g., `atorvastatin`, `rosuvastatin`) even if serum cholesterol concentration is normal

### 3. Non-Pharmacological Management

1. Implantable cardiac defibrillators
1. Cardiac resynchronisation therapy (CRT) devices
1. Coronary revascularisation
1. Cardiac transplantation
1. Ventricular assist devices

### 4. Lifestyle Modification

> SAVED

1. `S`moking
   - Cessation of smoking
1. `A`lcohol
   - Reduction or cessation of alcohol consumption
1. `V`accination
   - Consideration of influenza and pneumococcal vaccination
1. `E`ducation
   - Explanation of nature of disease, treatment and self-help strategies
1. `E`xercise
   - Regular moderate exercise within limit
1. `D`iet
   - Good nutrition and weight reduction
   - Avoidance of salty foods
