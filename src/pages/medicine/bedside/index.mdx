# Introduction

## History Format

0. Introduction
1. Particulars of the Patient
1. Chief Complaints
1. History of Present Illness (HOPI)
1. System Review
1. Past Medical History
1. Past Surgical History
1. Family History
1. Obstetrics and Gynaecological History [Female]
1. Personal History
1. Social History
1. Drug History
1. ICE

### 1. Particulars of the Patient

1. Name
1. Age
1. Sex
1. Marital status (_single/married/divorced/widow or widower_)
1. Address
1. Date of admission
1. Hopital/Ward Unit
1. Race, Religion

### 2. Cheif Complaints

> Do not use medical terms!!!

> Must be in chronological order by duration.

### 3. History of Present Illness

> Symptom analysis/Analysis of chief complaints

1. System/Symptom Analysis
1. Differential Diagnosis
1. Precipitating factors
1. Complication
1. On admission (_current medications, tests_)
1. [Background history] (_e.g., referred from other hospital_)

#### Analysis of Pain (_SOCRATES_)

1. **S**ite
1. **O**nset (_gradual/abrupt_)
1. **C**haracter (_stabbing/burning/pricking/gnawing_)
1. **R**adiation/**R**eferred pain
1. **A**ssociated symptoms
1. **T**iming
1. **E**xacerbating factors and relieving factors
1. **S**everity

### 4. System Review

> Can use medical terms.

#### Cardiovascular System

1. Chest pain/Chest discomfort
1. Dyspnoea (Breathlessness)
1. Palpitation
1. Syncope/Presyncope
1. Oedema

#### Respiratory System

1. Cough
1. Sputum
1. Haemoptysis
1. Chest pain/Chest discomfort
1. Dyspnoea
1. Wheeze

#### Gastro-intestinal System

1. General
   - Loss of weight & loss of appetite (anorexia)
   - Abdominal pain & distension
1. Upper GI
   - Painful lips, tongue & mouth
   - Dysphagia (difficulty in swallowing)
   - Odynophagia (pain on swallowing)
   - Heartburn & Reflux symptoms
   - Dyspepsis (Pain or discomfort in upper abdomen)
   - Nausea & Vomiting
   - Haematemesis
   - elaena
   - Wind (Belching)
1. Lower GI
   - Wind (Excessive/offensive flatus or borborygmi)
   - Altered bowel habit (constipation, diarrhoea, steatorrhoea)
   - Hematochezia (rectal bleeding)
1. Hepatobiliary
   - Jaundice
   - Pruritus (Itchiness)

#### Genitourinary System

1. Dysuria (pain on passing micturition)
1. Frequency
1. Urgency
1. Strangury (force to empty bladder)
1. nocturia
1. Haematuria
1. Polyuria
1. Oliguria
1. Anuria

#### Central Nervous System

1. Headache
1. Syncope
1. Epileptic seizures
1. Dizziness
1. Fall
1. Visual disturbance (_double vision, blurred vision_)
1. Ocular pain

#### Types of Chest Scars

1. Midline Sternotomy Scar
   - Vertical scar along the sternum
   - Coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), Valve replacement/repair, Heart transplant, Congenital heart defect repair
1. Thoracotomy Scar (anteriolaterl, axillary, posteriolateral thoracotomy)
   - Lateral chest wall (along the ribs)
   - Lung resections (lobectomy, pneumonectomy), Esophageal surgery, Open heart surgery (less common)
1. Submammary/Infra-mammary Scar
   - Below the breast, hidden in the inframammary fold
   - Pacemaker/defibrillator insertion, cosmetic breast surgery
1. Clamshell Incision Scars
   - Horizontal incision extending across the chest (bilateral thoracotomy)
   - Bilateral lung transplant, Complex thoracic surgeries
1. Minimally invasive Scars
   - Multiple small incisions in the chest wall or around the breastbone
   - Video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS), Robotic cardiac surgery
1. Trauma
