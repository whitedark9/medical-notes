1. **CVS**: Cardinal symptoms
1. **Respiratory**: Cardinal symptoms

##### 2. Oedema

1. Onset
1. Duration
1. Start & Spread
1. Worse on waking up?

<details>
<summary>Differential Diagnosis of Peripheral Oedema</summary>

1. **Cardiac failure**:
   - Right or Biventricular heart failure
   - Pericardial constriction
   - Cardiomyopathy
1. **Hypoalbuminaemia**: Often widespread, can affect arms and face
   - Nephrotic syndrome (_urine output, frothy urine, haematuria_)
   - Liver disease (_Viral hepatitis, alcohol, cirrhosis of liver_)
   - Protein-losing enteropathy
1. **Drugs**:
   - **Sodium retention**: `Fludrocortisone`, `NSAIDs`
   - **Increasing capillary permeability**: `Nifedipine`, `amlodipine`
1. **Chronic venous insufficiency**: Varicose veins
1. **Chronic lymphatic obstruction**
1. **Idiopathic**: Women > Men

</details>

## Examination

<details>
<summary>Types of Chest Scars</summary>

1. **Midline Sternotomy Scar**
   - Vertical scar along the sternum
   - Coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), Valve replacement/repair, Heart transplant, Congenital heart defect repair
1. **Thoracotomy Scar** (anteriolaterl, axillary, posteriolateral thoracotomy)
   - Lateral chest wall (along the ribs)
   - Lung resections (lobectomy, pneumonectomy), Esophageal surgery, Open heart surgery (less common)
1. **Submammary/Infra-mammary Scar**
   - Below the breast, hidden in the inframammary fold
   - Pacemaker/defibrillator insertion, cosmetic breast surgery
1. **Clamshell Incision Scars**
   - Horizontal incision extending across the chest (bilateral thoracotomy)
   - Bilateral lung transplant, Complex thoracic surgeries
1. **Minimally invasive Scars**
   - Multiple small incisions in the chest wall or around the breastbone
   - Video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS), Robotic cardiac surgery
1. **Trauma**

</details>
