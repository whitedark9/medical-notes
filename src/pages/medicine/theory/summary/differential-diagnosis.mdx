1. Lymphoma (Hodgkin's & Non-Hodgkin's)
1. Myelofibrosis
1. Thalassemia Major
1. Gaucher's Disease[^1]
1. Cirrhosis with Portal Hypertension
1. Autoimmune Disorders (Felty's Syndrome, SLE, Sarcoidosis)
1. Kala-azar (Visceral Leishmaniasis)
1. Malaria (Chronic or Severe _Falciparum_ Malaria)
1. Schistosomiasis

## Polyarthritis & Polyarthralgia in a 23-Year-Old Woman

1. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
1. Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)
1. Reactive Arthritis (Post-Infectious, e.g., Chlamydia, Salmonella)
1. Psoriatic Arthritis
1. Viral Arthritis (e.g., Parvovirus B19, Chikungunya, Hepatitis B & C)
1. Rheumatic Fever
1. Sjögren's Syndrome
1. Seronegative Spondyloarthritis (e.g., Ankylosing Spondylitis, IBD-associated Arthritis)
1. Mixed Connective Tissue Disease (MCTD)
1. Fibromyalgia (Mimicking Inflammatory Arthritis)

<details>
<summary>Definitions of differential diagnosis of polyarthritis & polyarthralgia in a 23-year-old woman</summary>

1. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
   - Chronic autoimmune disease causing symmetric arthritis in small joints (e.g., hands, wrists).
2. Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)
   - Autoimmune disease with joint pain, skin rashes, and organ involvement (kidneys, heart, CNS).
3. Reactive Arthritis
   - Post-infectious arthritis (e.g., after Chlamydia, Salmonella) with asymmetric joint involvement.
4. Psoriatic Arthritis
   - Arthritis associated with psoriasis, affecting DIP joints, often with nail changes and dactylitis.
5. Viral Arthritis
   - Arthritis from viral infections (e.g., Parvovirus, Chikungunya, Hepatitis), often self-limiting.
6. Rheumatic Fever
   - Post-streptococcal inflammation causing migratory arthritis and cardiac involvement.
7. Sjögren's Syndrome
   - Autoimmune disorder causing dry eyes, dry mouth, and arthritis.
8. Seronegative Spondyloarthritis
   - Group of inflammatory arthritis (e.g., Ankylosing Spondylitis) with enthesitis and asymmetric joint involvement.
9. Mixed Connective Tissue Disease (MCTD)
   - Overlap of features of SLE, systemic sclerosis, and polymyositis, including arthritis.
10. Fibromyalgia
    - Chronic pain syndrome with musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, and no joint inflammation.

</details>

## Macrocytic Anaemia

### Megaloblastic Macrocytic Anaemia

1. Vitamin B 12 deficiency (e.g., pernicious anaemia, malabsorpton)
1. Folate deficiency (e.g., malnutrition, pregnancy, haemolysis)
1. Drugs (e.g., methotrexate, phenytoin, zidovudine)

### Non-Megaloblastic Macrocytic Anaemia

1. Liver disease (e.g., cirrhosis, hepatitis)
1. Alcoholism
1. Hypothyroidism
1. Myelodysplastic syndromes
1. Aplastic anaemia
1. Reticulocytosis

---

[^1]: **Gaucher's Disease (Go-Shay)**: result of a buildup of certain fatty substances in certain organs
