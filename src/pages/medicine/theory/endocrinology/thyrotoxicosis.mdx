# Thyrotoxicosis

> Discuss clinical features and management of thyrotoxicosis. (UMMG)

> What are the common causes of thyrotoxicosis? A 35 year-old lady presented with palpitation sweating, loss of weight but increase appetite and a midline diffuse neck swelling? How would you manage this lady? (UMM)

## Definition

> Thyrotoxicosis is the constellation of clinical features arising from elevated circulating levels of thyroid hormone.

### Most Common Causes

1. Graves' disease
1. Multinodular goitre
1. Solitary thyroid adenoma
1. Thyroiditis
1. Iodide-induced

### Common Signs

1. Weight loss
1. Tremor
1. Sinus tachycardia
1. Palmar erythema
1. Lid lag, Lid retraction

- Not all thyrotoxicosis have a palpable goitre
- Only Graves' disease has ophthalmopathy, periorbital oedema, conjunctival irritation, exophthalmos, diplopia [^3], _pretibial myxoedema_ and _thyroid acropachy_ [^1]

1. Increase sweating, Heat intolerance, Weight loss despite appetite

1. Warm and moist palms, Palmar erythema, _Acropachy_ [^1], Onycholysis [^2]

1. Tremors, Proximal myopathy in upper arms and legs
1. _Pretibial myxoedema_

## Management

- Depends on the underlying cause

1. `Anti-thyroid drugs (carbimazole, methimazole, propylthiouracil (PTU))` **or** `Radioactive iodine` **or** `Surgery (Thyroidectomy)`
1. `Non-selective β-blocker (e.g., propranolol)` **or** `Verapamil` in asthma

> β-blocker is only for short term.

### Atrial Fibrillation in Thyrotoxicosis

- AF occurs in 10% of thyrotoxicosis
- Risk factor: _Subclinical thyrotoxicosis, Men, Old age_
- Treatment: `Digoxin` + `β-blocker` + `Anti-coagulants (if no contraindication)`
- Prognosis: Once TSH is normal, 50% of patients get sinus rhythm back.
