# Graves' Thyrotoxicosis

> What are the common causes of hyperthyroidism in Myanmar? Discuss the clinical features and management of Graves thyrotoxicosis. (UM2)

## Epidemiology

- Occur at any age, Unusual before puberty
- Most common in **women aged 30-50 years**
- Most common manifestation is thyrotoxicosis with or without goitre
- Has _strong genetic component_ (Monozygotic twins: 20-40%, Dizygotic twins: 5%)
- Triggered by _infection_ (viruses or bacteria)
- Risked factor: _Smoking_ [^1]

## Pathophysiology

> The thyrotoxicosis results from the production of **IgG antibodies directed against the TSH receptor** on the thyroid follicular cell, which stimulate thyroid hormone production and proliferation of follicular cells, leading to goitre in the majority of patients.

- These antibodies are called **thyroid-stimulating immunoglobulins** or **TSH receptor antibodies (TRAb)**
- TRAb can be detected in >95% of patients

#### 1. `Anti-thyroid drugs (carbimazole, methimazole, propylthiouracil (PTU))`

- Action:
  - Inhibit synthesis of new thyroid hormones
- Dosage:
  - Typically starts with high doses
  - Reach euthyroid stage within 6-8 weeks and dose can be reduced
- Prevention of remission:
  - Continue `carbimazole` for 12-18 months
- Relapse:
  - 50% - 70% Relapse within 2 years of stopping treatment
  - Common in younger age, male sex, presence of goitre and higherTRAb
- **Block and replace regimen**:
  - For patients who have unstable T4 and TSH levels between hyperthyroidism and hypothyroidism
  - `Carbimazole 30-40 mg daily` and `Levothyroxine 100-150 ╬╝g daily as replacement therapy`
- Indications:
  - **First episode in patients < 40 years**
- Contraindication:
  - ~~Breastfeeding~~ (PTU suitable)
- Disadvantages/Complications:
  1. _Hypersensitivity rash_
  1. _Agranulocytosis_ (reversible on stopping medicine)
  1. _Hepatotoxicity_ (in PTU, fatal, PTU is used as a second-line drug for breastfeeding & pregnancy)
  1. _> 50% relapse rate_

#### 2. `Thyroidectomy`

- Before surgical operation:
  - `Anti-thyroid drugs` (+ `Oral potassium iodide 60 mg three times daily`) for 10 days **to inhibit thyroid hormone relase and reduce the size of thyroid gland**
- Surgical method:
  1. `Near-total thyroidectomy`
     - Leaves behind only a small portion of gland adjacent to recurrent laryngeal nerves
     - Causes permanent hypothyroidism and high risk of hypoparathyroidism
  1. `Subtotal thyroidectomy`
- Indications:
  1. **Large goitre**
  1. **Poor drug adherence**
  1. **Recurrent thyrotoxicosis**
- Contraindication:
  1. ~~Previous thyroid surgery~~
  1. ~~Dependence on voice (e.g., singer)~~
- Disadvantages/Complications:
  1. _Hypothyroidism_
  1. _Transient hypocalcaemia_
  1. _Permanent hypoparathyroidism_
  1. _Recurrent laryngeal nerve palsy_

#### 3. `Radio-iodine`

- `Oral radio-iodine (131I) single dose ~400-600 MBq`
- Avoid `carbimazole` within 48 hours because it inhibits the efficacy of radio-iodine
- If thyrotoxicosis persists after 6 months, can repeat another dose
- Indications:
  1. **Patients > 40 years**
  1. **Recurrence following surgery**
  1. **Other serious comorbidity**
- Contraindication:
  1. ~~Pregnancy or planned pregnancy within 6 months of treatment~~ (includes men because of radioactive iodine exposure sperm)
  1. ~~Active Graves' ophthalmopathy~~
- Disadvantages/Complications:
  1. _Hypothyroidism_
  1. _Exacerbation of ophthalmopathy_

### Management of Graves' Ophthalmopathy

<details>
<summary>Features of Graves' Ophthalmopathy</summary>

- Common in men and cigarette smokers
- Corneal oedema and optic nerve compression reduce _visual acuity_, _colour vision_ and results _diplopia_
- Wind and bright light worsen lacrimation

</details>

- **Require no treatment**, Only supportive
- Smoking cessation
- `Mythylcellulose eye drops` and `gel` (to comfort dry eyes)
- Protection of eyes from wind and bright light
- Mild case: `Oral selenium (100 ╬╝g twice daily for 6 months)`
- Severe case: `Glucocorticoids` (sometimes `orbital radiotherapy`)

### Management of Pretibial Myxoedema

- `Topical glucocorticoids` for severe case
