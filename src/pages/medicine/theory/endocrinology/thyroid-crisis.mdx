# Thyroid Crisis

## Thyroid Crisis (**Thyroid Storm**)

- Rare but ~~Life-threatening~~ complication of Thyrotoxicosis

## Precipitating factor

1. _Infection_ (in previously unrecognised or inadequately treated thyrotoxicosis)
1. _Thyroidectomy_ (in an ill-prepared patient)
1. _A few days of Radio-iodine therapy_

## Management

- ~~Medical emergency~~ (10% motality even with early treatment)
- Need specialist endocrine help for diagnosis and treatment

1. IV access, fluids if dehydrated, nasogastric tube if vomiting
1. Take blood for: T3, T4, TSH, cultures (if infection suspected)
1. Sedate if necessary (e.g., `chlorpromazine`). Monitor BP
1. If not contraindication, and cardiac output is OK, give `propranolol PO or IV`
   - In asthma/poor cardiac output, use ultra-short-acting Œ≤-blockers, e.g. `IV esmolol`
   - Consider `diltiazem` if Œ≤-blockers contraindicated
   - **Get help**
1. May need `high-dose digoxin`, give with cardiac monitoring
1. Anti-thyroid drugs: `carbimazole PO or via nasogastric tube`;
   - After 4 hr, give `Lugol's solution (aqueous iodine oral solution)`
1. `Hydrocortisone IV` or `dexamethasone PO`
1. Treate suspected infection, e.g., `co-amoxiclav IV`
1. Adjust `IV fluids` as necessary; cool with tepid sponging ¬± `paracetamol`
1. **Continuing treatment**:
   - After 5 day, reduce `carbimazole` dose.
   - After 10 days, stop `propranolol` and `iodine`.
   - Adjust `carbimazole`.

1. IV access, fluids if dehydrated, nasogastric tube if vomiting
1. Take blood for: T3, T4, TSH, cultures (if infection suspected)
1. Sedate if necessary (e.g., `chlorpromazine 50 mg PO/IM`). Monitor BP
1. If not contraindication, and cardiac output is OK, give `propranolol 60 mg/4-6 hr PO`; max IV dose: 1 mg over 10 min; may need repeating every few hours
   - In asthma/poor cardiac output, `propranolol` has caused cardiac arrest in thyroid storm, so ultra-short-acting Œ≤-blockers have a role, e.g. `IV esmolol`
   - Consider `diltiazem` if Œ≤-blockers contraindicated
1. `High-dose digoxin` may be needed to slow the heart, but ensure adequately, give with cardiac monitoring
1. Anti-thyroid drugs: `carbimazole 15-25 mg/6h PO (or via nasogastric tube)`;
   - After 4 hr, give `Lugol's solution (aqueous iodine oral solution) 0.3 mL/8h PO` well diluted in water for 7-10 day to block thyroid
1. `hydrocortisone 100 mg/6h IV` or `dexamethasone 2 mg/6hr PO` to prevent peripheral conversion T4 to T3
1. Treate suspected infection, e.g., with `co-amoxiclav 1.2 g/8hr IV`
1. Adjust `IV fluids` as necessary; cool with tepid sponging ¬± `paracetamol`
1. Continuing treatment:
   - After 5 day reduce `carbimazole` to 15 mg/8hr PO.
   - After 10 days, stop `propranolol` and `iodine`.
   - Adjust `carbimazole`

---

[^1]: **Glucocorticoids & Iodine**: Reduce conversion of T4 to active T3

[^2]: **Sodium ipodate**: Restore serum T3 to normal within 48-72 hours

[^3]: **PTU**: Inhibit new thyroid hormone synthesis

[^4]: **Propranolol & PTU** can be given nasogastric tube
