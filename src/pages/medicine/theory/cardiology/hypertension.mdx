# Hypertension

## Definition

| Category                                                                        | Systolic BP                        | Diastolic BP                    |
| :------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------- | :------------------------------ |
| **Blood pressure**<br/>Optimal<br/>Normal<br/>High normal                       | <br/>< 120<br/>< 130<br/>< 130-139 | <br/>< 80<br/>85<br/>< 85-89    |
| **Hypertension**<br/>Grade 1 (mild)<br/>Grade 2 (moderate)<br/>Grade 3 (severe) | <br/>140-159<br/>160-179<br/>≥ 180 | <br/>90-99<br/>100-109<br/>>110 |

<details>
<summary>Blood Pressure Formula</summary>

> BP = CO x SVR.

- BP = Blood Pressure
- CO = Cardiac Output
- SVR = Systemic Vascular Resistance

> CO = SV x HR.

- SV = Stroke Volume
- HR = Heart Rate

> SV = EDV - ESV.

- EDV = End-Diastolic Volume
- ESV = End-Systolic Volume

> MBP = DBP + 1/3(SBP - DBP).

- MBP = Mean Blood Pressure
- DBP = Diastolic Blood Pressure
- SBP = Systolic Blood Pressure

</details>

## Classification

1. Primary or Idiopathic or Essential Hypertension (95%)
1. Secondary Hypertension (5%)

### Causes of Secondary Hypertension

> A RED COP

1. **A**lcohol
1. **R**enal disease
   - Glomerulonephritis
   - Pyelonephritis
   - Polycystic kidney disease [^1]
1. **E**ndocrine disease
   - Phaeochromocytoma
   - Cushing's syndrome
   - Hypothyroidism
   - Hyperthyroidism (Thyrotoxicosis)
1. **D**rugs
1. **C**oarctation of the aorta [^2]
1. **O**besity
1. **P**regnancy

## Complications

- HMOD (Hypertion Mediated Organ Damage or Hypertensive Target Organ Damage)

1. Brain
   - Stroke
   - Hypertensive encephalopathy
1. Eyes
   - Hypertensive Retinopathy
1. Heart
   - Left ventricular hypertrophy
   - Heart failure
   - Ventricular arrhythmias, Atrial fibrillation
   - Aortic dissection
   - Sudden cardiac death
1. Kidneys
   - Acute/Chronic kidney disease
1. Vessels
   - Transient ischemic attack (TIA)
   - Coronary artery disease (IHD, MI)
   - Peripheral arterial disease

<details>
<summary>Changes in vessels due to hypertension</summary>

1. Large arteries (> 1 mm in diameter):
   - Vessels dilate → Become tortuous
1. Small arteries (< 1 mm in diameter):
   - Arteriosclerosis → Lumen narrows → ~~Aneurysms~~
1. Plaques(Artheroma) in vessels → Narrow vessels → Worsen hypertension → More plaques → ↑Peripheral vascular resistance & ↓renal blood flow
1. ↓Renal blood flow → Activate RAAS (Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System) → **Hypertension**

</details>

<details>
<summary>Read about RAAS</summary>

- Has 4 organs
- `Liver` produce **Angiotensinogen** protein
- ↓Renal blood flow or ↓blood volume & pressure → `Kidney` produces **Renin** → **Renin** converts **Angiotensinogen** to **Angiotensin I** → **ACE (Angiotensin-Converting-Enzyme)** converts **Angiotensin I** to **Angiotensin II** in `Lungs` → Action of **Angiotensin II** (_vasoconstriction, thirst_) → Release of **Aldosterone** from `Adrenal glands` → Action of **Aldosterone** (_salt and water reabsorption_) → ↑Blood volume and pressure
- RAAS can be activated in hypertension when renal blood flow is decreased

</details>

## Diagnosis

### Risk Factors

1. Non-modifiable risk factors (_GAS_)
   - **G**enetics
   - **A**ge
   - **S**ex
1. Modifiable risk factors (_SOLD SEAS_)
   - **S**moking
   - **O**besity
   - Hyper**l**ipidaemia
   - **D**iabetes Mellitus
   - High **s**alt intake
   - Lack of **e**xercise
   - High **a**lcohol consumption
   - **S**tress

### Clinical Features

- Asymptomatics
- Features of underlying cause of hypertension
  - Radio-femoral delay in coarctation of aorta
  - Enlarged kidneys in polycystic kidney disease
  - Abdominal bruits in renal artery stenosis
  - Characteristic facies and habitus of Cushing's syndrome
- Features of complications
  - Signs of left ventricular hypertrophy, 4th heart sound, AF
  - Impaired renal function
  - Stroke, aortic aneurysms
- Presence of comorbidities
  - Diabetes
  - Hyperlipidaemia
  - Hyperuricemia
  - Ischemic heart disease
  - Cerebrovascular accident

### Investigations

### Accessment of Blood Pressure

- Urinalysis for blood, protein and glucose
- Blood urea, electrolytes and creatinine
- Blood glucose
- Serum total and HDL cholesterol (High-density lipoprotein)
- Thyroid function test
- 12-lead ECG

#### Specialized Investigations

- Chest X-ray: To detect cardiomegaly, heart failure, coarctation of the aorta
- Ambulatory BP recording: To assess borderline or 'white coat' hypertension
- Echocardiogram: To detect or quantify left ventricular hypertrophy
- Renal ultrasound: To detect possible renal disease
- Renal angiography: To detect or confirm renal artery stenosis
- Urinary catecholamines: To detect possible phaeochromocytoma
- Urinary cortisol and dexamethasone suppression test: To detect possible Cushing's syndrome
- Plasma renin activity and aldosterone: To detect possible primary aldosteronism

---

[^1]: **PKD (Polycystic kidney disease)**: Genetics (Autosomal dominant/recessive) disorder where inside kidneys is numerous fluid-filled cysts → Hypertension (earliest), Kidneys [Size↑, Pain, Failure, Stones, UTI]

[^2]: **Coarctation of aorta**: Congenital heart defect where aorta is narrow → hypertension (in older children & adults)
