# Anaemia

> What are the common causes of anemia in Myanmar? How would you investigate a 65-year
> old lady presenting with pallor and dyspnea for one month?

## Common Causes of Anaemia

### 1. Decreased RBC Production

1. Nutritional Deficiencies
   - Iron deficiency anaemia (IDA)
   - Vitamin B12 deficiency (Pernicious anaemia)
   - Folate deficiency
1. Bone Marrow Disorders
   - Aplastic anaemia
   - Myelodysplastic syndrome
   - Leukaemia
1. Chronic Diseases
   - Chronic kidney disease (↓ erythropoietin)
   - Chronic inflammation (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis, tuberculosis)
   - Endocrine disorders (e.g., hypothyroidism)

### 2. Increased RBC Destruction (Haemolysis)

1. Intrinsic RBC Defects
   - Sickle cell disease
   - Thalassemia (alpha & beta)
   - Hereditary spherocytosis
   - G6PD deficiency
1. Extrinsic RBC Destruction
   - Autoimmune haemolytic anaemia (AIHA)
   - Microangiopathic haemolytic anaemia (MAHA) (e.g., DIC, TTP, HUS)
   - Infections (e.g., malaria, _Clostridium perfringens_)
   - Drug-induced haemolysis

### 3. Blood Loss

1. Acute Blood Loss
   - Trauma
   - Gastrointestinal bleeding (e.g., peptic ulcer, varices, colorectal cancer)
   - Obstetric causes (e.g., postpartum haemorrhage)
1. Chronic Blood Loss
   - Heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia)
   - Occult GI bleeding (e.g., colon cancer, hookworm infection)

## Pallor & Dyspnoea for 1 Month Investigations

- Pallor + progressive dyspnoea over weeks → likely **anaemia** (Hb reduction) or chronic cardiopulmonary disease.
- In elderly, **anaemia of chronic disease, iron deficiency (esp. GI blood loss), B12/folate deficiency, or heart failure** are common.

### Objectives

- **First** confirm anaemia (objective lab data).
- **Next** classify (microcytic, normocytic, macrocytic) → narrows differential.
- **Then** find the underlying cause (especially blood loss in elderly, often from GI tract).
- **Finally** check for complications to guide urgency of treatment.

### 1. Initial Clinical Assessment

#### History

- Onset & progression (gradual → chronic; acute → bleeding)
- Associated symptoms: fatigue, palpitations, chest pain, syncope, weight loss, GI symptoms (melena, hematemesis), menstrual history (if premenopausal)
- Risk factors: NSAID use, anticoagulants, chronic illness, malignancy, dietary history
- Family history of haematological disorders

#### Examination

- General: pallor (conjunctiva, tongue, palmar creases), icterus, lymphadenopathy
- Cardiovascular: flow murmurs, tachycardia
- Respiratory: crepitations (heart failure)
- Abdomen: hepatosplenomegaly, masses
- Signs of nutritional deficiency: glossitis, koilonychia, neuropathy

### 2. Investigations

#### A. For Diagnosis – Confirm anaemia & type

1. **Complete blood count (CBC)** → Hb, MCV, MCH, RBC count, WBC, platelets
2. **Peripheral blood smear** → RBC morphology (microcytic, macrocytic, normocytic)
3. **Reticulocyte count** → bone marrow response

#### B. For Causes – Determine aetiology

##### If Microcytic (MCV < 80 fL) → `Iron deficiency` / `thalassaemia`

- Serum ferritin (low in iron deficiency)
- Serum iron, TIBC
- Hb electrophoresis (thalassaemia)

##### If Macrocytic (MCV > 96 fL) → `B12/folate deficiency`, `myelodysplasia`, `alcohol`, `hypothyroidism`

- Serum B12 & folate
- Thyroid function tests
- LFTs (alcohol, liver disease)
- Bone marrow examination if myelodysplasia suspected

##### If Normocytic (MCV normal) → `anaemia of chronic disease`, `haemolysis`, `acute blood loss`

- ESR, CRP (inflammation, malignancy)
- Renal function (chronic kidney disease)
- LDH, indirect bilirubin, haptoglobin, direct Coombs (if haemolysis suspected)

#### C. For Source of Blood Loss (especially in elderly)

- Stool for occult blood (3 samples)
- Upper GI endoscopy & colonoscopy (malignancy, ulcer, angiodysplasia)
- Pelvic ultrasound (if gynaecological cause suspected)

#### D. For Complications

- ECG (tachycardia, strain pattern, ischaemia due to low Hb)
- CXR (heart failure if severe anaemia)

### 3. Summary Table

| Step               | Investigation                                                          | Purpose                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Diagnosis**      | CBC, PBS, reticulocyte count                                           | Confirm anaemia & classify type       |
| **Aetiology**      | Iron studies, B12/folate, TFT, renal/liver function, haemolysis screen | Identify cause                        |
| **Source of loss** | FOBT, endoscopy/colonoscopy, gynaecological US                         | Find bleeding site                    |
| **Complications**  | ECG, CXR                                                               | Detect cardiac/respiratory compromise |
