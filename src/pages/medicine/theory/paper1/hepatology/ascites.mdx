# Ascites

> Accumulation of free fluid in the peritoneal cavity.

import { Tabs } from "nextra/components";

## Clinical Features

- Small amounts are asymptomatics
- Large amounts (> 1L):
  - Abdominal distension
  - Fullness in the flanks
  - Shifting dullness on percussion
  - Fluid thrill present
  - Eversion of the umbilicus
  - Herniae
  - Abdominal striae
  - Divarication of the recti
  - Scrotal oedema
  - Dilated superficial abdominal veins (in portal hypertension)

## Causes

### Low Serum-Ascites Albumin Gradient (SAAG)

- **Malignant disease**:
  - Hepatic
  - Peritoneal
- Acute pancreatitis
- Lymphatic obstruction
- Infection: Tuberculosis
- Nephrotic syndrome
- Protein-losing enteropathy
- Severe malnutrition
- Hypothyroidism (rare)
- Meigs syndrome (rare)

### High SAAG

- **Cirrhosis**
- **Cardiac failure**
- Hepatic venous occlusion:
  - Budd-Chiari syndrome
  - Sinusoidal obstruction syndrome (veno-occlusive disease)
- Constrictive pericarditis

## Investigations

1. **Ultrasound** (best method)
1. **Paracentesis** (if necessary under ultrasound guidance): To obtain ascitic fluid for analysis

Appearance

- **Cirrhosis**: Clear, straw-coloured or light green
- **Malignant disease**: Bloody
- **Infection**: Cloudy
- **Biliary communication**: Heavy bile staining
- **Lymphatic obstruction**: Milky-white (chylous)

Useful Investigations

- Total albumin (plus serum albumin) and protein: To calculate serum-ascites albumin gradient (SAAG)
- Amylase
- Neutrophil count
- Cytology
- Microscopy and culture

> SAAG: \> 11 g/L & Total protein concentration > 30 g/L → Portal hypertension (96%)
> SAAG: < 11g/L → Nephrotic syndrome, Protein-losing enteropathy, Severe malnutrition

<Tabs.Tab>

> Successful treatment relieves discomfort but does not prolong life!

1. Restricting sodium intake
1. Promoting urine output with diuretics
1. If necessary, removing ascites directly by paracentesis

### Sodium Restriction

- Restriction of dietary sodium intake
- Restriction of drugs that promote sodium retention (e.g. `NSAIDs`)

### Diuretics

- `Aldosterone antagonist` (`Spironolactone` 100-400 mg/day) + `Loop diuretics` (`Furosemide` 40-160 mg/day)
- Side effect of `spironolactone` : ~~Painful gynaecomastia~~, ~~Hyperkalaemia~~
- _Alternative to `spironolactone`_: `Amiloride` (5-10 mg/day)
- Use other measures in refractory ascites (diuretic-intolerant or renal impairment)

### Paracentesis

- `Large-volume paracentesis` (First line for refractory ascites)
- `IV colloid (human albumin solution - HAS)` for every 2-3 L of ascites drained
- Drainage interval 6-12 hours to reduce risk of infection
- Can be used an initial therapy or when other treatment fails

### Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Stent Shunt (TIPSS)

- _Alternative to paracentesis_
- Used in patients awaiting liver transplantation

## Complications

1. **Renal failure**
   - Due to dehydration, diuretics, sepsis
1. **Hepato-renal syndrome**
   - Stop `diuretics`, `beta-blockers`, any nephrotoxic drugs (`NSAIDs`, `vasodilators`)
   - `IV albumin + Terlipressin (1-2 mg every 4-6 hours)` or `IV albumin + Midodrine + Octreotide`
   - TIPSS
   - Consider haemodialysis if above treatment fails
1. **Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP)**
   - Treatment: Broad spectrum anti-biotics (`cefotaxime` or `piperacillin/tazobactam`)
   - Prophylactic anti-biotics: `Norfloxacin` or `ciprofloxacin` or `cotrimoxazole`

## Prognosis

- 10%-20% of 5 years survival rate for **ascites due to cirrhosis**
- Mortality at 1 year 50% following **bacterial peritonitis**
