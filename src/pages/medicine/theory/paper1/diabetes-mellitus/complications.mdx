# Diabetes Complications

> Chronic hyperglycaemia causes irreversible damage over time to a number of tissues: particularly the small blood vessels of the retina (retinopathy), the glomerulus (nephropathy) and peripheral nerves (peripheral neuropathy).

### Microvascular

1. **Retinopathy, cataract**
   - Common cause of visual loss
1. **Nephropathy**
   - Renal failure, requirement for dialysis
1. **Peripheral neuropathy (foot disease)**
   - Sensory loss
   - Pain
   - Ulceration
   - Charcot neuroarthropathy
1. **Autonomic neuropathy**
   - Postural hypotension
   - Gastroparesis
   - Sexual dysfunction

### Macrovascular

1. **Coronary circulation**
   - Angina
   - Acute coronary syndrome/myocardial infarction
1. **Cerebral circulation**
   - Transient ischaemic attack (TIA)
   - Stroke
1. **Peripheral circulation (foot disease)**
   - Claudication
   - Ischaemia
   - Ulceration

### Skin

- Diabetic dermopathy
- Necrobiosis lipoidica
- Xerosis

### Infection

- Increased risk of infectio, e.g. _Candida spp._, urine infection, fungal nail disease
- Increased risk of severe forms of infection, e.g. COVID-19, influenza, tuberculosis
- Increased risk of opportunistic infections, e.g. emphysematous pyelonephritis

### Rheumatological

- Cheiroarthropathy, limited joint movement
- Dupuytren's contracture
- Adhesive capsulitis
- Trigger finger
