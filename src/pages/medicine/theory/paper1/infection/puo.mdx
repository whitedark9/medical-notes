# Pyrexia of Unknown Origin (PUO)

> A temperature > 38.0Â°C on multiple occasions for > 3 weeks, without diagnosis, despite investigation over 3 days of inpatient care, three outpatient vists or 1 week of intensive ambulatory investigation.

## Aetiology

1. Infections (~30%)
1. Malignancy (~20%)
1. Connective tissue disorders (~15%)
1. Miscellaneous (~20%)
1. Idiopathic (~15%)

### Infections

1. Specific locations
   - Abscesses: Lung, Liver, Brain, Urinary tract
   - Infections of oral cavity, head and neck, bone and joint
   - Infective endocarditis
1. Specific organisms
   - **TB**, HIV, CMV, EBV, Fungal infections (_Aspergillus spp._, _Candida spp._)
1. Specific patient groups
   - Recently spent time in a region with geographically restricted infection: **Malaria**, Dengue, Enteric fever
   - Residence in or travel to a region with endemic infection: **TB**, XDR-TB, HIV-1
   - Nosocomial infection[^1]: **Pneumonia**, UTI, Central venous catheter infection, Infections from medical procedures
   - HIV-positive individuals: Acute retroviral syndrome, AIDS-defining infections (Disseminated _Mycobacterium avium_ complex, Pneumocystic _jrovecii_ pneumonia, CMV, etc.)

### Malignancy

1. Haematological malignancy:
   - **Lymphoma**, Leukaemia, myeloma
1. Solid tumors:
   - Kidney, Liver, Colon, Stomach, Pancreas

### Connective Tissue Disorders

1. Older adults:
   - **Temporal arteritis/polymyalgia rheumatica**
1. Younger adults:
   - **Juvenile rheumatoid arthritis**, SLE, Vasculitis, Rheumatic fever

### Miscellaneous

1. **Drug reactions**:
   - Antibiotic fever, Drug hypersensitivity reactions, etc.
1. Cardiovascular:
   - Atrial myxoma, Aortitis, Aortic dissection
1. Respiratory:
   - Sarcoidosis, Pulmonary embolism
1. Gastrointestinal:
   - Inflammatory bowel disease, Granulomatous hepatitis, Alcoholic liver disease, Pancreatitis
1. Endocrine/metabolic:
   - Thyrotoxicosis, Thyroditis, Adrenal insufficiency
1. Haematological:
   - Haemolytic anaemia, Paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria, thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura
1. Inherit:
   - Familial Mediterranean fever, Periodic fever syndromes
1. Factitious fever

## Investigations

### Location-independent Investigations

1. Microscropy
   - Blood (for atypical lymphocytes, EBV, CMV, HIV)
   - Respiratory samples (for mycobacteria and fungi )
   - Stool (for ova, cysts and parasites)
   - Biopsy (for bacteria, mycobacteria, fungi, virus, protozoa)
   - Urine (for blood cells and mycobacteria)
1. Culture
   - Aspirates and biopsies
   - Blood
   - Sputum
   - CSF
   - Gastric aspirate
   - Stool
   - Swabs
   - Urine
1. Antigen detection
   - Blood (for HIV, cryptococcal antigen, etc.)
   - CSF (for cryptococcal antigen)
   - Bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (for _Aspergillus_ galactomannan)
   - Nasopharyngeal aspirate/throat swab (for respiratory viruses)
   - Urine (for _Legionella_ antigen, etc.)
1. Nucleic acid detection
   - Blood (for _Bartonella spp._, viruses)
   - CSF (for viruses and meningococcus, pneumococcus, _Listeria monocytogenes_)
   - Nasopharyngeal aspirate/thraot swab (for respiratory viruses)
   - Sputum (for _Mycobacterium tuberculosis_, geneXpert, NAAT)
   - Bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (for respiratory viruses)
   - Tissue specimens (for _T. whipplei_)
   - Urine (for _Chlamydia trachomatis_, _Neisseria gonorrhoeae_)
   - Stool (for norovirus, rotavirus)
1. Immunological tests
   - Serology (antibody detection) (for viruses, HIV)
   - Interferon-gamma release assay (IGRA) (for TB)

### Geographically Restricted Tests

1. Microscropy
   - Blood (for trypanosomiasis, malaria, _Borrelia spp._)
   - Stool (for ova, cysts, parasites)
   - Biopsy (for dimorphic fungi, _Leishmania spp._ and other parasites)
   - Urine (for RBCs and schistosome ova)
1. Antigen detection
   - Blood (for dengue, _Histoplasma_ antigen, malaria)
1. Nucleic acoid detection
   - Blood (for viral haemorrhagic fever)
   - CSF (for JE)
   - Nasopharyngeal aspirate/throat swab or bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (for MERS-CoV)
1. Immunological tests
   - Serology (antibody detection) (for viruses, dimorphic fungi, protozoa)

### Additional Investigations

1. Serological tests for connective tissue disordrs
1. Ferritin
1. Echocardiography
1. Ultrasound abdomen
1. CT/MRI of thorax, abdomen, brain
1. Imaging of skeletal system: Plain X-rays, CT/MRI, Isotope bone scan
1. Labelled white cell scan
1. Fluorodeoxyglucose-position emission tomography (FDG-PET)/Single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT)
1. Biopsy and Lumbar puncture

## Prognosis

- No cause is found in up to one-half of PUO cases
- If no significant weight loss or signs of another disease, long-term motality is low

---

[^1]: **Nosocomial infection**: An infection that develops while a patient is in a healthcare facility
