# Acute Renal Injury (AKI)

> The situation where there is a sudden and often reversible loss of renal function, which develops over days or weeks and is often accompanied by a reduction in urine volume.

import { Tabs } from "nextra/components";

## Causes

<details>
<summary>Pathophysiology</summary>

1. Pre-renal: When perfusion to the kidney is reduced.
1. Renal: When the primary insult affects the kidney itself
1. Post-renal: When there is obstruction to urine flow at any point from the tubule to the urethra.

</details>

### Pre-renal

- Impaired perfusion:
  - Cardiac failure
  - Sepsis
  - Blood loss
  - Dehydration
  - Vascular occlusion

### Renal

- Glomerulonephritis
- Small-vessel vasculitis
- Acute tubular necrosis
  - Drugs
  - Toxins
  - Prolonged hypotension
- Interstitial nephritis
  - Drugs
  - Toxins
  - Inflammatory disease
  - Infection, including COVID-19

### Post-renal

- Urinary calculi (bilateral)
- Retroperitoneal fibrosis
- Benign prostatic hypertrophy (BPH)
- Bladder cancer
- Prostate cancer
- Cervical cancer
- Urethral stricture/valves
- Meatal stenosis/phimosis

### Pre-renal [#CF]

1. **History**
   - Volume depletion (vomiting, diarrhoea, burns, haemorrhage)
   - Sepsis
   - Cardiac disease
   - Liver disease
   - Drugs (`diuretics`, `ACE inhibitors`, `ARBs`, `NSAIDs`, `calcineurin inhibitors`, `iodinated contrast`)
1. **Examination**
   - Low BP relative to normal for patient (including postural drop)
   - Tachycardia
   - Weight decrease
   - Dry mucous membranes
   - Decreased skin turgor
   - JVP not visible even when lying down
1. **Investigations**
   - Urine Na+ < 20 mmol/L
   - Fractional excretion Na+ < 1%
   - High serum urea: Creatinine ratio
   - Urinalysis bland

### Renal [#CF]

#### 1. ATN

1. **History**
   - Ischaemic injury due to severe or prolonged pre-renal state
   - Toxic ATN: drugs (`aminoglycosides`, `cisplatin`, `tenofovir`, `methotrexate`, `iodinated contrast`)
   - Other (rhabdomyolysis, snake bite, Amanita mushrooms)
1. **Examination**
   - Vital signs
   - Fluid assessment
   - Limbs for compartment syndrome
1. **Investigations**
   - Urine Na+ > 40 mmol/L
   - Fractional excretion Na â‰¥ 1%
   - Dense granular ('muddy brown') casts
   - Creatine kinase

#### 2. Glomerular

1. **History**
   - Rash, weight loss, arthralgia, ENT and chest symptoms (pulmonary renal syndromes)
   - IV drug use
   - Recent infection
1. **Examination**
   - Hypertension
   - Oedema
   - Purpuric rash, uveitis, arthritis
1. **Investigations**
   - Proteinuria, haematuria
   - Red cell casts, dysmorphic red cells
   - ANCA, anti-GBM, ANA, C3 and C4
   - Viral hepatitis screen, HIV
   - Renal biopsy

#### 3. Tubulo-Interstitial

1. **History**
   - Interstitial nephritis: drugs (`PPIs`, `Penicillins`, `NSAIDs`)
   - Sarcoidosis
   - Tubular obstruction:
   - Myeloma (cast nephropathy)
   - Tubular crystal nephropathy:
   - Drugs (`aciclovir`, `indinavir`, `triamterene`, `methotrexate`)
   - Oxalate (fat malabsorption, ethylene glycol)
   - Urate (tumour lysis)
1. **Examination**
   - Fever
   - Rash
1. **Investigations**
   - Leucocyturia
   - White cell casts
   - Minimal proteinuria
   - Paraprotein, Bence, Jones protein
   - Calcium (Myeloma, Sarcoidosis)
   - Urine microscopy for crystals
   - Serum urate
   - Urine collection for oxalate

#### 4. Vascular

1. **History**
   - Flank pain, trauma
   - Anticoagulation
   - Recent angiography (cholesterol emboli)
   - Nephrotic syndrome (renal vein thrombosis)
   - Systemic sclerosis (renal crisis)
   - Diarrhoea (HUS - Haemolyt Uraemic Syndrome)
1. **Examination**
   - Hypertension
   - Hypertensive changes on fundoscopy
   - Livedo reticularis (cholesterol emboli)
   - Sclerodactyly
1. **Investigations**
   - Normal urinalysis or some haematuria
   - CT angiography
   - Doppler renal ultrasound
   - C3 and C4 (cholesterol emboli)
   - Platelets, haemolytic screen, LDH (HUS)
   - Consider ADAMTS13 and complement genetics (if TMA)

### Post-renal [#CF]

1. **History**
   - Bladder outlet symptoms
   - History of BPH or prostate, bladder or cervical cancer
   - Retroperitoneal fibrosis
   - Neurogenic bladder
1. **Examination**
   - Rectal examination (prostate and anal tone)
   - Distended bladder
   - Pelvic mass
1. **Investigations**
   - Urinalysis frequently normal (may reveal haematuria depending on cause)
   - Renal ultrasound (hydronephrosis)
   - Isotope renogram (delayed excretion) if ultrasound inconclusive

## Management

<Tabs items={['Simple', 'Details']}>
<Tabs.Tab>

1. **Assess fluid status**:
   1. If _hypovolaemic_: Optimise systemic haemodynamic status with fluid and `inotropes` if necessary
   1. If _euvolaemic_: Match fluid intake to urine output + Additional 500 mL/24hrs for insensible losses
   1. If _fluid-overloaded_: Diuretics (loop diuretics high dose); Dialysis if unsatisfactory response
1. **Treat underlying cause**
1. If _K+ > 6.5 mmol/L and ECG changes of hyperkalaemia_:
   - `Calcium gluconate` to stablise myocardium,
   - `Oral potassium exchange resin` to prevent K+ absorption **or** `IV glucose/insulin or Sodium bicarbonate` to move K+ intracelluarly
1. **Discontinue potentially nephrotoxic drugs** and reduce doses of therapeutic drugs according to level of renal function
1. Ensure **adequate nutritional support**
1. Consider PPIs to **reduce the risk of upper GI bleeding**
1. Screen for **intercurrent infections** and treat promptly if present

</Tabs.Tab>
<Tabs.Tab>

### Fluid Volume

- **Regular assessment of volume status**
  - Consider nvasive monitoring of central venous pressure if assessment is difficulty such as in obesity
- **Hypovolaemia**:
  - `IV fluid or blood`
  - If large volume is needed, `balanced crystalloid solutions` (`Plasma-Lyte` or `Hartmann's`) is better than isotonic saline (0.9% NaCl) (To avoid hypochloraemic acidosis)
  - `Inotropes` in relative intravascular volume depletion due to vasodilation (sepsis or liver failure)
- **Volume Replete or Euvolaemia**:
  - `Fluid intake = Urine output + 500 mL per day` (to cover insensible losses, unless additional losses such as diarrhoea or high nasogastric aspirates)
- **Fluid Overload**:
  - `Diuretics` (may need high dose)
  - `Dialysis` in pulmonary oedema

### Treatment of Underlying Cause

- Identify and manage underlying causes (blood loss, sepsis, cardiac events)
- **In suspected ATN**:
  - Optimise volume status
  - Discontinue offending drugs
  - Identify and treat conditions (spesis, compartment syndrome, myeloma)
  - Support with renal replacement therapy during recovery time (days to weeks)
- **In post-renal AKI**:
  - Relieve obstruction stats
  - `Urinary catheterisation` for bladder outflow obstruction
  - `Ureteric stent` or `percutaneous nephrostomies` for ureteric obstruction

### Electrolyte and Acid-Base Disturbances

1. **Hyperkalaemia**:
   - Rhabdomyolysis, burns, haemolysis, metabolic acidosis, ACE inhibitors, ARBs
   - If _K+ > 6.5 mmol/L and ECG changes of hyperkalaemia_:
     - `Calcium gluconate` to stablise myocardium,
     - `Oral potassium exchange resin` to prevent K+ absorption **or** `IV glucose/insulin or Sodium bicarbonate` to move K+ intracelluarly
1. **Severe acidosis**:
   - Sodium bicarbonate (may help to lower serum K+)
1. **Dilutional hyponatraemia**:
   - Due to drink freely despite oliguria or high IV dextrose
   - Treatment: `Fluid restriction`
1. **Modest hypocalcaemia**:
   - Rarely requires treatment
1. **Phosphate**:
   - High
   - Fall in daily or continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT); Need phosphate replacement

### Dietary Measures

- Adequate calories and protein intake (especially for sepsis and burns)
- Use enteral or parenteral if necessary

### Infection

- Treat according to standard principles if infection is discovered (sepsis)

### Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT)

- If above measures fail, use RRT
- Two main options in AKI:
  1.  `Intermittent haemodialysis` (if unavailable, `peritoneal dialysis`)
  1.  `Continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT)`

## Recovery

- Reduction of urine output and serum creatinine
- **Diuretic phase**:
  - Urine output increases rapidly, returns to normal after several days
- Provide supplement fluid and potassium
- If necessary, provide calcium, phosphate and magnesium
