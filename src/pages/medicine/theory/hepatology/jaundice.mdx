# Jaundice

> Jaundice is usually detectable clincally when the plasma bilirubin exceeds 40 μmol/L (\~2.5mg/L).

## Causes

### Pre-Hepatic

- Haemolysis
- Congenital hyperbilirubinaemia
  - Unconjugated: Gilbert, Crigler-Najjar Type I, II
  - Conjugated: Dubin-Johnson, Rotor

### Hepatic Causes of Cholestatic Jaundice

1. Primary biliary cholangitis
1. Primary sclerosing cholangitis
1. Alcohol
1. Drugs
1. Hepatic infiltrations (lymphoma, granuloma, amyloid, metastases)
1. Cystic fibrosis
1. Sepsis
1. Pregnancy
1. Inherited cholestatic liver disease (e.g., benign recurrent intrahepatic cholestasis)
1. Right heart failure

### Post-Hepatic Causes of Cholestatic Jaundice

1. Carcinoma:
   - Ampullary
   - Pancreatic
   - Bile duct (cholangiocarcinoma)
   - Liver metastases
1. Parasitic infection
1. IgG4 cholangitis
1. Traumatic biliary strictures
1. Choledocholithiasis
1. Primary sclerosing cholangitis
1. Chronic pancreatitis

## Clinical Features and Complications

1. Jaundice
1. Abdominal pain
1. Cholangitis
1. Abdominal scar
1. Irregular hepatomegaly
1. Palpable gallbladder
1. Abdominal mass
1. Occult blood in stools

### Cholestatic Jaundice

1. Cholestasis
   - Early:
     - Jaundice
     - Dark urine
     - Pale stools
     - Pruritus
   - Late:
     - Malabsorption (vitamins A,D,E,K)
     - Weight loss
     - Steatorrhoea
     - Osteomalacia
     - Bleeding tendency
     - Xanthelasma and xanthomas
1. Cholangitis
   - Fever
   - Rigors
   - Pain (if gallstones present)
