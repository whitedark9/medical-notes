# Meningitis

## Causes

### Infective

#### 1. Viruses

1. **Enteroviruses** (echo, Coxsackie, polio)
1. **Mumps**
1. Measles
1. Herpes simplex virus type 1, 2
1. Varicella zoster virus
1. Epstein-Barr virus
1. Cytomegalovirus
1. Human immunodeficiency virus

#### 2. Bacteria

1. _Streptococcus pneumoniae_
1. _Neisseria meningitidis_
1. _Mycobacterium tuberculosis_
1. _Haemophilus influenzae_
1. _Listeria monocytogenes_
1. Other _streptococci_ including _Strep. suis_
1. _Staphylococcus aureus_ (skull fracture)

#### 3. Fungi

1. _Cryptococcus neoformans_
1. _Candida_
1. _Histoplasma_
1. _Blastomyces_
1. _Cocidioides_
1. _Sporothrix_

### Non-Infective

#### 1. Malignant Disease

> Beast-Bronchus-Blood

1. Breast cancer
1. Bronchogenic cancer
1. Leukaemia
1. Lymphoma

#### 2. Inflammatory Disease

1. Sarcoidosis
1. Systemic lupus erythematosus
1. Behcet's disease

### Viral Meningitis [#CF]

- **Headache**
- **Irritability**
- Rapid development of meningism
- High pyrexia
- Focal neurological signs (rare)

### Bacterial Meningitis [#CF]

- **Fever**
- **Headache**
- Meningism
- Altered consciousness, drowsiness
- Rash (meningococcal meningitis)
- Seizure, coma (severe)
- Recurrent fever, sweating, joint pain, transient rash (chronic meningococcaemia)
- Otitis media (pneumococcal and _Haemophilus_ infections)
- Pneumonia (pneumococcal meningitis; in older patients, alcoholics, hyposplenism)
- Brainstem encephalitis(rhombencephalitis) (_Listeria monocytogenes_; in immunosuppressed, neonates and old age, diabetes, alcoholics, pregnant women)

### TB Meningitis [#CF]

<details>
<summary>Staging of severity of TB meningitis</summary>

1. **Stage I (early)**: Non-specific symptoms and signs without alteration of consciousness
1. **Stage II (intermediate)**: Altered consciousness without coma or delirium + Minor focal neurological signs
1. **Stage III (advanced)**: Stupor or coma, severe neurological deficits, seizures or abnormal movements

## Investigations

1. Lumbar puncture
1. Polymerase chain reaction (PCR)
1. Neucleic acid amplification test (NAAT)
1. Xpert MT/RIF Ultra (in TB meningitis)
1. Brain imaging (CT, MRI): To detect hydrocephalus

### CSF Analysis in Different Types of Meningitis

| Parameter              | Viral                                    | Bacterial                                        | Tuberculous                                                                    | Fungal                                                           |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Opening Pressure**   | Normal or mildly elevated                | Elevated                                         | Elevated                                                                       | Elevated                                                         |
| **Color**              | Clear                                    | Cloudy or purulent                               | Clear or Fine clot ('spider web')                                              | Clear to slightly cloudy                                         |
| **WBC Count**          | Elevated (10-500 cells/µL), lymphocytes  | Elevated (100-10,000 cells/µL), neutrophils      | Elevated (10-500 cells/µL), Predominantly lymphocytes (with small neutrophils) | Elevated (10-500 cells/µL), lymphocytes                          |
| **Protein**            | Normal or mildly elevated (50-100 mg/dL) | Markedly elevated (>100 mg/dL)                   | Elevated (100-500 mg/dL)                                                       | Elevated (100-500 mg/dL)                                         |
| **Glucose**            | Normal                                   | Decreased (< 40 mg/dL or < 2/3 of serum glucose) | Decreased (< 45 mg/dL)                                                         | Decreased (< 45 mg/dL)                                           |
| **Culture**            | Negative                                 | Positive for bacterial pathogens                 | Positive for _Mycobacterium tuberculosis_                                      | Positive for fungal pathogens (e.g., _Cryptococcus_)             |
| **Gram Stain**         | Negative                                 | Positive in most cases                           | Negative                                                                       | Negative                                                         |
| **PCR/Specific Tests** | Positive for viral DNA/RNA               | Not commonly used                                | Positive for _M. tuberculosis_ DNA                                             | Positive for fungal DNA or antigens (e.g., Cryptococcal antigen) |

<details>
<summary>Investigation of meningitis based on the British Infection Association acute meningitis guideline</summary>

1. Resusciate and stablise patient
1. Initial tests
   1. Blood cultures
   1. Meningococcal and pneumococcal PCR
   1. Throat swab for Enterovirus PCR if viral meningitis likely
   1. Full blood count
   1. Urea, creatinine, electrolytes
   1. Glucose
   1. Liver function tests and clotting screen
   1. Procalcitonin or CRP
   1. Serology sample
1. Immediate management
   1. Assess GCS: within the first hour after arrival
   1. Assess need for intensive care
   1. Perform LP if no contraindication
   1. If LP cannot be performed immediately, start empiric treatment after blood cultures taken and within the first hour

> If predominantly sepsis or rapidly evolving rash, give antibiotics immediately after blood cultures, follow sepsis guideline and postphone LP

</details>

<details>
<summary>Contraindications for immediate lumbar puncture</summary>

1. Signs of severe sepsis or rapidly evolving rash
1. Anti-coagulant therapy/known thrombocytopenia
1. Respiratory or cardiac compromise
1. Focal neurological signs
1. Papilloedema (inability to see the fundus is not a contraindication)
1. Continuous or uncontrolled siezures
1. GCS ≤ 12

> Once the patient is stable ± sepsis, an LP may still be diagnostically useful even after several days.

- If _4,5,6,7 are present_: CT brain → No mass lesion, hydrocephalus or other contraindication to LP → Lumbar puncture
- If ~~absent~~: Lumbar puncture

</details>

## Management

### Viral Meningitis [#MG]

- No specific treatment
- Self-limiting
- Recovery within days but lymphocytic pleocytosis [^1] persists in CSF

### Bacterial Meningitis [#MG]

- Meningococcal meningitis: **around 7 days**
- Pneumococcal meningitis treatment: **10-14 days** duration
- _Listeria meningitidis_: **21 days**

<details>
<summary>Treatment of bacterial meningitis of unknown cause (based on the British Infection Association Guideline 2016)</summary>

1. Adults aged < 60 years: `Cefotaxim` or `Ceftriaxone`
1. If penicillin-resistant pneumococcal infection is suspected: **1** + (`Vancomycin` or `Rifampicin`)
1. Age > 60 and _Listeria monocytogenes_ is suspected: **1** + (`Ampicillin` or `Amoxicillin`)
1. **2** + **3** (Age > 60 + Suspected _Listeria monocytogenes_ + Penicillin-resistant pneumococcal infection): **2** + **3**
1. Clear history of anaphylaxis to β-lactams: `Chloramphenicol` + `Vancomycin` (Add `Co-trimoxazole` if age > 60)
1. Adjunctive treatment: `Dexamethasone` (To reduce hearing loss and neurological sequelae in both children and adults in high-income countries)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Chemotherapy of bacterial meningitis when the cause is known (based on the British Infection Association Guideline 2016)</summary>

| Pathogen                                         | Regimen of choice                | Alternative agents                      |
| :----------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------- |
| _Neisseria meningitidis_                         | `Cefotaxime` or `Ceftriaxone`    | `Benzylpenicillin` or `Chloramphenicol` |
| _Streptococcus pneumoniae_ (β-lactams sensitive) | `Cefotaxime` or `Ceftriaxone`    | `Chloramphenicol`                       |
| _Strep. pneumoniae_ (β-lactams resistant)        | Add `Vancomycin` or `Rifampicin` | `Chloramphenicol`                       |
| _Haemophilus influenzae_                         | `Cefotaxime` or `Ceftriaxone`    | `Moxifloxacin`                          |
| _Streptococcus suis_                             | `Cefotaxime` or `Ceftriaxone`    | `Chloramphenicol`                       |
| _Listeria monocytogenes_                         | `Amoxicillin`                    | `Co-trimoxazole`                        |

</details>

### TB Meningitis [#MG]

1. Chemotherapy (Anti-TB treatment)
   1. Intensive phase (2 months): `HRZE`
   1. Continuation phase (7-10 months): `HR`
1. Glucocorticoids
