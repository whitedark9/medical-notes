# Tuberculosis

- Causative Organism

  - _Mycobacterium tuberculosis_

    - Acid-fast bacillus (AFB)
    - Obligate aerobe, non-motile, slow-growing
    - Cell wall contains mycolic acid → acid-fastness
    - **Human TB**: usually caused by _M. tuberculosis_
    - **Zoonotic TB** (rare): _M. bovis_

- Incubation Period

  - **2–12 weeks** (average: 4–8 weeks)

- Pathogenesis

  - Inhalation of aerosolized bacilli → alveolar macrophages → Primary complex (Ghon focus + lymph node) → Latent TB or progression → Reactivation (if immunosuppressed or malnourished) → Pulmonary or extrapulmonary TB

- Staining and Culture

  1. **Ziehl-Neelsen stain** → Acid-fast bacilli (pink rods)
  1. **Lowenstein-Jensen medium** → Slow growth (2–8 weeks)
  1. **GeneXpert MTB/RIF** → Rapid molecular diagnosis

## History Suggestive of Childhood TB

- Age < 5 years
- Fever:
  - Lowe grade (38°C)
  - Evening rise in temperature
  - Loss of weight, Loss of appetite
  - Night sweating
- Cough:
  - \> 3 weeks duration
  - with blood-stained sputum
- Failure to gain weight or weight loss
- Close TB contact
- Previous TB treatment
- Low immune status:
  - HIV infection
  - Immunosuppressive drugs such as steroids
  - Severe malnutrition
  - Illness
- no BCG vaccination
- Poor ventilation and overcrowded

## Physical Examination Suggestive of Childhood TB

### General Physical Signs

1. **Failure to Thrive / Weight Loss**

   - Child not gaining weight despite adequate feeding
   - Weight below 3rd percentile or falling growth curve

2. **Fever**

   - Low-grade, prolonged (>2 weeks), typically in evenings

3. **Night Sweats**

   - Suggestive in the absence of other infections

4. **Lethargy / Irritability / Anorexia**

   - Nonspecific but concerning with other TB features

5. **Pallor**

   - Due to chronic disease or anemia of inflammation

### Respiratory Examination

1. **Persistent Cough (>2 weeks)**

   - Dry or productive, may be unresponsive to antibiotics

2. **Tachypnea / Respiratory Distress**

   - Especially in progressive primary pulmonary TB

3. **Reduced Air Entry / Crackles**

   - Consolidation or collapse due to lymph node compression

4. **Mediastinal Shift / Tracheal Deviation**

   - Due to large hilar lymphadenopathy or effusion

5. **Signs of Pleural Effusion**

   - Stony dullness, decreased breath sounds

### Neurological Signs (in TB Meningitis)

1. **Signs of meningisms**
   - **Neck Stiffness**
   - **Kernig's sign**
   - **Brudzinski's sign**
2. **Bulging Fontanelle** (in infants)
3. **Cranial Nerve Palsies**
4. **Altered Consciousness, Seizures**
5. **Signs of Raised ICP**

### Musculoskeletal TB

1. **Spinal TB (Pott's disease)**

   - Gibbus deformity (sharp angular kyphosis)
   - Local tenderness, restricted spinal movement

2. **Joint TB**

   - Monoarthritis (hip or knee), cold swelling, limited motion

### Lymphadenopathy

1. **Cervical or Supraclavicular Lymph Nodes**

   - Non-tender, matted, may fistulate
   - Caseating lymphadenitis

### Other Relevant Clinical Findings

- **BCG scar absent**: raises suspicion in unvaccinated child
- **Contact with known TB case**: especially household adult with cough

## Investigations in Childhood TB

### 1. For Diagnosis

- **Tuberculin Skin Test (Mantoux)**

  - Positive if induration ≥10 mm (≥5 mm in immunocompromised)

- **Chest X-ray**

  - Hilar lymphadenopathy, consolidation, miliary pattern, pleural effusion

- **Gastric Aspirate or Induced Sputum for AFB**

  - 3 early morning samples (as children \<5 can't expectorate well)

- **GeneXpert MTB/RIF (on sputum, gastric aspirate, CSF)**

  - Detects TB DNA and rifampicin resistance

- **Bronchoalveolar Lavage (BAL)**

  - In selected hospitalised or diagnostic uncertainty cases

- **Lymph Node Biopsy/FNAC**

  - In TB lymphadenitis — caseating granuloma, AFB, or GeneXpert

- **CSF Analysis**

  - In suspected TB meningitis: ↑ protein, ↓ glucose, lymphocytic pleocytosis

- **Urine AFB / PCR**

  - If suspected genitourinary TB

### 2. For Causes

- **HIV Testing (Rapid test or ELISA)**
- **Nutritional assessment**

  - Weight for age, MUAC, height

- **Contact tracing**

  - TB history in household or recent exposure

- **BCG scar status**

### 3. For Complications

- **Neuroimaging (CT/MRI)**

  - In suspected TB meningitis or TB abscess

- **Liver Function Tests**

  - Baseline before hepatotoxic therapy

- **CBC, ESR**

  - Supportive findings (not diagnostic)

### 4. Tests for Extrapulmonary TB

| Disease                     | Special Investigation                          |
| :-------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- |
| Cervical/other lymph glands | Biopsy/ Fine needle aspiration cytology (FNAC) |
| TB Meningitis               | Lumbar puncture, CT brain scan                 |
| TB Arthritis                | Aspiration, Biopsy                             |
| TB Abdomen/ascites          | Ultrasound, Aspiration                         |
| TB Vertebra                 | Vertebral X-ray                                |

## Treatment

### Recommended Treatment Regimens

Two phases:

1. **Intensive phase**: To rapidly eliminate the majority of organisms and to prevent the emergence of drug resistance
2. **Continuation phase**: To eradicate the dormant organisms.
