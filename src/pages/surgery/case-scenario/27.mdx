# Case 27

> A middle-aged lady consulted a general surgeon for a slow growing painless lump below her left ear. On examination from behind, the ear lobule was displaced. There was no facial palsy.

<details>
<summary>From what structure is the lump derived?</summary>

Left parotid gland

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the most probable diagnosis?</summary>

Pleomorphic adenoma of left parotid gland

</details>

<details>
<summary>Give differential diagnosis.</summary>

- Parotid cancer
- Warthin tumour

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the surgical treatment for your diagnosis?</summary>

Superficial conservative parotidectomy (Facial nerve preserving)

</details>
