# Case 06

> A 30 year old lady came to ERC for jaundice with pale stools of recent onset. History and examination is not relevant except for mild pyrexia.

<details>
<summary>Arrange relevant lab tests for this girl.</summary>

- Liver function test (ALT, AST, ALP)
- Total and differential bilirubin

</details>

<details>
<summary>What initial imaging would you order? Give reasons.</summary>

- Ultrasound (abdomen): Stone, biliary tree dilatation, thickness of gall bladder wall

</details>

<details>
<summary>Give 2 common causes for this condition at this age.</summary>

1. Stones in Common bile duct (CBD)
1. Worms in CBD

</details>

<details>
<summary>Name one treatment option for any of above two causes.</summary>

ERCP (Endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography) and stone removal

</details>
