# Case 04

> A 85 year old man was admitted for vomiting of fermented emals and drinks of 1 month duration. He looks emaciated with a defnite mass in epigastrium.

<details>
<summary>What is your diagnosis?</summary>

Gastric outlet obstruction (GOO) due to Ca stomach

</details>

<details>
<summary>Any differentials?</summary>

1. Chronic duodenal ulcer with duodenal stenosis
1. Cancer of transverse colon
1. Chronic gastric ulcer with hourglass deformity

</details>

<details>
<summary>What nutritional support will you choose for this patient?</summary>

- Total parenteral nutrition (TPN)
- (Enteral nutrition: Feeding jejunostomy can be used)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Name possible surgical options for your diagnosis. (any two)</summary>

1. Curative treatment:
   - `Total or subtotal gastrectomy`
1. Palliative treatment:
   - `PGGJ` (Partial gastrectomy & gastrecto-jejunostomy) (Bypass surgery)

</details>
