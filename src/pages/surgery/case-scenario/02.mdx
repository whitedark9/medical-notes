# Case 02

> An elderly male was admitted for one day history of continuous upper abdominal pain with no other symptoms except absolute constipation and decreased urine output. His abdomen is rigid.

<details>
<summary>What is your provisional diagnosis?</summary>

Duodenal ulcer perforation

</details>

<details>
<summary>Give 2 differential diagnosis.</summary>

1. Acute pancreatitis
1. Acute cholecystitis

</details>

<details>
<summary>How would you confirm the diagnosis?</summary>

Plain X-ray abdomen (erect): Gas under diaphragm

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the surgical treatment for your diagnosis?</summary>

- Laprotomy through upper midline incision
- Suturing omentoplasty
- Peritoneal toilet & Drain

</details>
