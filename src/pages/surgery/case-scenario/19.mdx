# Case 19

> A 10 year old boy fell onto the ground with his outstretching right hand. There was a rapid swelling around his elbow with pain.

<details>
<summary>What sort of fracture do you expect?</summary>

Supracondylar fracture of right elbow

</details>

<details>
<summary>What anatomical structures are at risk around elbow?</summary>

1. Brachial, radial and ulnar arteries
1. Median and ulnar nerves

</details>

<details>
<summary>What X-ray will confirm your diagnosis?</summary>

X-ray of both elbows (AP and Lateral View)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Outline the treatment.</summary>

- Non-displaced: Arm-sling or POP (Plaster of Paris) posterior slab x 3 weeks
- Displaced: Open reduction under GA and internal fixation (Kirschner's wire)

</details>
