# Case 28

> A major trauma patient receiving blood transfusion complained of bilateral loin pain and light headedness. His vitals were stable.

<details>
<summary>What is the possible complication of blood transfusion you should consider?</summary>

Acute haemolytic transfusion reaction

</details>

<details>
<summary>Give any other 3 complications of blood transfusion.</summary>

1. Transfusion related acute lung injury (TRALI)
1. Transfusion associated circulatory overload (TACO)
1. Transfusion transmitted infection (TTI)
1. Febrile non-haemolytic transfusion reaction

</details>

<details>
<summary>Outline management for your answer to Q1.</summary>

- ~~Stop transfusion immediately~~
- Give basic life support if required
- Keep the bag and giving set for analysis; Inform haematology
- Give `crystalloid` and `furosemide` to encourage diuresis
- `Dialysis` may be required

</details>
