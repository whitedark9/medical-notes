# Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO)

## Causes

| Group         | Causes                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Benign**    | Chronic peptic ulcer disease (pyloric channel, duodenum) <br/> Ingestion of caustics <br/> Chronic pancreatitis with inflammatory mass <br/> Crohn’s disease (duodenum) <br/> Bezoar, gastric polyp |
| **Malignant** | Carcinoma of stomach (antrum, pylorus) <br/> Carcinoma of pancreas (head) <br/> Lymphoma involving stomach/duodenum <br/> Ampullary carcinoma                                                       |

## Fluid And Electrolyte Changes

### Mechanism Of Change

1. **Persistent vomiting** of gastric contents → loss of **HCl** and NaCl from stomach.
2. Loss of acid → **metabolic alkalosis** (due to loss of hydrogen ions).
3. Kidney compensates for alkalosis by excreting bicarbonate → loss of Na⁺ and K⁺ in urine.
4. **Hypovolaemia** from fluid loss → secondary hyperaldosteronism → Na⁺ reabsorption in exchange for K⁺ and H⁺ loss in urine → worsens **hypokalaemia** and alkalosis.
5. **Chloride depletion** from gastric losses → kidneys retain bicarbonate (since Cl⁻ is required for bicarbonate excretion), maintaining alkalosis.

### Typical Changes

| Parameter        | Change               | Reason                                                          |
| ---------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fluid status** | Hypovolaemia         | Repeated vomiting and inability to take oral fluids             |
| **Na⁺**          | Low (hyponatraemia)  | Loss in vomitus + renal bicarbonate–Na loss                     |
| **K⁺**           | Low (hypokalaemia)   | Vomiting + renal loss from hyperaldosteronism                   |
| **Cl⁻**          | Low (hypochloraemia) | Loss in vomitus                                                 |
| **Acid–base**    | Metabolic alkalosis  | Loss of gastric H⁺ ions, Cl⁻ depletion prevents HCO₃⁻ excretion |
| **Urea**         | Increased            | Pre-renal azotaemia from dehydration                            |
