# Tetanus

> A neurological disorder caused by tetanospasmin, a neurotoxin produced by _Clostridium tetani_, leading to muscle rigidity and spasms.

## Aetiology

### Causative Organism

- _Clostridium tetani_:
  - **Gram-positive, spore-forming, obligate anaerobic bacillus**.
  - Found in **soil, dust, manure, and animal feces**.
  - Produces **tetanospasmin**, a potent neurotoxin.

### Incubation Period

- **3-21 days** (average **7-10 days**).
- ~~Shorter incubation = worse prognosis~~.

### Mode of Infection

- **Spores enter the body** through wounds, germinate in anaerobic conditions, and release tetanospasmin.
- Common sources:
  - **Puncture wounds** (nails, needles, animal bites).
  - **Open fractures, burns, surgical wounds**.
  - **Postpartum or post-abortion infections** ("Puerperal tetanus").
  - **Neonatal umbilical stump infection** ("Neonatal tetanus").

## Clinical Presentation

### 1. Generalized Tetanus (Most Common, ~80%)

- `Trismus` (lockjaw) → **inability to open mouth due to masseter spasm**.
- `Risus sardonicus` (sardonic smile) → **facial muscle spasm**.
- `Opisthotonus` → **severe hyperextension & arching of the back**.
- **Painful muscle spasms** triggered by touch, sound, or light.
- **Dysphagia & respiratory muscle involvement** → → **respiratory failure**.

### 2. Localized Tetanus

- **Spasms restricted to a limb or wound site**.
- May progress to generalized tetanus.

### 3. Cephalic Tetanus

- **Rare, affects cranial nerves** (often after head trauma or otitis media).
- **Facial weakness, dysphagia, ophthalmoplegia**.
- **High risk of progression to generalized tetanus**.

### 4. Neonatal Tetanus

- **Severe muscle spasms, inability to suck or cry**.
- Typically due to **unhygienic umbilical cord handling**.
- High mortality.

## Management

### 1. Prevention

#### Active Immunization (Tetanus Toxoid - TT)

- **Primary series**: 3 doses (at 6, 10, 14 weeks).
- **Booster doses**: At **5 years, 10 years, then every 10 years**.

#### Post-Exposure Prophylaxis

| Wound Type         | Vaccination Status      | Management                                           |
| ------------------ | ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Clean, minor wound | ≥3 TT doses             | No TIG, TT booster if >10 years since last dose      |
| Clean, minor wound | < 3 TT doses or unknown | TT booster, no TIG                                   |
| Contaminated wound | ≥3 TT doses             | TT booster if >5 years since last dose, consider TIG |
| Contaminated wound | < 3 TT doses or unknown | TT booster + TIG                                     |

### 2. Local Wound Care

#### A. Wound Debridement (Early & Aggressive)

> Essential to remove necrotic tissue & reduce anaerobic bacterial load.<br/>Best performed within 24 hours of presentation.

1. `Incision & drainage`: Open up the wound to **increase oxygenation**, making the environment less favorable for _Clostridium tetani_.
1. `Excision of necrotic & infected tissue`: Remove **all devitalized tissue**, foreign bodies, and pus.
1. `Irrigation`: Use
   - `Normal saline` or `hydrogen peroxide` (helps oxygenate tissue).
   - `Povidone-iodine` or `chlorhexidine` to reduce bacterial load.
1. `Leave wound open`: Do not suture unless absolutely necessary → promotes **aerobic healing**.

#### B. Fasciotomy (Rare, Only if Needed)

- Consider **only if there is compartment syndrome** due to deep wound infection.

### 3. Definitive Treatment

#### A. Neutralization of Toxin

- `Human Tetanus Immunoglobulin (TIG) 3000-6000 IU IM` ASAP → **neutralizes unbound toxin**.
- If TIG unavailable → **equine antitoxin** (test for hypersensitivity first).

#### B. Antibiotic Therapy

- **First-line:** `Metronidazole 500mg IV q8h for 7-10 days`.
- **Alternative:** `Penicillin G 2-4 million IU IV q4-6h` (not preferred due to GABA inhibition).

### 4. Supportive Care

#### A. Muscle Spasm Control

- `Benzodiazepines` (`Diazepam 10mg IV q4h`) → enhances GABA inhibition.
- `Baclofen (intrathecal infusion)` in severe cases.
- `Neuromuscular blockers` (`Vecuronium/Rocuronium`) if on ventilation.

#### B. Autonomic Dysfunction Management

- `Labetalol` for hypertension (avoids reflex tachycardia).
- `Magnesium sulfate` to reduce muscle spasms & autonomic instability.

#### C. Airway & Respiratory Support

- **Intubation & mechanical ventilation** if respiratory muscles are affected.

#### D. Nutritional & Fluid Support

- **Enteral feeding via NG tube** if dysphagia.
- **IV fluids & electrolytes** correction.

## Complications

- **Respiratory failure** → ~~leading cause of death~~.
- **Autonomic dysfunction** → hypertension, tachycardia, arrhythmias.
- **Fractures & rhabdomyolysis** → due to intense spasms.
- **Aspiration pneumonia** → due to swallowing impairment.
