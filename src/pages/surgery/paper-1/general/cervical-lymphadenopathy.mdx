# Cervical Lymphadenopathy

- Enlargement of lymph nodes in the cervical (neck) region due to **infective, inflammatory, or neoplastic causes**.

## TYPES (Based on Etiology)

### 1. **Infective Causes**

- Acute bacterial lymphadenitis (e.g., Staphylococcus, Streptococcus).
- Viral infections (e.g., EBV – infectious mononucleosis, CMV, HIV).
- Tuberculous lymphadenitis.
- Atypical mycobacteria.
- Fungal (rare).

### 2. **Reactive / Inflammatory**

- Secondary to upper respiratory tract infection, dental infection, scalp infection.
- Autoimmune diseases (e.g., SLE, RA, Kikuchi’s disease).

### 3. **Neoplastic Causes**

- **Lymphoma** (Hodgkin’s, Non-Hodgkin’s).
- **Metastatic lymphadenopathy** → from head and neck malignancies (oral cavity, oropharynx, larynx, thyroid) or distant primaries (lung, breast, GI).

## CLINICAL FEATURES

### History

- **Acute, painful, tender, fever** → infective.
- **Chronic, painless, matted nodes, constitutional symptoms** → tuberculosis.
- **Firm, rubbery, non-tender, generalized** → lymphoma.
- **Hard, irregular, fixed, progressive** → metastasis.

### Examination

- Site (levels I–VI of neck).
- Number (single vs multiple).
- Consistency (soft, firm, hard, rubbery).
- Mobility (mobile vs fixed).
- Associated signs → sinus, overlying skin changes, cranial nerve palsy, systemic signs (hepatosplenomegaly, B-symptoms).

## INVESTIGATIONS

### For Diagnosis

- Blood tests: CBC, ESR, CRP.
- Chest X-ray: TB, mediastinal nodes.
- Ultrasound neck: node size, architecture.
- FNAC: first-line for cytology (benign vs malignant).
- Excisional biopsy: **gold standard** for definitive diagnosis.

### For Causes

- Tuberculin test, sputum AFB, GeneXpert (if TB suspected).
- Serology: HIV, EBV, CMV.
- LDH, flow cytometry → for lymphoma.

### For Complications / Spread

- CT/MRI neck: extent, relation to adjacent structures.
- PET-CT: staging of lymphoma / metastasis.

## MANAGEMENT

### General Principles

- Treat underlying cause.
- Avoid blind excision before diagnosis (esp. lymphoma).

### Infective / Reactive

- Antibiotics, analgesics.
- Anti-tubercular therapy (if TB proven).

### Neoplastic

- **Lymphoma** → chemotherapy ± radiotherapy (not surgery).
- **Metastasis from H\&N primary** → neck dissection + treatment of primary tumor.
- **Metastasis from distant primary** → treat primary malignancy + palliative neck management.

## COMPLICATIONS

- Abscess formation (acute bacterial).
- Sinus/fistula formation (tubercular).
- Airway compromise (massive nodal disease).
- Spread of malignancy.
