# Malignant Obstructive Jaundice

> Jaundice due to malignant obstruction of extrahepatic or intrahepatic bile ducts → usually painless, progressive, with dark urine, pale stools, pruritus, weight loss

## Initial assessment & stabilization

- **A**irway, **B**reathing, **C**irculation → stabilize
- Treat **sepsis/cholangitis** promptly with IV fluids + broad-spectrum antibiotics + urgent drainage
- Correct dehydration, electrolyte imbalance
- Nutritional support for cachexia

## Investigations

**For diagnosis**

- LFTs → cholestatic pattern (↑ ALP, ↑ GGT, ↑ conjugated bilirubin)
- Tumor markers: CA 19-9, CEA
- Ultrasound → bile duct dilation, masses
- CT scan (contrast-enhanced) → staging, resectability
- MRCP → level of obstruction, ductal anatomy
- EUS → fine needle aspiration for tissue diagnosis (ampullary/pancreatic lesions)
- ERCP → diagnostic + stenting (if unresectable)
- PTC → if ERCP not possible

**For cause**

- ERCP brush cytology / biopsy
- EUS-guided FNA for pancreatic lesions
- Laparoscopic biopsy for unresectable tumors

**For complications**

- Blood cultures (if cholangitis)
- Coagulation profile (bile salt depletion → vitamin K deficiency)
- Renal function, metastatic workup

## Definitive management

### A. Resectable disease

1. **Carcinoma of head of pancreas / distal cholangiocarcinoma / ampullary carcinoma**
   `Whipple Procedure` (_Pancreaticoduodenectomy_)

   - **Resection**:

     - Head of pancreas
     - Duodenum
     - Distal bile duct
     - Gallbladder
     - ± Distal stomach (standard vs pylorus-preserving)

   - **Reconstruction**:

     - `Pancreaticojejunostomy`
     - `Hepaticojejunostomy`
     - `Gastrojejunostomy`

2. **Perihilar cholangiocarcinoma (Klatskin tumor)**

   - `Extended hepatic lobectomy` **with** en bloc resection of extrahepatic bile ducts
   - Reconstruction: `Hepaticojejunostomy`

3. **Gallbladder carcinoma (without distant metastasis)**

   - **Extended cholecystectomy**:

     - Gallbladder
     - `Wedge resection of liver segments` IVb and V
     - `Regional lymphadenectomy` ± `bile duct resection` (if involved)

### B. Unresectable disease

- **Palliative biliary drainage**:

  - Endoscopic biliary stenting (plastic or self-expanding metal stent)
  - PTC drainage if endoscopic route not feasible

- **Palliative bypass surgery** (for fit patients unsuitable for resection):

  - Hepaticojejunostomy
  - Cholecystojejunostomy

- **Symptom control**:

  - Pain → opioids ± celiac plexus block (especially in pancreatic cancer)
  - Pruritus → cholestyramine, antihistamines
  - Nutrition support

- **Oncology input**:

  - Chemotherapy (e.g., `gemcitabine` for pancreatic cancer, `fluoropyrimidines` for cholangiocarcinoma)
  - Radiotherapy in selected cases

## Prognosis

- Resectable disease: 5-year survival up to 20–30% (best for ampullary carcinoma)
- Unresectable disease: median survival 3–12 months
