# Goiter

> Abnormal enlargement of the thyroid gland, which may or may not be associated with thyroid dysfunction.

## Classification

### 1. Based on Functional Status

1. **Euthyroid goiter** → Normal thyroid hormone levels.
1. **Hypothyroid goiter** → Decreased thyroid hormone production.
1. **Hyperthyroid goiter** → Excess thyroid hormone production (`toxic goiter`).

### 2. Based on Morphology

1. **Diffuse goiter** → Uniform enlargement of the thyroid.
1. **Nodular goiter** → Focal enlargement with nodules.
   - **Uninodular goiter** (single nodule).
   - **Multinodular goiter** (multiple nodules).

### 3. Based on Etiology

1. **Simple (non-toxic) goiter** → No inflammation, no malignancy, normal thyroid function.
1. **Toxic goiter** → Associated with thyrotoxicosis.
1. **Inflammatory goiter** → Due to thyroiditis.
1. **Neoplastic goiter** → Due to benign or malignant tumors.

<details>
<summary>Causes of Goiter</summary>

1. Iodine-related causes
   - **Iodine deficiency** (most common worldwide).
   - **Iodine excess** (Wolff-Chaikoff effect).
1. Thyroid hormone synthesis defects
   - **Dyshormonogenesis** (congenital enzyme defects).
1. Autoimmune diseases
   - **`Graves' disease`** → Diffuse toxic goiter.
   - **`Hashimoto's thyroiditis`** → Chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis.
1. Inflammatory diseases
   - **Subacute thyroiditis** (viral, painful).
   - **Chronic thyroiditis** (e.g., Riedel's thyroiditis).
1. Neoplastic causes
   - **Benign** (e.g., follicular adenoma).
   - **Malignant** (e.g., thyroid carcinoma).
1. Drug-induced goiter
   - `Amiodarone`, `lithium`, `anti-thyroid drugs`.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Causes of Anterior Neck Swelling</summary>

1. Thyroid-related
   - **Goiter** (diffuse, nodular, multinodular).
   - **Thyroid cysts**.
   - **Thyroid carcinoma**.
   - **Thyroiditis (acute, subacute, chronic, autoimmune)**.
1. Lymph node-related
   - **Reactive lymphadenopathy** (infection, inflammation).
   - **Lymphoma**.
   - **Metastatic lymphadenopathy** (from thyroid, head and neck cancers).
1. Congenital anomalies
   - **Thyroglossal duct cyst** (midline, moves with swallowing).
   - **Branchial cleft cyst** (lateral, does not move with swallowing).
1. Vascular swellings
   - **Carotid body tumor**.
   - **Arteriovenous malformations (AVMs)**.
1. Other soft tissue tumors
   - **Lipoma, dermoid cyst, sebaceous cyst**.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Causes of Thyrotoxicosis</summary>

1. Primary Hyperthyroidism (Excess thyroid hormone production)
   - **`Graves' disease`** (autoimmune, diffuse goiter).
   - **Toxic multinodular goiter** (Plummer's disease).
   - **Toxic adenoma** (single hyperfunctioning nodule).
   - **TSH-secreting pituitary adenoma** (rare).
1. Secondary Thyrotoxicosis (Excess hormone releasincreased production)
   - **Subacute thyroiditis** (viral destruction of thyroid follicles).
   - **Postpartum thyroiditis** (autoimmune).
   - **Iodine-induced thyrotoxicosis** (Jod-Basedow phenomenon).
1. Exogenous Thyrotoxicosis
   - **Excessive thyroid hormone intake (factitious thyrotoxicosis)**.
   - **Struma ovarii** (ectopic thyroid tissue in ovarian teratoma).

</details>
