# Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)

> The **Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)** is a standardized neurological scale used to assess a patient's **level of consciousness** following **head injury**. It evaluates **eye, verbal, and motor responses**, providing a score between **3 (deep coma)** and **15 (fully alert)**.

## Components of GCS

| Response Type           | Score Range | Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------- | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Eye Opening (E)**     | 4–1         | **4** – Spontaneous<br/>**3** – To speech<br/>**2** – To pain<br/>**1** – No response                                                                                                                                      |
| **Verbal Response (V)** | 5–1         | **5** – Oriented<br/>**4** – Confused conversation<br/>**3** – Inappropriate words<br/>**2** – Incomprehensible sounds<br/>**1** – No response                                                                             |
| **Motor Response (M)**  | 6–1         | **6** – Obeys commands<br/>**5** – Localizes to pain<br/>**4** – Withdraws from pain<br/>**3** – Flexion to pain (decorticate posture[^1])<br/>**2** – Extension to pain (decerebrate posture[^2])<br/>**1** – No response |

## Interpretation of Total GCS Score

| GCS Score | Interpretation                                         |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **13–15** | Mild head injury                                       |
| **9–12**  | Moderate head injury                                   |
| **≤8**    | Severe head injury → **intubation generally required** |

## Modifications and Considerations

- **Intubated patients**: verbal score not possible → document as "V = 1T"
- **Pediatric GCS**: modified scale for children < 5 years
- **Eye swelling or facial trauma**: note if E score untestable
- Repeated assessments over time are crucial to detect deterioration or improvement

---

[^1]: **Decorticate posture**: abnormal flexion of arms, indicates damage above brainstem

[^2]: **Decerebrate posture**: abnormal extension, indicates brainstem damage
