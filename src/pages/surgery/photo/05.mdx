This manual worker aged 35 has a sudden onset of right hand condition.

<details>
<summary>What is the clinical findings?</summary>

Dry gangrene of the distal phalanx of the right middle finger and both middle and distal phalanges of the index and ring fingers

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the most likely cause?</summary>

Embolization (Due to sudden onset)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Which organ will you examine to help you arrive the diagnosis?</summary>

Cardiovascular system, especially left heart for valvular heart disease and atrial fibrillation

</details>
