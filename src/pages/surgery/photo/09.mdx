# Photo 09

![Photo](/surgery/photo/09.jpeg)

This is the photo of 34 year old man. He had noticed this swelling for the last 6 years. It is growing slowly and there is no pain.

<details>
<summary>What is the clinical diagnosis?</summary>

Multinodular goiter

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the treatment?</summary>

Subtotal thyroidectomy under general anaesthesia

</details>

<details>
<summary>Name the likely complications of this treatment.</summary>

Local complications

1. Haemorrhage
1. Liquefying haematoma
1. Wound infection
1. Laryngo-tracheitis
1. Pneumothorax
1. Air embolism

Special complications

1. Glottic oedema[^1], Tracheal collapse[^2]
1. Nerve injury
1. Endocrine changes

</details>

---

[^1]: Glottic oedema or Oedema glottidis: Abnormal accumulation of fluid in tissues involving the supraglottic and subglottic region where laryngeal mucosa is loose

[^2]: Tracheal collapse makes it difficult for air to get to the lungs
