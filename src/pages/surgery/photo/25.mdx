# Photo 25

![Photo](/surgery/photo/25.jpeg)

This 62-year-old male patient has chronic painless ulcer for 3 years.

<details>
<summary>What is the diagnosis?</summary>

Squamous cell carcinoma of the scalp

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the diagnostic features?</summary>

1. 62-year-old male patient has chronic painless ulcer for 3 years
1. Irregular ulcer with raised and everted edges and irregular nodular floor showing areas of blackish discoloration and pinking areas

</details>

<details>
<summary>How would you confirm the diagnosis?</summary>

`Wedge biopsy` of the ulcer margin

</details>

<details>
<summary>Name 2 differential diagnoses?</summary>

1. Basal cell carcinoma
1. Malignant melanoma

</details>

<details>
<summary>Name 2 methods of treatment.</summary>

1. Wide excision and skin grafting
1. Radiotherapy

</details>
