The patient has the condition for 2 years with recurrent episodes of pain and swelling relieved by discharge of pus.

<details>
<summary>What is the clinical diagnosis?</summary>

Fistula in ano

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the points for diagnosis?</summary>

1. 2 years history of recurrent episodes of pain and swelling relieved by discharge of pus
1. Presence of an opening in the perianal skin

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the Goodsall's rule?</summary>

> A fistula with the external opening anterior to an imaginary transverse line across the anus has its internal opening at the same radial position and for an external opening posterior to this line, the internal opening is in the midline posteriorly with a horse-shoe tract.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the treatment in low type?</summary>

`Fistulectomy` under spinal anaesthesia after bowel preparation

</details>
