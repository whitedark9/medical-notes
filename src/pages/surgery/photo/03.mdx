# Photo 03

![Photo](/surgery/photo/03.jpeg)

This 66-year-old post-menopausal lady presents with this condition. Previously she had noticed a swelling in the breast for 3 years.

<details>
<summary>What is your clinical diagnosis?</summary>

Carcinoma of right breast - Stage IV

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the other possible presentation of this condition?</summary>

- Painless lump in the breast
- Recent retraction of nipple
- Dimpling of the skin
- Peau de orange
- Lump in the axilla

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the stage of this disease?</summary>

Stage IV (The disease has spread beyond the breast by the presence of satellite nodules on the upper abdomen and other side of chest)

</details>

<details>
<summary>How will you treat this patient?</summary>

As the disease is Stage IV, only palliative therapy can be applied.

1. `Palliative surgery` (e.g. palliative mastectomy)
1. `Palliative radiotherapy` (e.g. To down stage the tumour)
1. `Chemotherapy` with `CMF` and `Hormonal therapy` with `tamoxifen`, `herceptin` and `astromazole`

</details>
