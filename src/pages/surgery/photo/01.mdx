# Photo 01

![Photo](/surgery/photo/01.jpeg)

A 60 year old female presents with colicky abdominal pain, absolute constipation and abdominal distension.

<details>
  <summary>What is the physical sign of the picture?</summary>
  Step ladder pattern of visible peristalsis
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What is the provisional diagnosis?</summary>
  Intestinal obstruction
</details>

<details>
<summary>Name 2 X-ray that you would like to do?</summary>

1. Plain X-ray abdomen erect (Will show multiple air fluid levels)
1. Barium enema X-ray (Will show obstruction in the colon due to carcinoma of the large intestine)

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the 2 likely causes?</summary>

1. Carcinoma of the left colon
1. Adhesive intestinal obstruction

</details>

<details>
<summary>Management</summary>

- Pre-operative resuscitation including nasogastric suction and intravenous fluid
- Pre-operative anti-biotics active against gram-negative and anaerobic organisms
- Laparotomy followed by resection of affected segment of the colon and end to end anastomosis

</details>
