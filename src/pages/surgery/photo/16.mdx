# Photo 16

![Photo](/surgery/photo/16.jpeg)

This patient develops pain and bleeding.

<details>
<summary>What is the diagnosis?</summary>

Prolapsed strangulated haemorrhoids

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the points for diagnosis?</summary>

1. Prolapse of the haemorrhoids
1. The covering mucosa is oedematous and the mucosal folds are radiating

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the differential diagnoses?</summary>

1. Prolapsed rectum
1. Prolapsed intussusception[^1] through the anus

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the treatment?</summary>

`Conservative management` followed 3 months later by `haemorrhoidectomy`.

</details>

---

[^1]: Intussusception is a medical condition in which a part of the intestine folds into the section immediately ahead of it.
