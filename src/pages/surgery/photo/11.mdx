# Photo 11

![Photo](/surgery/photo/11.jpeg)

This 16-year-old patient presents with left inguino-scrotal swelling for 3 months.

<details>
<summary>What is the clinical diagnosis?</summary>

Left indirect inguinal hernia

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the other differential diagnosis?</summary>

Funicular type of left sided hydrocele

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are 2 important clinical signs to confirm the disease?</summary>

1. Visible and palpable cough impulse
1. Reducibility

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the treatment?</summary>

Herniotomy only

</details>
