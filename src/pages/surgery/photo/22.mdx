The patient develops this condition at 9 post-operative day after removal of sutures.

<details>
<summary>What is the diagnosis?</summary>

Burst abdomen complete and revealed

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are 2 important clinical features that precede this condition?</summary>

1. Serosanguinous Discharge[^1]
1. Sense[^2]

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the 3 types of this condition?</summary>

1. Superficial and revealed
1. Deep and concealed which later developed into an incisional hernia
1. Complete and revealed (i.e. burst abdomen)

</details>
