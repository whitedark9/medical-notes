# Photo 24

![Photo](/surgery/photo/24.jpeg)

This patient had the chronic discharging lesion of the right side of neck for 3 months.

<details>
<summary>What is the clinical diagnosis?</summary>

Tuberculosis sinus neck right side

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the points for diagnosis?</summary>

1. Chronic discharging lesion in the right side of the neck for 3 months
1. The surrounding skin of the sinus is unhealthy with bluish tinge and there is undermined edges

</details>

<details>
<summary>How will you confirm the diagnosis?</summary>

Biopsy of the lesion showing granulomatous tubercles containing epitheloid cells and Langhan's giant cells

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the treatment?</summary>

Standard anti-tuberculous chemotherapy

</details>
