# Photo 30

![Photo](/surgery/photo/30.jpeg)

This patient has a slowly growing painless swelling for 4 years

<details>
<summary>From which organ does the swelling arise?</summary>

Left parotid gland

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the point for that?</summary>

The swelling is situated in the parotid triangle in front, below and behind the ear, displacing the ear lobe laterally

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the most likely diagnosis?</summary>

`Pleomorphic adenoma` of the parotid gland (as it is the commonest tumour of the parotid gland and is a slow growing tumour and is painless)

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the treatment?</summary>

`Superficial parotidectomy` under general anaesthesia with identification and preservation of the facial nerve and its branches

</details>
