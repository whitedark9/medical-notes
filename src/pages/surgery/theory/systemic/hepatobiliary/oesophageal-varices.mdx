# Oesophageal Varices

<details>
<summary>Causes of Portal Hypertension</summary>

1. Pre-hepatic Causes
   - Portal vein thrombosis
   - Splenic vein thrombosis
   - Congenital portal vein atresia or stenosis
     . Intra-hepatic Causes
   1. Pre-sinusoidal
      - Schistosomiasis (granulomatous periportal fibrosis)
      - Congenital hepatic fibrosis
      - Sarcoidosis
      - Primary biliary cholangitis
   1. Sinusoidal
      - Cirrhosis (most common cause)
      - Alcoholic hepatitis
      - Chronic hepatitis (HBV, HCV)
      - Toxins (e.g., methotrexate)
   1. Post-sinusoidal
      - Veno-occlusive disease
      - Sinusoidal obstruction syndrome
      - Radiation injury
1. Post-hepatic Causes
   - Budd–Chiari syndrome (hepatic vein thrombosis)
   - Constrictive pericarditis
   - Right heart failure
   - Tricuspid regurgitation

</details>

> Acute upper gastrointestinal (GI) bleeding due to **rupture of dilated submucosal veins (varices)** in the lower esophagus, commonly associated with **portal hypertension**, especially from **cirrhosis**.

## Emergency Management

### A – Airway

- Secure airway if altered consciousness or risk of aspiration
- Consider endotracheal intubation before endoscopy in unstable patients

### B – Breathing

- Administer oxygen via mask or nasal cannula
- Monitor SpO₂ and respiratory rate

### C – Circulation

- Two large-bore IV lines
- **Resuscitate with crystalloids**, then **blood transfusion** (target Hb: 7–9 g/dL)
- **Correct coagulopathy** (vitamin K, FFP, platelets as needed)

### D – Disability

- Monitor GCS
- Assess for **hepatic encephalopathy**

### E – Exposure

- Full physical exam to identify stigmata of chronic liver disease
- Look for other bleeding sources

### F – Fluids

- Maintain perfusion
- Start `IV terlipressin or octreotide` to reduce portal pressure

## Definitive Management

### 1. Pharmacologic

1. `Vasopressors`:

- `Terlipressin` (first-line) or `Octreotide`
- Reduces splanchnic blood flow → ↓ portal pressure

1. `Antibiotics`:

- IV `ceftriaxone` or `norfloxacin` to prevent SBP (spontaneous bacterial peritonitis)

1. `Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs)` (if non-variceal cause not ruled out)

### 2. Endoscopic Therapy (First-line definitive treatment)

- Perform **urgent endoscopy** within **12 hours**
- **Endoscopic variceal ligation (EVL)** preferred
- **Sclerotherapy** if EVL not feasible

### 3. Surgical Management

**Indications for surgery:**

- Failure of medical + endoscopic treatment
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Recurrent hemorrhage despite optimal non-surgical care
- No access to TIPS or contraindicated

#### A. Preoperative Preparation

- Optimize coagulation status (vitamin K, platelets, FFP)
- Correct anemia and electrolyte imbalance
- Control encephalopathy
- Stabilize liver function (Child-Pugh assessment)

#### B. Surgical Options

1. Emergency portosystemic shunt procedures:
   1. `Distal splenorenal shunt (DSRS)`: connects splenic vein to left renal vein
   - Selective shunt → preserves portal flow to liver
   1. `Non-selective shunts` (e.g., portocaval shunt)
   - Diverts all portal blood → ↑ risk of encephalopathy
2. Esophageal transection with `devascularization`
   - **Sugiura procedure**: transection + devascularization of esophagus and upper stomach
   - Rarely used; reserved for centers without TIPS

#### C. Postoperative Complications

- Hepatic encephalopathy (↑ with non-selective shunts)
- Shunt thrombosis
- Rebleeding
- Ascites worsening
- Infection

### 4. TIPS (Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt)

- Minimally invasive alternative to surgery
- Creates intrahepatic channel between portal and hepatic veins
- Used when EVL fails or patient unsuitable for surgery
- ↓ portal pressure effectively
- Risk: hepatic encephalopathy (\~30%)

### 5. Balloon Tamponade (Temporary)

- **Sengstaken-Blakemore tube** if massive bleeding and awaiting endoscopy or surgery
- Temporary (< 24 h) bridge to definitive treatment

## Complications of Variceal Bleeding

- Hypovolemic shock
- Aspiration pneumonia
- Hepatic encephalopathy
- Rebleeding
- Death (mortality rate \~20–30%)

## Prevention of Rebleeding

- **Non-selective beta-blockers** (`propranolol` or `nadolol`)
- Repeat **EVL (endoscopic variceal ligation) sessions**
- TIPS or shunt in recurrent/refractory cases
- Treat underlying portal hypertension
