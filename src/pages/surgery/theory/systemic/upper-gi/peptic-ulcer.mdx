# Peptic Ulcer (PU)

## General Approach

1. **Identify and eliminate the cause** (e.g., _H. pylori_, NSAIDs)
1. **Promote ulcer healing** (PPIs, lifestyle modifications)
1. **Prevent complications** (bleeding, perforation)

## Medical Management

### A. First-Line Treatment

#### 1. H. pylori Eradication (If Positive)

- **Triple Therapy (14 days):**
  1. `PPI` (Omeprazole, Pantoprazole)
  1. `Amoxicillin` (1 g BID)
  1. `Clarithromycin` (500 mg BID)
- **Quadruple Therapy (If resistance or failed triple therapy):**
  1. `PPI`
  1. `Bismuth subsalicylate`
  1. `Metronidazole`
  1. `Tetracycline`

#### 2. Acid Suppression Therapy

- **Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) → First-line**
  - `Omeprazole` 20 mg/day, `Pantoprazole` 40 mg/day (4-8 weeks)
- **H2-Receptor Antagonists (Alternative to PPIs)**
  - `Ranitidine`, `Famotidine`

#### 3. Discontinue NSAIDs (If NSAID-Induced PU)

- If NSAIDs are necessary → Use **PPI prophylaxis**

### B. Lifestyle Modifications

- Avoid smoking, alcohol, caffeine, and spicy foods
- Eat small, frequent meals
- Reduce stress

## Surgical Treatment

### Indications

Surgery is indicated in **complicated** or **refractory** peptic ulcers, including:

1. **Perforation** → Severe peritonitis, free air under the diaphragm
1. **Uncontrolled bleeding** → Endoscopic treatment failure
1. **Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO)** → Persistent vomiting, weight loss
1. **Refractory PU** → Persistent symptoms despite optimal medical therapy

### Types

#### A. Perforated Peptic Ulcer Surgery

| Procedure                                     | Description                                            | Indications                                    | Complications                                |
| --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| `Graham Patch Repair (Omental Patch Closure)` | Simple closure of perforation using a piece of omentum | Small perforations (< 2 cm) in stable patients | Leak, infection, recurrence                  |
| `Laparoscopic Perforation Repair`             | Minimally invasive closure with omental patch          | Small perforations, stable patients            | Pneumoperitoneum, conversion to open surgery |
| `Partial Gastrectomy` + `Billroth II`         | Removal of distal stomach + gastrojejunostomy          | Large perforations, extensive tissue necrosis  | Dumping syndrome, bile reflux gastritis      |

#### B. Bleeding Peptic Ulcer Surgery

| Procedure                           | Description                                               | Indications                                     | Complications                              |
| ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| `Oversewing of Bleeding Vessel`     | Ligation of bleeding artery (e.g., gastroduodenal artery) | Severe bleeding not controlled by endoscopy     | Rebleeding, ischemia                       |
| `Truncal Vagotomy` + `Pyloroplasty` | Vagus nerve division + widening of pylorus                | Recurrent bleeding ulcers                       | Delayed gastric emptying, diarrhea         |
| `Subtotal Gastrectomy`              | Removal of ulcer-bearing stomach                          | Large, non-healing ulcers with massive bleeding | Nutritional deficiencies, dumping syndrome |

#### C. Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO) Surgery

| Procedure                                        | Description                                                   | Indications                          | Complications                              |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| `Pyloroplasty`                                   | Enlargement of pyloric opening                                | GOO due to chronic ulcer scarring    | Dumping syndrome                           |
| `Gastrojejunostomy (Bypass Surgery)`             | Connects stomach to jejunum, bypassing the obstructed pylorus | Severe GOO with duodenal obstruction | Malabsorption, bile reflux                 |
| `Antrectomy` + `Billroth I (Gastroduodenostomy)` | Removes antrum, reattaches to duodenum                        | Severe antral ulcers with GOO        | Anastomotic leak, delayed gastric emptying |

#### D. Elective Surgery for Chronic/Refractory PU

| Procedure                                            | Description                                                                       | Indications                                   | Complications                        |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| `Highly Selective Vagotomy (Parietal Cell Vagotomy)` | Selective vagus nerve division to reduce acid secretion while preserving motility | Refractory duodenal ulcer                     | Less effective than truncal vagotomy |
| `Truncal Vagotomy` + `Antrectomy (Billroth I or II)` | Removes acid-producing antrum + vagus nerve division                              | Refractory ulcers, high acid output           | Dumping syndrome, bile reflux        |
| `Roux-en-Y Reconstruction`                           | Bypasses duodenum to prevent bile reflux                                          | Severe bile reflux gastritis post-gastrectomy | Malabsorption, vitamin deficiencies  |

### Choice of Surgery Based on Condition

| Condition         | Best Surgical Option                                             |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Perforated PU** | Graham patch repair (if small) or Partial gastrectomy (if large) |
| **Bleeding PU**   | Oversewing of artery ± Vagotomy                                  |
| **GOO**           | Pyloroplasty ± Gastrojejunostomy                                 |
| **Refractory PU** | Truncal vagotomy + Antrectomy                                    |

### Complications After PU Surgery

| Complication                             | Cause                       | Features                           |
| ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Dumping Syndrome**                     | Rapid gastric emptying      | Diarrhea, sweating, dizziness      |
| **Bile Reflux Gastritis**                | Reflux of bile into stomach | Epigastric pain, vomiting bile     |
| **Anastomotic Leak**                     | Breakdown of surgical join  | Fever, peritonitis                 |
| **Delayed Gastric Emptying**             | Vagus nerve damage          | Fullness, vomiting                 |
| **Malabsorption & Vitamin Deficiencies** | Post-gastrectomy            | Iron, B12 deficiency, osteoporosis |
