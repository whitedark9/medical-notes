# Sterilization & Disinfection

## Sterilization

> Complete elimination or destruction of all forms of microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, and spores.

- Used for surgical instruments, implants, and culture media.

## Disinfection

> Destruction of most microorganisms, but not necessarily spores.<br/>Reduces microbial load to a safe level.

- Used for surfaces, equipment, and skin antisepsis.

## Methods

### 1. Sterilization Methods

#### A. Physical Methods

##### 1. Heat

- **Moist Heat (Autoclaving) - Gold Standard**
  - Uses steam under pressure.
  - `121°C at 15 psi for 15-20 minutes`.
  - Kills bacteria, spores, and viruses.
  - **Example**: _Surgical instruments_, _dressing materials_.
- **Dry Heat (Hot Air Oven)**
  - Uses `high temperature (160-180°C for 1-2 hours)`.
  - Kills by protein denaturation & oxidation.
  - **Example**: _Glassware_, _metal instruments_, _powders_.

##### 2. Radiation

- **Ionizing Radiation (Gamma rays, X-rays)**
  - Produces free radicals → Damages microbial DNA.
  - **Example**: _Sterilizing disposable syringes, catheters_.
- **Non-Ionizing Radiation (UV rays)**
  - Disrupts DNA replication.
  - **Example**: _Air sterilization in operation theaters_.

##### 3. Filtration

- Used for **heat-sensitive solutions (e.g., vaccines, antibiotics)**.
- `Membrane Filters (0.22 μm or 0.45 μm pores)` trap microbes.

#### B. Chemical Methods

- Used for **heat-sensitive items**.

##### 1. Ethylene Oxide (ETO) Gas

- Alkylates DNA → Kills all microorganisms including **spores**.
- Used for **plastics, delicate surgical instruments**.
- **Toxic**, requires aeration after sterilization.

##### 2. Formaldehyde Gas

- **Used for fumigation of operation theaters**.
- Highly **toxic & irritant**.

##### 3. Hydrogen Peroxide Plasma

- Free radicals destroy microbes.
- Used for **endoscopes, delicate instruments**.

---

### 2. Disinfection Methods

#### A. Physical Disinfection

- `Boiling (100°C for 10-30 minutes)` → Kills most pathogens (not spores).
- `Pasteurization (63°C for 30 min or 72°C for 15 sec)` → Used for milk sterilization.

#### B. Chemical Disinfectants

| Type                                                                                      | Examples                                                                     | Uses                                    |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **High-Level Disinfectants** (Kills bacteria, fungi, viruses, some spores)                | **Glutaraldehyde (Cidex)**, **Hydrogen Peroxide**, **Peracetic Acid**        | Endoscopes, surgical instruments        |
| **Intermediate-Level Disinfectants** (Kills most bacteria, fungi, viruses but not spores) | **Alcohols (Ethanol, Isopropanol, 70%)**, **Phenol**                         | Skin antisepsis, disinfecting surfaces  |
| **Low-Level Disinfectants** (Kills vegetative bacteria, some fungi, and viruses)          | **Chlorhexidine**, **Quaternary Ammonium Compounds (Benzalkonium Chloride)** | Hand antisepsis, environmental cleaning |

## Key Differences: Sterilization vs. Disinfection

| Feature                          | Sterilization                    | Disinfection                            |
| -------------------------------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **Kills all microbes & spores?** | Yes                              | No (spores may survive)                 |
| **Methods Used**                 | Heat, radiation, gas, filtration | Chemicals, heat, UV light               |
| **Examples**                     | Autoclaving, Gamma radiation     | Alcohols, Chlorhexidine, Glutaraldehyde |
| **Used for**                     | Surgical instruments, implants   | Surfaces, skin antisepsis               |
