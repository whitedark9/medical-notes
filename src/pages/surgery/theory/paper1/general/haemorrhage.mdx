# Hemorrhage

> Hemorrhage refers to the escape of blood from blood vessels due to trauma, surgery, or pathological conditions.

## Classification

### 1. Based on Origin

1. **Arterial Hemorrhage**
   - **Bright red, pulsatile bleeding** due to high-pressure arterial flow.
   - Example: `Femoral artery injury` in trauma.
2. **Venous Hemorrhage**
   - **Dark red, non-pulsatile bleeding**, flows steadily.
   - Example: `Varicose vein rupture`.
3. **Capillary Hemorrhage**
   - **Oozing of blood** from small vessels.
   - Example: `Abrasion wounds`.

### 2. Based on Timing

1. **Primary Hemorrhage**
   - Occurs **immediately** after injury or surgery.
   - Example: `Cut during surgery`.
2. **Reactionary Hemorrhage**
   - Occurs **within 24 hours** due to clot dislodgement, hypertension, or inadequate hemostasis.
   - Example: `Postoperative bleeding from a slipped ligature`.
3. **Secondary Hemorrhage**
   - Occurs **after 7-14 days** due to **infection, erosion of vessels**, or necrosis.
   - Example: `Burst abdominal wound due to anastomotic leak`.

### 3. Based on Amount of Blood Loss (American College of Surgeons)

| Class         | Blood Loss            | Heart Rate | Blood Pressure | Mental Status |
| ------------- | --------------------- | ---------- | -------------- | ------------- |
| **Class I**   | < 750 mL (≤ 15%)      | Normal     | Normal         | Alert         |
| **Class II**  | 750-1500 mL (15-30%)  | > 100 bpm  | Normal         | Anxious       |
| **Class III** | 1500-2000 mL (30-40%) | > 120 bpm  | Low            | Confused      |
| **Class IV**  | > 2000 mL (> 40%)     | > 140 bpm  | Very low       | Unconscious   |

### 4. Based on Location

1. **External Hemorrhage**: Blood loss outside the body (e.g., scalp laceration).
2. **Internal Hemorrhage**: Blood loss into body cavities (e.g., intra-abdominal bleeding in liver trauma).

## Methods to Arrest Hemorrhage

### 1. Temporary Methods (First-Aid & Emergency Measures)

1. **Direct Pressure**
   - Apply **firm pressure** with a sterile dressing.
   - `Tourniquet`: Used in **life-threatening limb hemorrhage**.
1. **Indirect Pressure (Compression of Arteries)**
   - Compress the **proximal artery** to reduce bleeding:
     - `Carotid artery` → For neck bleeding.
     - `Brachial artery` → For forearm bleeding.
     - `Femoral artery` → For leg bleeding.
1. **Positioning**
   - Elevate the **bleeding limb** to reduce blood flow.

### 2. Definitive Surgical Methods

#### A. Mechanical Hemostasis

1. `Suturing & Ligation`
   - Ligation of bleeding arteries using **silk, vicryl, or absorbable sutures**.
   - Example: **Ligation of inferior epigastric artery in abdominal surgery**.
1. `Clamping & Hemostats`
   - **Artery forceps (e.g., Kocher's forceps)** clamp vessels until they can be ligated.
1. `Tourniquet`
   - Used in **limb surgery** but should not exceed **2 hours** to avoid ischemia.

#### B. Thermal Hemostasis

1. `Electrocautery (Diathermy)`
   - **Monopolar or Bipolar diathermy** burns the vessel to stop bleeding.
1. `Laser Coagulation`
   - Used in **endoscopic surgery** (e.g., GI bleeding).
1. `Cryotherapy`
   - Freezing small bleeding vessels (e.g., in dermatologic procedures).

#### C. Chemical Hemostasis

- **Hemostatic Agents** (Topical Application)
  1. `Fibrin Glue` - Used in **vascular and liver surgery**.
  1. `Oxidized Cellulose (Surgicel®)` - Used in **neurosurgery**.
  1. `Thrombin Powder` - Enhances clotting in **capillary bleeding**.

#### D. Packing & Tamponade

1. `Abdominal Packing`
   - Used in **massive intra-abdominal hemorrhage** (e.g., liver trauma).
1. `Nasal Packing`
   - Used for **epistaxis (nosebleeds)**.
1. `Balloon Tamponade`
   - **Sengstaken-Blakemore tube** for **esophageal variceal bleeding**.

#### E. Endovascular Methods

1. `Angiographic Embolization`
   - Used for **uncontrolled pelvic, GI, or renal hemorrhage**.
1. `Stent Placement`
   - Used in **vascular injuries** to seal bleeding vessels.

## Summary of Hemorrhage Management

| Method           | Examples                         | Uses                              |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Mechanical**   | Sutures, Ligation, Clamping      | Arterial & venous bleeding        |
| **Thermal**      | Electrocautery, Laser            | Surgical hemostasis               |
| **Chemical**     | Fibrin glue, Thrombin powder     | Capillary & diffuse bleeding      |
| **Packing**      | Nasal packing, Abdominal packing | Severe internal bleeding          |
| **Endovascular** | Embolization, Stenting           | Pelvic, GI, and vascular injuries |
