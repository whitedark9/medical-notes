# WHO Analgesic Ladder

> The **WHO analgesic ladder** is a stepwise approach to pain management, initially designed for cancer pain, but now applied broadly in chronic and acute pain settings. It guides the selection of analgesics based on **pain severity**.

## Three-Step WHO Analgesic Ladder (1986)

### Step 1: Mild Pain

> Use non-opioids ± adjuvants

- **Non-opioids**:
  - `Paracetamol` (Acetaminophen)
  - NSAIDs:
    - `Ibuprofen`
    - `Diclofenac`
    - `Naproxen`
- **Adjuvants** (if needed):
  - Antidepressants (e.g., `amitriptyline` for neuropathic pain)
  - Anticonvulsants (e.g., `gabapentin`)

### Step 2: Moderate Pain

> Use weak opioids + non-opioid ± adjuvants

- **Weak opioids**:
  - `Codeine`
  - `Tramadol`
  - `Dihydrocodeine`
- **Combine with**:
  - `Paracetamol` or NSAIDs
  - Adjuvants (e.g., antidepressants, corticosteroids, anxiolytics)

### Step 3: Severe Pain

> Use strong opioids + non-opioid ± adjuvants

- **Strong opioids**:
  - `Morphine` (first-line strong opioid)
  - `Fentanyl`
  - `Oxycodone`
  - `Hydromorphone`
  - `Methadone`
- **Combine with**:
  - `Paracetamol` or NSAIDs
  - Adjuvants as needed

### Key Principles

- Administer **regularly**, not just on demand.
- **Oral route** preferred if possible.
- Individualize based on:

  - Patient’s pain intensity
  - Response to previous medications
  - Comorbid conditions

- Step **up or down** the ladder depending on pain control and side effects.

### Fourth Step

While not part of the original WHO ladder, some guidelines propose a **fourth step** for **interventional pain management**:

- **Examples**:
  - Nerve blocks
  - Epidural or intrathecal opioids
  - Spinal cord stimulation
  - Palliative surgery
