# Vagotomy

> Surgical division of the vagus nerve (CN X) fibers to reduce gastric acid secretion by denervating the parietal cells in the stomach.

- Used mainly for **duodenal ulcer disease** and **Zollinger-Ellison syndrome** (~~before PPIs became first-line~~).

## Anatomy of the Vagus Nerve (CN X)

- **Origin**: Medulla oblongata.
- **Course**: Descends in the `carotid sheath` with the `carotid artery and internal jugular vein`.
- **Thoracic Course**:
  - Enters the `esophageal hiatus` of the diaphragm at `T10`.
  - Forms the `esophageal plexus` → Reorganizes into `anterior & posterior vagal trunks`.

### Branches Relevant to Vagotomy

1. `Celiac Branches` → Supply the **celiac plexus** (affect gut motility).
1. `Hepatic Branches` → Supply **liver, biliary tree, and pylorus**.
1. `Gastric Branches` → Supply the **stomach's parietal cells (acid secretion)**.
1. `Latarjet Nerves` → Terminal branches supplying the **antrum and pylorus**.

## Types of Vagotomy

### 1. Truncal Vagotomy

> Complete division of both anterior & posterior vagal trunks at the esophageal hiatus.

- **Effect**:
  - `↓ Acid secretion` (parietal cells denervated).
  - `↓ Motility` (pylorus loses innervation → gastric stasis).
- Requires drainage procedure (`pyloroplasty` or `gastrojejunostomy`) to prevent gastric outlet obstruction.

### 2. Selective Vagotomy

> Only the gastric branches (parietal cell innervation) are cut**, sparing **hepatic & celiac branches.

- **Effect**:
  - `↓ Acid secretion`.
  - `Preserves biliary & intestinal motility` → No need for `pyloroplasty`.

### 3. Highly Selective Vagotomy (HSV) / Parietal Cell Vagotomy

> Only the vagal branches to the fundus & body (parietal cells) are cut**, sparing the **Latarjet nerves.

- **Effect**:
  - `↓ Acid secretion`.
  - `Antral & pyloric function preserved` → No need for drainage procedure.
- Preferred for duodenal ulcer surgery.

## Comparison of Vagotomy Types

| **Type**                      | **Acid Reduction** | **Motility Affected?**                   | **Need for Drainage Procedure?**         |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------ | ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Truncal Vagotomy**          | **High**           | Yes (pyloric dysfunction)                | **Yes** (pyloroplasty/gastrojejunostomy) |
| **Selective Vagotomy**        | **Moderate**       | No (preserves hepatic & celiac branches) | **No**                                   |
| **Highly Selective Vagotomy** | **Least**          | No (preserves antrum & pylorus)          | **No**                                   |
