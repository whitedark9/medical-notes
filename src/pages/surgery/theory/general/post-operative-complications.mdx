# Common Post-Operative Complications After Major Surgery

## 1. General Complications

- **Fever**: Due to infection, atelectasis, thrombophlebitis, or drug reaction.
- **Pain**: Inadequate pain control can lead to complications like atelectasis or deep vein thrombosis (DVT).
- **Nausea & Vomiting**: Common due to anesthetic agents and opioids.
- **Delirium**: Can be caused by hypoxia, sepsis, metabolic imbalance, or anesthesia.

## 2. Wound-Related Complications

- **Surgical Site Infection (SSI)**: Redness, warmth, discharge from the wound.
- **Dehiscence**: Partial or complete reopening of a surgical wound.
- **Evisceration**: Protrusion of abdominal contents through a dehisced wound.

## 3. Neurological Complications

- **Stroke**: Increased risk in patients with cardiovascular risk factors.
- **Peripheral Nerve Injury**: Due to positioning during surgery or prolonged immobilization.

## 4. Cardiovascular Complications

- **Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)** → Risk of **Pulmonary Embolism (PE)**.
- **Myocardial Infarction (MI)**: Increased risk in elderly or those with pre-existing cardiac disease.
- **Hypotension**: Due to hypovolemia, bleeding, or anesthetic effects.
- **Hypertension**: Can result from pain, fluid overload, or withdrawal from chronic antihypertensive therapy.

## 5. Respiratory Complications

- **Atelectasis**: Common within 24–48 hours post-op, caused by poor ventilation.
- **Pneumonia**: Often secondary to atelectasis or aspiration.
- **Pulmonary Embolism (PE)**: Due to DVT; presents with sudden dyspnea and chest pain.

## 6. Gastrointestinal Complications

- **Paralytic Ileus**: Temporary bowel paralysis leading to distension, nausea, and vomiting.
- **Bowel Obstruction**: Can occur due to adhesions or herniation.
- **Anastomotic Leak**: Serious complication after bowel surgery.

## 7. Genitourinary Complications

- **Urinary Retention**: Common post-anesthesia effect, requiring catheterization.
- **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)**: Due to hypovolemia, nephrotoxic drugs, or sepsis.

## 8. Hematological Complications

- **Bleeding/Hemorrhage**: Can be primary (intra-op), reactionary (within 24 hours), or secondary (after 7 days).
- **Anemia**: Due to intraoperative or post-op blood loss.

## 9. Metabolic & Endocrine Complications

- **Electrolyte Imbalance**: Hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, or hypokalemia due to IV fluids or medications.
- **Hyperglycemia/Hypoglycemia**: Especially in diabetic patients.
- **Adrenal Insufficiency**: Can occur in steroid-dependent patients.

## 10. Psychological Complications

- **Postoperative Depression**: Common in elderly and chronically ill patients.
- **Postoperative Delirium**: Confusion, agitation, hallucinations, often seen in ICU patients.
