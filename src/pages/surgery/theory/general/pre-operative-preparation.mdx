# Preoperative Preparations

## 1. Goals of Preoperative Preparation

> Optimize **patient's health** to reduce surgical risks.<br/> Identify **comorbidities** that may affect anesthesia/surgery.<br/>Prevent **complications** (infection, thromboembolism, bleeding).<br/>Ensure proper **informed consent** and patient understanding.

## 2. Preoperative Assessment

### A. History

- **Surgical History**: Previous surgeries, complications (e.g., anesthesia reactions).
- **Medical History**: Cardiovascular, respiratory, renal, liver disease.
- **Medications**: Anticoagulants, antiplatelets, immunosuppressants, steroids, diabetes medications.
- **Allergies**: Latex, antibiotics, anesthetic agents.
- **Smoking & Alcohol Use**: Smoking → Increased pulmonary complications. Alcohol → Liver dysfunction, bleeding risk.
- **Functional Status**: Baseline activity tolerance, frailty.

### B. Physical Examination

- **Airway Assessment**: Mallampati score (for difficult intubation).
- **Cardiovascular**: BP, heart sounds, peripheral pulses.
- **Respiratory**: Auscultation, oxygen saturation.
- **Abdomen**: Liver size, hernias (important for abdominal surgery).
- **Neurological**: Baseline mental status (for post-op delirium risk).
- **Extremities**: Edema (cardiac status), DVT risk.

### C. Investigations (as needed based on history & surgery type)

| Test                                    | Indication                                 |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **CBC**                                 | Anemia, infection risk, bleeding tendency  |
| **Electrolytes**                        | Kidney disease, dehydration, diuretics use |
| **Coagulation Profile (PT, INR, aPTT)** | If on anticoagulants, liver disease        |
| **Blood Glucose**                       | Diabetes control                           |
| **Liver Function Tests**                | If liver disease suspected                 |
| **Renal Function** (BUN, Creatinine)    | Chronic kidney disease, nephrotoxic drugs  |
| **ECG**                                 | > 50 years old, cardiac risk factors       |
| **Chest X-ray**                         | Respiratory disease, major surgery         |
| **Pulmonary Function Tests**            | COPD, asthma, lung surgery                 |
| **Crossmatch**                          | If major blood loss expected               |

## 3. Risk Stratification

### A. Cardiac Risk (Revised Cardiac Risk Index - RCRI)

- **High-Risk Factors:**
  - History of ischemic heart disease, CHF, stroke, CKD, diabetes on insulin.
  - High-risk surgery (vascular, major abdominal/thoracic).
- **Management:**
  - **Low risk** → Proceed with surgery.
  - **High risk** → Further cardiac evaluation (e.g., stress test, echocardiogram).

### B. Pulmonary Risk (ARISCAT Score)

- **Risk Factors:**
  - Age > 60, smoking, COPD, prolonged surgery > 3h, emergency surgery.
- **Management:**
  - Smoking cessation, incentive spirometry, bronchodilator therapy if needed.

### C. Venous Thromboembolism (VTE) Risk

- **Risk Factors:**
  - Immobility, malignancy, previous DVT/PE, obesity.
- **Management:**
  - LMWH prophylaxis, early ambulation, compression devices.

## 4. Preoperative Optimization

### A. Medication Management

| Medication                           | Pre-Op Instructions                                            |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `Anticoagulants (Warfarin, DOACs)`   | Stop 3-5 days before (bridge if needed)                        |
| `Aspirin/Clopidogrel`                | Stop 7 days before (except high-risk cardiac patients)         |
| `Diabetes Meds (Metformin, Insulin)` | Hold `metformin` on surgery day; adjust insulin                |
| `Steroids`                           | Stress dose if chronic use (to prevent adrenal insufficiency)  |
| `Antihypertensives`                  | Continue most, but hold `ACE inhibitors`/`ARBs` on surgery day |

### B. Lifestyle Modifications

- **Smoking cessation** (at least 4-8 weeks before).
- **Alcohol cessation** (reduces risk of withdrawal, liver complications).
- **Bowel Preparation** (only for certain GI surgeries).
- **Fasting (NPO status)**:
  - **Solid food**: Stop **6-8 hours** before.
  - **Clear liquids**: Allowed up to **2 hours** pre-op.

## 5. Day of Surgery Preparations

- **Confirm NPO status** (avoid aspiration risk).
- **Pre-op antibiotics** (within 60 min before incision if needed).
- **DVT prophylaxis** (LMWH, compression stockings).
- **Patient Consent** (procedure risks, alternatives explained).
- **Pre-medications** (Anxiolytics, pain control if indicated).
