# Chest X-Ray Techniques

## Positioning

1. Posterio-anterior (PA): Upright position
1. Anterio-posterior (AP): Supine position
1. Lateral: Upright position
1. Lateral decubitus: ဘေးစောင်း (To access the volume of pleural effusion, and if it is mobile or loculated)

### PA vs AP

- Quality: PA > AP
- AP is used only when patient cannot stand up

## Inspiration

- Full inspiration >>> Intrapulmonary abnormalities can be visible
- Diaphragm: At the level of 8th-10th posterior rib or 5th-6th anterior rib on full inspiration
- If in expiration >>> Abnormal chest X-ray, Heart silhouette may be lost

## Penetration

- Adequate penetration >>> Optimal radiograph
- PA view
  - Thoracic spines may not be visible
  - Bronchovascular structures can be seen
- Lateral view
  - Thoracic spines can be seen
  - Sternum, Two ribs behind it can be seen

## Rotation

- If patient rotates during X-ray, mediastinum looks very unusual
- Check clavicle heads if they are equal distance from the spinous process of the thoracic vertebral bodies

## Opacity

- In abnormal opacity
  - Well-defined border >>> Mass
  - Poorly defined >>> Airspace disease such as pneumonia
